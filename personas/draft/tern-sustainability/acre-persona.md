# ACRE Persona 32 — The No-Contract Outer Ring
### Tern Sustainability — a 10-person ESG consultancy collecting data from its clients' suppliers, who owe it nothing

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #24 (Family 6, hubs of hubs); ◆ adds the no-contract outer ring.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Second-Order Data Hub
**Instance:** Tern Sustainability, Bristol. Founder, six consultants, a data analyst, ops and marketing. Carbon accounting, ESG reporting and net-zero strategy for mid-market companies — which means, increasingly, extracting emissions data from those companies' *suppliers*: Scope 3 is 80% of the footprint and 100% of the difficulty.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | A hub-of-hubs where the decisive ring is two hops out: Tern serves clients, but the data lives with the clients' suppliers — hundreds of businesses with no contract, no incentive and no patience for a consultancy they've never heard of. |
| Tooling | Carbon-accounting platforms, Excel beyond all reason, survey tools for supplier data requests, the clients' procurement systems (glimpsed, never granted), and an inbox of half-completed spreadsheets in seventeen formats. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The consultants per engagement — and disproportionately the *clients' own staff*, conscripted as relays to their suppliers ("can you chase your packaging guy again?"). |
| Buying behaviour | Mission-driven but commercially maturing; evaluates tools on data-collection leverage above all. Regulation (CSRD ripple-down, retailer mandates) is the market's engine and the deadline-setter. |
| What failure costs | A client's report built on supplier guesses fails assurance and embarrasses everyone; a data request campaign that annoys a client's strategic supplier damages the *client's* commercial relationship — Tern's mistakes detonate two hops away, on edges it doesn't own. |

---

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A consultancy hub whose deliverable depends on standardised data from an outer ring of organisations bound to nobody in the constellation — reachable only across the client's own commercial edges, motivated only by whatever the request design itself can manufacture.

**Cast:**
- **Dr Anna Brodie, 44** — founder; climate scientist turned consultant (Bristol)
- **Felix Nwosu, 31** — senior consultant; runner of supplier-data campaigns (Bristol)
- **Steph Walcott, 39** — client-side head of sustainability at a food manufacturer (the conscripted relay)
- **Len Hodgkiss, 51** — ops manager at a packaging supplier; outer ring, no contract, no time (Wakefield)
- **Plus:** the data analyst, the client's procurement team (gatekeepers of supplier relationships), the client's board (consumers of the glossy output), assurance providers, ~200 suppliers per major engagement

**External immovable nodes:**
- **Reporting frameworks and regulators** — CSRD, SECR, SBTi, B Corp: methodologies and deadlines set elsewhere, revised often
- **The client's customers** — retailers and corporates demanding the client's data with mandates that cascade down the chain; the reason the client is paying at all
- **Assurance/audit providers** — the late gate that tests every estimate's provenance
- **The suppliers' own capacity** — the outer ring's immovable truth: Len has a factory to run

**Topology:** ◆ Hub-of-hubs with a contractless rim: Tern → client (contract) → client's suppliers (the client's commercial edges) → *their* suppliers (nobody's edges at all). Influence attenuates with each hop; data quality decays identically. The constellation's success is decided on edges its centre cannot even see.

**Lifecycle (per engagement, 4–12 months, annualising):**
- *Scoping & baseline:* the client's own data (hard enough), then the supplier map — discovering procurement's spreadsheet is two years stale.
- *The campaign (the defining phase):* data requests out to 200 suppliers via the client's name; response curves flat for weeks, then a trickle of partials in incompatible formats.
- *Midpoint reset:* the coverage reckoning — 60% of spend responded; the team decides what to estimate, with which proxies, defensible to which auditor. The methodology debate the proposal glossed over happens now, mid-engagement.
- *Synthesis & reporting:* the footprint assembled (data + estimates + judgement), the report written, the board presentation polished.
- *Assurance & dissolution-into-annual:* the auditor's provenance questions; then next year's cycle begins — same suppliers, same requests, fresh amnesia.
- *Trust curve:* the client's trust tracks the coverage number; supplier goodwill — the scarcest resource — is *spent by every badly designed request* and pooled across all consultancies: Len's view of "these carbon questionnaires" was formed by the last five, and Tern inherits it.

**Cadence:** Campaign waves (request → silence → reminder → partial → clarification), client check-ins monthly, the annual cycle's gravity, regulation-update shocks reshaping methodologies mid-stream.

**Boundary objects:** The data-request questionnaire (the artefact the whole model balances on — every ambiguous question costs 200 confused replies), the supplier spreadsheet in its seventeen dialects, the emissions factor database, the coverage dashboard, the final report (glossy) and its methodology annex (where the truth lives), the assurance evidence pack.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On Felix (campaign logistics, format wrangling, estimate judgement) and on *Steph* — the client-side conscript relaying requests to suppliers procurement won't let Tern contact directly; her internal capital is the campaign's real fuel.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Len knows his factory's actual energy and materials reality — in units no questionnaire anticipated. Steph knows which suppliers matter commercially and which contacts are real. Procurement knows the supplier map (stale). Anna knows the methodology and its defensible shortcuts. Felix knows the response-rate folklore (what subject lines work, which sectors answer). The data analyst knows where last year's estimates are buried. The truth of the footprint is distributed across two hundred companies that mostly haven't measured it themselves.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Outer-ring silence: response rates under 40% by spend, campaign after campaign — **eliminate** the *causes Tern controls* (request design, timing, burden, reciprocity); **contain** the rest — the ring owes nothing
2. Format chaos: 200 responses in 17 dialects — **eliminate**
3. Questionnaire fatigue across the commons: every consultancy's badly designed request poisons the pool for all — **contain** industry-wide; **eliminate** Tern's contribution to it
4. Estimate opacity: proxies and assumptions buried until assurance exhumes them — **eliminate** (provenance as a first-class property)
5. Relay burnout: Steph's internal capital consumed by chasing on Tern's behalf — **eliminate** the burden, not the relay
6. The annual amnesia: supplier answers re-requested from scratch each cycle — **eliminate**
7. Anna's "we can't claim that reduction; the data doesn't support it" against marketing pressure — **preserve**: the anti-greenwash spine is the firm's licence and the client's protection

**Conflicting interests:**
- The client wants a good number; the methodology wants a true one; the board wants it on a slide
- Suppliers bear the reporting cost; the benefit lands two hops up the chain — a structural externality
- Procurement protects commercial relationships from exactly the friction the data campaign creates
- The frameworks demand precision the underlying reality can't yet supply

*Contain-class: the cost/benefit asymmetry on the outer ring is the family's defining tension — unfixable by contract (there is none), addressable only by making response cheap and visibly worthwhile.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap the data analyst: last year's estimate archaeology slows. Swap *Felix*: the response-rate folklore and 200 supplier-contact histories leave; next campaign underperforms by 15 points. Swap *Steph* (client churn is high in sustainability roles — burnout is endemic): the relay's relationships and internal capital reset; the campaign loses its engine mid-cycle and Tern discovers how little of the supplier network it ever directly held. Swap *Len's employer* (the client re-sources packaging): a year of his data history exits the footprint; the baseline restates. The pattern: the constellation's memory lives disproportionately on edges that churn the most.

**Afterlife:** Annualised by design — the engagement never quite ends, and every year's data, estimates and supplier interactions should compound into next year's head start (they currently don't: the amnesia is structural). Supplier goodwill is a cross-engagement, cross-*industry* commons. The client's reported numbers live in public documents forever — restatements are reputational events. The methodology decisions of year one constrain year five.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Design the outer-ring interaction as a product, not a survey: requests pre-filled from last year and public data ("confirm or correct" beats "complete"), in the supplier's units, sized to ten minutes, returning instant value (their own benchmark, a shareable certificate). Remember everything across cycles — per supplier, across clients where legitimate — so the ring is asked less every year. Normalise the seventeen dialects automatically. Carry provenance on every number from supplier keystroke to board slide, assurance-ready. Protect Steph: requests drafted in her voice, chases sequenced to spend her capital sparingly. And hold Anna's line: the system should make the true number easier to produce than the good one.

| Len | Peripheral Expert · (unwitting) Production Realiser | 2 — on the contractless rim |

### Dr Anna Brodie, 44 — Founder *(The Methodology Conscience)*
**Bristol.** Atmospheric scientist who left academia to make companies actually do something. Rigour-first, allergic to greenwash, increasingly fluent in commercial reality. **Attitude to AI:** scientifically literate and provenance-obsessed — estimates are fine, *unlabelled* estimates are lies. **Winning her looks like:** every number carries its lineage, the true footprint becomes cheaper to produce than the flattering one, and her methodology survives both the auditor and the marketing department.

### Felix Nwosu, 31 — Senior Consultant *(The Campaign Runner)*
**Bristol.** Geography MSc, three years in; runs supplier campaigns like a political field operation — message testing, wave timing, response folklore. **Attitude to AI:** maximal — format normalisation and chase automation are his entire bottleneck. **Winning him looks like:** the seventeen dialects parse themselves, the folklore becomes a playbook, and coverage breaks 80% by spend for the first time.

### Steph Walcott, 39 — Client Head of Sustainability *(The Conscripted Relay)*
**Client-side, food manufacturer.** A department of one with a board mandate, a retailer's deadline and no budget; spends her credibility chasing suppliers for data her procurement colleagues wish she wouldn't. **Attitude to AI:** desperate-pragmatic; uses ChatGPT for everything already. **Winning her looks like:** the campaign runs under her name without consuming her week, procurement stops glaring, and the retailer's portal gets its numbers on time.

### Len Hodgkiss, 51 — Supplier Ops Manager *(The Outer Ring)*
**Wakefield, packaging manufacturer.** Runs a factory; gets four carbon questionnaires a year from customers' consultants, each different, none explaining what happened to the last one. Fills them in honestly, eventually, in the units he has. **Attitude to AI:** none; attitude to questionnaires: weary. **Winning him looks like:** ten minutes, his own units, pre-filled from last year, and something back — his benchmark against his sector, a certificate his own customers accept, *anything* that makes it worth the kettle going cold.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tern ↔ Steph (client) | Engagement contract | Strong, mission-aligned → **capital-consuming**: every chase Tern needs spends her internal standing | Calls + shared trackers | Her conscription is the model's hidden cost centre |
| Steph ↔ procurement | Internal | Strained → territorial | Internal | The gate to the supplier map; sustainability's requests vs. procurement's relationships |
| Client ↔ suppliers (Len) | Commercial (theirs, not Tern's) | Established → **borrowed, not owned**: Tern's requests travel on this edge at the client's risk | The client's letterhead | ◆ The defining geometry: the decisive edge belongs to someone else |
| Tern ↔ Len | **None** | None → **manufactured per interaction**: the request design *is* the relationship | Questionnaire + email | The no-contract rim: motivation must be built into the artefact itself |
| Tern ↔ assurance providers | Professional | Procedural → provenance-tested | Evidence packs | The late gate that makes failure mode 4 existential |
| Client ↔ its customers (retailers) | Commercial mandates | Power-asymmetric → cascade source | Portals + scorecards | The deadline engine two hops above the data |
| **Anna ↔ AI** | — | Provenance-conditional | In-platform | Lineage on every figure or nothing |
| **Felix ↔ AI** | — | Day-one maximalist | Campaign engine | Normalisation + chase + folklore-encoding |
| **Steph ↔ AI** | — | Already improvising her own | Client surface | Govern the desperation before it governs itself |
| **Len ↔ AI** | — | Unknowing; experiences only the artefact | The request itself | The product's quality is measured at the rim, by a man with a factory to run |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- ◆ The no-contract rim demands a new design discipline: when no agreement, payment or relationship exists, *the artefact must carry its own incentive* — pre-filled, ten-minute, value-returning requests. The questionnaire is the product's most important screen, and its user never bought anything.
- Cross-cycle (and, governed carefully, cross-client) supplier memory attacks the commons problem directly: the ring gets asked less every year, and Tern's requests become the ones worth answering — goodwill as accumulating, measurable infrastructure.
- Provenance-as-architecture serves three masters at once (Anna's rigour, the auditor's evidence, the client's restatement risk) — the same lineage spine as Fieldnote's consent register and Calder's verification layer: a confirmed cross-family pattern.
