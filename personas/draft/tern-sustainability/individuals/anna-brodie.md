# Dr Anna Brodie
**Founder, Tern Sustainability · 44 · Bristol**
*The Methodology Conscience — from Tern, ACRE Persona 32*

> “An estimate is fine. An *unlabelled* estimate is a lie wearing a number's clothes. Half this industry's footprints are unlabelled estimates, and the assurance providers are starting to notice. Good.”

## Her world
Anna's deliverable depends on data held by hundreds of companies who owe her nothing — her clients' suppliers, surveyed across commercial edges she doesn't own. Her discipline is provenance: every number's lineage from supplier keystroke to board slide, estimates labelled, assumptions exposed. Her commercial maturity is recent and real: rigour that can't be delivered affordably converts no one.

## What she's trying to do
- Produce footprints that survive assurance and mean something
- Get supplier response rates above the industry's dismal norm — by better asks, not louder ones
- Keep the true number cheaper to produce than the flattering one

## What gets in her way
- The outer ring's silence: 40% response by spend, campaign after campaign
- Seventeen spreadsheet dialects per two hundred suppliers
- Marketing departments discovering her annex's caveats after the press release

## How she feels about AI
Scientifically literate and provenance-obsessed. Estimation models, gladly — with lineage. Anything that blurs data and inference earns the full force of her precision.

## In her own words
> “Carry the lineage on every figure, automatically, to assurance grade. Do that and I'll trust the machinery with scale. Lose one lineage and we're done.”

## Win her by
Provenance as architecture and the outer-ring response problem attacked at the artefact: requests that respect the supplier enough to get answered.
