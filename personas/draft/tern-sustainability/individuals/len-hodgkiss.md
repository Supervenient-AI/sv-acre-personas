# Len Hodgkiss
**Ops Manager, packaging supplier · 51 · Wakefield**
*The Outer Ring — from Tern, ACRE Persona 32*

> “Four carbon questionnaires a year, from four customers' consultants, each one different, none of them explaining what happened to the last one. I fill them in honestly, eventually, in the units I've actually got. The kettle's gone cold twice by the end. Nobody's ever once sent anything back.”

## His world
Len's factory makes good packaging on thin margins, and somewhere above his four biggest customers, a regulation is cascading questionnaires down onto his desk. He answers honestly because that's who he is — kWh from the bills, tonnages from the system, a shrug for the questions written by someone who's never seen a factory. The cost is his; the benefit lands two hops up a chain he'll never see.

## What he's trying to do
- Run the plant: output, safety, margins — the job
- Keep his customers happy, which now apparently includes carbon homework
- Spend less than an hour a quarter on forms that thank him with silence

## What gets in his way
- Questionnaires in consultants' units asking for data he holds in factory units
- The same questions, annually, from scratch, as if last year never happened
- Zero return: no benchmark, no certificate, no acknowledgement — nothing

## How he feels about AI
He doesn't. He feels about *forms*: weary. The mechanism behind a ten-minute, pre-filled, something-back questionnaire would never occur to him, and shouldn't have to.

## In his own words
> “Ten minutes, my units, filled in from last year, and something back I can show *my* customers. Do that and I'll answer same-week, every year, with the kettle still hot.”

## Win him by
The request as a product: pre-filled, unit-native, ten minutes flat, returning a sector benchmark and a shareable certificate — the contractless edge, paid in usefulness.
