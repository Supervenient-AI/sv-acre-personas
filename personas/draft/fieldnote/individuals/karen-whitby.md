# Karen Whitby
**Director, field recruitment agency · 51 · Glasgow**
*The Gate to the Public — from Fieldnote, ACRE Persona 17*

> “‘We'll get there' isn't a lie. It's a forecast. Sometimes the weather changes. The trick is knowing when to say so — and saying it before Thursday is bad for business, until it's worse for business.”

## Her world
Karen supplies the most unpredictable component in research: the public. Her recruiters work lists, charm gatekeepers, and pad estimates because the truth wobbles daily — two confirmed ABC1 males can become zero by a school pickup. Her opacity isn't deceit; it's shock absorption. But every shortfall surfaced late costs Fieldnote a fieldwork date, and she knows the padding economy is living on borrowed trust.

## What she's trying to do
- Fill quotas her clients can rely on, from a participant pool everyone's burning
- Protect her margin, which lives partly in the padding and mostly in repeat business
- Keep her database clean of professional respondents — her quality, her reputation

## What gets in her way
- Participants who are humans with lives, not inventory
- Panicked client calls that consume the recruiting hours that would fix the problem
- Late incentive payments — not hers, but her participants blame her anyway

## How she feels about AI
Wary. Her opacity is part of her margin, and a shared pipeline view feels like an audit. But the panicked calls and the over-recruit padding cost her real money — if transparency provably reduces both, she'll trade.

## In her own words
> “Show the live picture and stop ringing me about it — that's the deal. Transparency for peace. I'd take that deal.”

## Win her by
Making shared status cheaper than padding: fewer panicked calls, smarter over-recruitment maths, same-day incentives keeping her participants sweet — her costs down before her secrets out.
