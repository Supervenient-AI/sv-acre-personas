## Her world
Lauren commissions rigour and needs ammunition — and the gap between those is her job's permanent ache. She watches groups from behind the glass forming early hunches, asks the corridor question ("what are you hearing?"), and socialises fragments upward that the final analysis is then judged against. She knows she does this. The deadline does it through her.

## What she's trying to do
- Walk into the decision meeting with findings that survive hostile questioning
- Manage her stakeholders' expectations *during* fieldwork, not after
- Get research that's both true and on time — and she needs both, not either

## What gets in her way
- The analysis arriving 24 hours before the meeting it serves
- Her own corridor-fragment habit reshaping expectations prematurely
- Stakeholders who treat qual as a vote count

## How she feels about AI
Daily user; the debrief deck goes into her own tools the minute it lands. She'd far rather have a sanctioned early-signals channel than her current improvised one.

## In her own words
> “Give me caveated interim themes I'm *allowed* to share. The corridor version exists because nothing official does.”

## Win her by
The sanctioned early-signals surface — interim themes, properly caveated, shareable — plus a debrief whose every claim links to transcript evidence she can quote under fire.
