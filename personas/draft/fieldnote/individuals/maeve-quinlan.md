## Her world
Maeve runs research the right way in a market that increasingly accepts the fast way. Her studies route human beings' words through recruiters, moderators and transcribers — three companies' inboxes carrying PII she's accountable for — and her retention policy is honoured mostly by intention. The deadline is always the client's decision meeting; the data is always murkier than the deadline.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the rigour while hitting decision-meeting dates — the permanent trade
- Get the consent-and-PII trail into one auditable place, with deletion that actually happens
- Protect participants' voices from becoming anyone's training data

## What gets in her way
- The consent trail fragmented across three organisations
- Transcription latency stalling analysis against fixed debrief dates
- Clients asking "what are you hearing?" mid-fieldwork and repeating the corridor version upward

## How she feels about AI
Professionally conflicted, precisely drawn lines: desk research and deck drafting yes; raw participant data only with provable provenance and deletion. Synthesis suggestions must cite transcripts line-by-line or stay out of her debrief.

## In her own words
> “Show me deletion that happened, not deletion that's policy. Then we can talk about what else you can touch.”

## Win her by
The consent register as one auditable spine — every participant, form, recording and retention date — with evidenced deletion. Compliance anxiety converted to adoption is the entire sales motion.
