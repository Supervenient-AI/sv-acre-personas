## Her world
Jess's studies are chains of small handoffs — recruit, consent, field, transcribe, analyse — where any dropped link is visible to a client or a regulator. She chases recruiters about quota gaps, moderators about consent forms, transcribers about deadlines, and finance about incentive payments, in rotating order, all week. The handoffs are the job; the trackers are her exoskeleton.

## What she's trying to do
- Land every study's fieldwork dates with quotas full and consents complete
- See recruitment truth early — not Karen's optimism, the actual pipeline
- Pay participants same-day, because the participant pool is everyone's commons

## What gets in her way
- Quota shortfalls surfacing days before fieldwork
- Consent paperwork scattered across three companies' inboxes
- Being the human integration layer for systems that don't talk

## How she feels about AI
Immediate adopter. Chasing, scheduling and incentive admin is her week; she'd hand all of it over by Friday and keep the judgement calls.

## In her own words
> “Quota status, consent status, payment status — things I check, not things I assemble. That's the dream. It's not even a big dream.”

## Win her by
Live pipeline visibility across the chain and automated chasing with her tone — turning four studies of assembly work into one morning's review.
