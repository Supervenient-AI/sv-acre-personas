## Her world
Asha's skill is making eight strangers tell the truth in ninety minutes. She holds what no recording captures — the room-feel, the unsaid — and delivers it in voice-note debriefs from car parks that the analysis may or may not ever hear. She protects her participants' dignity fiercely: observation rooms want performances; she gives them people.

## What she's trying to do
- Run groups where real things get said — her reputation across a dozen agencies rests on it
- Get the room-feel *into* the analysis instead of evaporating in a car park
- Keep her craft respected as discussion guides arrive increasingly pre-baked

## What gets in her way
- Leading questions she has to professionally launder mid-group
- Tools visible to respondents — instant rapport death
- Her richest output (the debrief voice note) having no formal home

## How she feels about AI
Open for logistics, fierce about the room: no sentiment-scoring of human beings, nothing visible to respondents, no recording analysis that flattens people into datapoints. Her debrief, though — credited, transcribed, woven into analysis — that she'd love.

## In her own words
> “Take my car-park voice note seriously. It's the best data in the study and it currently goes nowhere.”

## Win her by
Making her debrief a first-class input — captured in twenty seconds, credited, surfaced in synthesis — while the product stays invisible from the participants' side of the glass.
