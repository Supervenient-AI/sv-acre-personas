# Paul Ferris
**MD, manufacturing firm (litigation client) · 47 · Sheffield**
*The Aggrieved Principal — from Quill & Marsh, ACRE Persona 27*

> “The supplier's failure nearly took down a business my dad built. I don't just want damages. I want it *acknowledged* — in a judgment, in writing, with their name on it. My accountant says settle. My accountant didn't watch the lay-offs.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 47 |
| Lives | Dore, Sheffield — wife, two teenagers, the factory fifteen minutes away where he still walks the floor daily |
| Background | Took over the family manufacturing firm at 35; 60 staff, real products, second-generation pride |
| Situation | Suing a failed supplier; eighteen months in, six figures spent, trial eight months out |
| Emotional state | Vindication-hungry, invoice-weary; reads every billing line, understands a tenth of the procedure |
| Channels | Calls with Helena (half update, half therapy), letters he files in a ring binder labelled THE CASE |
| Devices | Office PC, a phone, the ring binder |

## His world
Paul entered litigation for justice and discovered process: disclosure orders, costs budgets, expert timetables — a machine that consumes money monthly and produces resolution in years. His trust in Helena is real and his patience is rented: every invoice without visible progress, every month of procedural silence, tests it. The settlement conversation, when it comes, will collide with the lay-offs he watched.

## What he's trying to do
- Win — or at least make the failure *cost them* publicly
- Understand where his six figures are going, stage by stage
- Protect the business meanwhile: the case can't eat the company it's avenging

## What gets in his way
- A process whose pace no one controls and whose language excludes him
- Costs accruing monthly against an outcome measured in years
- His own conflation of vindication and value — which only good information can untangle

## How he feels about AI
Indifferent to mechanism, hungry for legibility. A plain-English matter view would change his relationship with the entire process — and with the invoices.

## In his own words
> “Show me the map: where we are, what it's cost, what's next, what settling looks like in pounds. I can make hard decisions. I run a factory. I just can't make them blind.”

## Win him by
The client window: stage, spend against budget, next milestone, settlement ranges when they exist — converting an anxious hostage into an informed principal who can finally weigh vindication against value.
