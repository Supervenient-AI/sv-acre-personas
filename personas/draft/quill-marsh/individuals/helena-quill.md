## Her world
Helena's matters run for years through CPR deadlines that forgive nothing, counsel whose attention arrives in expensive bursts, experts whose independence is law, and clients bleeding costs while needing therapy. Her craft is the settlement moment — reading when the other side's resolve has peaked — and her terror is procedural: the missed date that loses a winnable case regardless of merits.

## What she's trying to do
- Win or settle well — the right outcome at the right moment at survivable cost
- Make deadlines structurally impossible to miss
- Keep Paul (and every client) informed enough to stay patient and solvent

## What gets in her way
- The deadline system being partly Tobi's vigilance and partly luck
- Counsel briefed late performing below their fees
- The client information vacuum filling with invoice resentment

## How she feels about AI
Cautiously engaged — e-disclosure analytics are already normal. Her gate is absolute: provable privilege boundaries or nothing generative comes near the matters.

## In her own words
> “The calendar that cannot be missed, the client who never rings anxious, and the privilege wall I can demonstrate to my insurers. In that order, and I'm listening.”

## Win her by
Malpractice-proof deadline infrastructure first, the client window second — and a privilege architecture she can explain to the SRA, her insurers and her own sleep.
