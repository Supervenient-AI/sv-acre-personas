# Adrian Foss KC
**Leading Counsel · 54 · London chambers**
*The Prestige Freelancer — from Quill & Marsh, ACRE Persona 27*

> “I am, structurally, a freelancer with a wig. Quadruple-booked, clerk-managed, and engaged in bursts of total focus. The instructing solicitors who understand that get my best hours. The rest get my Tuesday-evening hours, which are merely excellent.”

## His world
Adrian's attention is the scarcest resource in any matter it touches — rationed by his clerk, bought by brief fees, and won, in practice, by *preparation quality*: the instructing firms whose papers arrive complete, organised and asking precise questions get the silk at full power. He leads in court and depends utterly outside it; the hierarchy inverts at the courtroom door, and the wise solicitor works both sides of that inversion.

## What he's trying to do
- Win the matters worth winning; advise the rest into sensible settlements
- Protect his preparation bursts — the total-focus days his results are made of
- Maintain the practice's prestige economy: chambers, clerk, reputation, fees

## What gets in his way
- Briefs that arrive thin, late, or shaped like archaeology
- Conferences that should have been a precise written question
- The diary's quadruple-booking resolving itself at someone's expense

## How he feels about AI
Privately fluent (the skeleton drafts), publicly traditional, professionally indifferent to tooling — what reaches him is what matters. A firm whose papers are always brief-ready has, functionally, upgraded him.

## In his own words
> “Send me the question, the documents that answer it, and nothing else. Brevity is preparation wearing its best suit.”

## Win him by
Never selling to him: Quill & Marsh's briefs become the complete, early, precise ones — and his clerk learns which firm's matters flow smoothest, which is the only marketing the Bar has ever respected.
