## His world
Tobi is the matter's substrate: every fact's location, every date's implication, every bundle's architecture. The deadline system is partly his vigilance — a fact that frightens him more than it flatters him. Trial prep means weeks of paralegal nights assembling what the record already knew, and he can describe, precisely, the system that would make it instant. Nobody's built it. He checks, quarterly.

## What he's trying to do
- Master the documents so completely that the case argues itself
- Make the calendar self-watching — his malpractice anxiety is structural, not neurotic
- Get to advocacy-adjacent work: the strategy table, not just the engine room

## What gets in his way
- Bundles assembled by heroics instead of by the tagging he already did
- His document mastery being personal instead of queryable
- The 2am disclosure session before every CMC

## How he feels about AI
Power user within the rules: disclosure review assistance, chronology drafts, bundle automation — checked meticulously, used daily. Privilege discipline is bone-deep; he wants tools that share it.

## In his own words
> “I've already tagged everything. Build the bundle from my tags, watch the calendar from the rules, and let me query my own reading. The work's done — it's just locked in my head and a platform that won't talk.”

## Win him by
Bundles and chronologies generated from his own discipline, the CPR calendar watching itself — his mastery converted from heroics into infrastructure.
