## His world
Ben is the constellation's institutional memory for how Wildfen sounds — Priya relies on it and has never once said so. He works wry: dry comments in the margins, diplomatically furious when strategy he was excluded from invalidates copy he's already perfected. He's been around long enough to know which battles matter; sign-off that stays signed off is one of them.

## What he's trying to do
- Write to the real constraints — strategy, illustration, dieline — *before* he writes, not after
- Keep the tone-of-voice work recognised, or at least not invisibly load-bearing
- Have decisions stay decided: re-opened sign-off is unpaid rework with extra resentment

## What gets in his way
- Being last to learn everything: the strategy context, the layout, the character count
- The brief's four remembered versions, none of them his
- “Urgent” weekend requests born of other people's slippage

## How he feels about AI
Intrigued but territorial. Perfectly happy for it to *route* his words — assemble the context, track the versions, flag the conflicts. Watchful to the point of hostility about it *rewriting* them.

## In his own words
> “Show me the strategy, the artwork-in-progress and the dieline before I write a word. That's it. That's the whole feature request.”

## Win him by
Context before composition, and sign-off that stays given. If his copy never again meets a surprise dieline, he'll forgive the product almost anything.
