# Dave Hartley
**Production Director, Calverton Print Group · 53 · Nottingham**
*The Constraint-Holder — from Wildfen, ACRE Persona 1*

> “Every job, same surprises. I could tell them in week one what'll bounce at preflight. Nobody asks in week one.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 53 |
| Lives | Outskirts of Nottingham — married, two grown kids, an allotment he actually uses |

| Role | Production director at Calverton Print Group; joins projects “when there's artwork to quote” — after every decision his constraints should have shaped |

## His world
Dave speaks in tolerances, lead times and dieline gospel. He's the person who knows exactly why the artwork will fail — weeks before it fails — and the industry has organised itself so that nobody asks him until it's expensive. Privately it exasperates him; publicly he just sighs and quotes the reprint. There's a quiet conflict in his job: Dave-the-craftsman wants constraints surfaced early; Calverton-the-business profits when they surface late.

## What he's trying to do
- Get artwork through preflight first time — that's a good week
- Have his constraints heard *before* the design is finished, just once
- Get to 4pm without anyone ringing him in a panic

## What gets in his way
- Industry convention that brings him in at week five of a ten-week sprint
- Designers who've never seen his dieline template until their artwork bounces off it
- Tools. He will not open a dashboard. He has never opened a dashboard

## How he feels about AI
None whatsoever — until it saves him a reprint. Then evangelical, in his own gruff way, in the pub, to other print people. The champion inside Calverton is Dave, not the firm.

## In his own words
> “Send it to my email. If it's not in my email, it didn't happen.”

## Win him by
Meeting him entirely in his inbox: his specs surfaced to the illustrator in week one, the artwork arriving right first time, and not one new interface anywhere in his day.
