# Marianne Kessler
**Psychotherapist, The Arches Clinic · 52**
*The Necessary Wall — from Arches, ACRE Persona 43*

> “My notes are statutorily separate and will remain so against every product roadmap ever written. That isn't obstruction — it's the discipline working. People tell me things because the wall exists. Strengthen the wall and I'll consider your booking system. Weaken it and I'll consider my room elsewhere.”

## Her world
Marianne receives the clinic's deepest referrals — the knee that was carrying grief, the back pain that was a marriage — and holds them inside confidentiality that isn't a preference but the treatment's precondition. Her relationship to the federation is warm and bounded: she'll coordinate bookings and handoffs gladly; the content wall is non-negotiable, and her consent is, correctly, the gate every clinic-wide tool must pass.

## What she's trying to do
- Practise well: the work is the point, the building is a convenience
- Keep handoffs smooth *and* walls absolute — she rejects the premise that these trade off
- Protect the trauma-adjacent space from every ambient technology, forever

## What gets in her way
- Referrals arriving with too much content (she returns them, kindly, redacted)
- Booking friction that delays clients already at courage's edge
- Product roadmaps that treat confidentiality as a permissions setting

## How she feels about AI
Principled scepticism with one precise opening: a system that *enforces* her boundaries — handoff events flowing, content structurally unreachable, nothing ambient near her room — makes her wall stronger. That, grudgingly and decisively, she'd consent to.

## In her own words
> “Show me the architecture where my notes are unreachable by design, not by policy. If the wall is concrete, the booking layer can be whatever you like.”

## Win her by
The wall as architecture: per-discipline sovereignty provable, handoff events only, zero ambient anything — her consent earned by making her boundaries stronger, which makes her the product's most credible reference in the entire care family.
