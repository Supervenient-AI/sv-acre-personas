# Joel Mercer
**Owner & Clinic Director, The Arches Clinic · 45 · Altrincham**
*The Brand With a Caseload — from Arches, ACRE Persona 43*

> “To patients we're one clinic. Legally we're fifteen businesses in a building, and every referral between us crosses a boundary the patient can't see. I see thirty patients a week and run the federation in the gaps. The gaps are losing.”

## His world
Joel built the clinic on clinical trust and discovered he'd built a constellation disguised as a company: care pathways crossing business boundaries in corridors, compliance multiplying across regulators, and the "what does the clinic *do* for my 30%?" reckoning arriving from each associate on schedule. The patient experience must feel seamless; the legal structure forbids the seams from actually closing.

## What he's trying to do
- Make the clinic as joined-up as it looks — pathways that don't leak between diaries
- Answer the 30% question with a coordination layer associates would *pay* for
- Keep CQC-readiness a state, not a fortnight

## What gets in his way
- Corridor handoffs that never become diary entries
- Fifteen businesses' compliance documents, one inspection
- The player-manager bind: his caseload funds the building and starves its management

## How he feels about AI
Keen on anything that joins the pathways up; instantly deferential to any associate's regulatory concern — fourteen consents stand between him and any rollout, and Marianne's is the one that matters.

## In his own words
> “Pathway visibility without touching anyone's notes. If Marianne signs off the architecture, the rest of the federation follows — and so does my evenings problem.”

## Win him by
The pathway layer with the wall built in: handoffs tracked, gaps flagged, notes sovereign — the 30% question answered with infrastructure.
