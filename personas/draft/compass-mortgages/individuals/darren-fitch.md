## His world
Darren is paid on completion and regulated on suitability — a tension he manages with genuine care and an FCA-grade paper trail. His firm's moat is placement craft: cases other brokers bounce, he lands, because twenty years of criteria folklore lives in his head. Which is also the problem: the folklore retires when he does, and product expiries don't wait for anyone's case to be ready.

## What he's trying to do
- Compress time-to-completion — every week saved is a rate protected and a client kept
- Externalise the folklore so the firm scales past his memory
- Keep suitability evidence pristine and visibly separate from completion pressure

## What gets in his way
- Documents trickling in from clients while product clocks run
- Underwriters requesting evidence serially, a week per item
- The post-offer black hole inside the solicitors' chain, where his fee sits invisible

## How he feels about AI
Commercially hungry: complexity-plus-speed is the moat and AI feeds both. One non-negotiable — nothing may ever blur the line between his advice and the machine's admin.

## In his own words
> “Pre-empt the underwriter's full list before submission. That single trick is two weeks per case, and two weeks per case is the whole business.”

## Win him by
The criteria-folklore engine: every completed case teaching the firm's collective placement brain — and document chases, expiry clocks and underwriting pre-emption running themselves underneath his judgement.
