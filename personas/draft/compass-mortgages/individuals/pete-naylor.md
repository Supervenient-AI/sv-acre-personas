# Pete Naylor
**BDM, specialist lender · 41 · M6 corridor**
*The Human Backchannel — from Compass, ACRE Persona 21*

> “Underwriting is a black box by design — I'm the bit of the box that answers the phone. I can rescue maybe three cases a week with a quiet word. The brokers who get those words are the ones whose submissions never waste my credit.”

## His world
Pete lives between two languages: underwriting's silence and brokers' panic. His professional value is the favour economy — the quiet word that un-sticks a case — and its rules are strict: clean submissions earn favours, chaotic ones burn them. He likes Darren because Compass's complex cases arrive *complete*, which makes Pete look good internally, which mints the very credit he spends on Compass's emergencies.

## What he's trying to do
- Hit his lending targets through brokers who make his lender look good
- Ration the favour economy so it keeps working
- Spot which firms are getting better (more business) and worse (more distance)

## What gets in his way
- Submissions missing the evidence his underwriters predictably want
- Brokers who treat the backchannel as a first resort
- A black box he must defend without controlling

## How he feels about AI
Fine with anything that sends him cleaner submissions; protective of the personal-favour economy that is his entire professional moat. A tool that makes Compass's cases arrive complete is, from his seat, a gift.

## In his own words
> “Send me cases that are ready and I'll spend my credit when you genuinely need it. That's the whole relationship, and it's a good one.”

## Win him by
Never selling to him at all: the product makes Compass the firm whose submissions are always complete — and Pete's favour economy does the rest.
