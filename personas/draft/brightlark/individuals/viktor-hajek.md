## His world
Viktor is the constellation's quiet paradox: the deepest technical knowledge in the least attached seat. He's professional about it — documents more than most, comments generously — but the architecture's *living model* is in his head, and everyone politely declines to do the maths on his notice period. He's seen the ending before: the contractor gets hired by the client. He's in no hurry, but he notices who treats his knowledge as a resource versus a flight risk.

## What he's trying to do
- Do excellent work, invoice promptly, renew comfortably
- Keep his knowledge valuable *and visible* — credit is contract security
- Maintain the boundaries that make twenty years of contracting sustainable

## What gets in his way
- Knowledge-sharing initiatives that read like succession planning for his replacement
- Context requests that consume unbilled hours
- The structural weirdness of being essential and temporary at once

## How he feels about AI
Uses everything, trusts provenance only. His one wariness is precise: tools that extract his knowledge anonymously lower his value; tools that share it *with attribution* raise it. He can tell the difference in one demo.

## In his own words
> “Make me the cited source, not the mined deposit. I'll feed a system that builds my reputation. I'll starve one that builds my replacement.”

## Win him by
Attribution-first knowledge capture: his name on his reasoning, his interrupts reduced, his renewal conversation strengthened by a visible record of how much the system learned from him.
