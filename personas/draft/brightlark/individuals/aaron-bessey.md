## His world
Aaron's agency runs on the best coordination rails in the business — tickets, repos, CI, staging links — and he's watched them capture *everything except what matters most*: the reasoning. His developers have AI autocomplete; what they don't have is team-level memory. He dives into code Holly has scheduled around, knows he shouldn't, does it anyway when the problem's interesting.

## What he's trying to do
- Make the why-layer exist: decisions attached to reasoning, queryable forever
- Reuse patterns across clients without any client feeling less bespoke
- Stay technical while running a company — the eternal player-manager bargain

## What gets in his way
- Reasoning that evaporates between standup and the PR description
- Contractor knowledge concentrating in heads with 30-day notice periods
- His own appetite for the hardest tickets

## How he feels about AI
Deeply fluent and demanding: anything that doesn't cite the repo gets rejected; anything that does gets extended. Team-level memory, not individual autocomplete, is the gap he'd pay for.

## In his own words
> “Ride our rails or don't come in. We're not adopting a parallel app — we're enriching the tickets, the PRs and the Slack we already live in.”

## Win him by
Attaching the why to the work at the moment it's spoken — standup, review, Slack — and answering architecture questions with citations into his own repo's history.
