*The Three-Pod Juggler — from Brightlark, ACRE Persona 15*

> “Estimates aren't wrong because we're bad at estimating. They're wrong because reality drifts weekly and we report it quarterly. By then it's not data, it's a confession.”

## Her world
Holly runs the layer between engineering honesty and client expectation. She knows each client's tells (which "just checking in" means panic), each pod's velocity truth, and exactly how much optimism each estimate contains — knowledge that lives nowhere but her. Her recurring nightmare is the quarterly review where drift surfaces as betrayal instead of as the weekly data it always was.

## What she's trying to do
- Make estimate-reality drift continuously visible to both sides — boring, weekly, undramatic
- Run three pods without becoming the only person who knows how any of them are really doing
- Keep Aaron out of the sprint (a lost cause she manages with grace)

## What gets in her way
- Ceremony overhead that produces records nobody reads
- The contractor dependency she can see and can't contractually fix
- Clients who treat the burndown as a promise instead of a forecast

## How she feels about AI
Practical maximalist: automate the ceremony, surface the drift, draft the updates — and leave the judgement calls (when to flag, how to frame) exactly where they are.

## In her own words
> “A quarterly reveal is a relationship event. A weekly data point is just Tuesday. Make drift a Tuesday thing.”

## Win her by
The continuous estimate-reality ledger, visible to clients by design — converting her quarterly diplomacy crisis into a boring chart everyone trusts.
