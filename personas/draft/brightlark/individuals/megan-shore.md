**Product Owner (client-side) · 36 · Leeds**
*The Embedded Outsider — from Brightlark, ACRE Persona 15*

> “I'm in their standups, their Slack, their sprint reviews. I'm on their team in every way except the one that matters at renewal time — and then suddenly I'm 'the client' again.”

## Her world
Megan does unpaid product management for two companies at once: she converts warehouse complaints into tickets the pod can build, and pod progress into stories her stakeholders will fund. She's embedded enough to feel the team's rhythms and external enough to carry a contract boundary through every candid conversation. The annual budget meeting that decides the pod's existence happens without her or them.

## What she's trying to do
- Ship things her users actually feel — her credibility is built in the warehouse, not the boardroom
- Halve the translation burden: complaints → tickets, progress → stakeholder stories
- Keep the pod funded by making its value legible upward

## What gets in her way
- Translating all day, in both directions, alone
- Priorities whiplash from stakeholders who've never seen the backlog
- Tickets that lose their why between her writing them and the pod building them

## How she feels about AI
Keen and overloaded — already drafts tickets from complaint emails with ChatGPT. She'd adopt a governed version instantly if it kept the provenance her stakeholders ask about.

## In her own words
> “Draft the ticket from the complaint, keep the complaint attached, and give me a demo link with an honest status. That's my whole job made survivable.”

## Win her by
Halving the translation tax in both directions — and arming her for the stakeholder meetings that decide everyone's funding.
