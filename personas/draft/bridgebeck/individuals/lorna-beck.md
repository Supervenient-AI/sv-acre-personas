**Managing Director, Bridgebeck Engineering · 53 · London**
*The Fee Defender — from Bridgebeck, ACRE Persona 24*

> “We give away roughly a fifth of our hours to unflagged iteration — re-analysing other firms' changed minds for free. I sign the PI renewal every year with a stiff drink. Those two facts are related.”

## Her world
Lorna's firm sells judgement by the hour into meshes where everyone else's revisions consume those hours unbilled. She knows the iteration tax exists; she's never had the evidence to price it. Her commercial instincts are sharp and her engineering instincts sharper — she'll fund any tool that turns the firm's invisible generosity into a number with a client's name attached.

## What she's trying to do
- Defend fees with evidence instead of arm-wrestling
- Keep the PI premium sane: the archive's quality literally prices the insurance
- Give her engineers careers that aren't subsidised by unflagged rework

## What gets in her way
- Re-analysis hours dissolving into project goodwill
- Fee structures that misprice the mesh's iterative reality
- Sixty appointments' records at varying archival quality, awaiting some future claim

## How she feels about AI
ROI-shaped: show her the iteration ledger — every re-analysis logged with cause and hours — and she'll fund the rollout herself by Friday. Governance questions follow, sharply, from a woman who reads insurance policies recreationally.

## In her own words
> “Turn the unpaid fifth into a report I can put on a client's desk. That's not software. That's a fee negotiation I've waited eight years for.”

## Win her by
The iteration ledger as the headline, the liability-grade archive as the foundation — commercial evidence first, everything else after.
