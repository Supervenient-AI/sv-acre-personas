## His world
Tomas is the constraint-holder the architecture mesh needs and resents on schedule: consulted late by convention, blamed politely for gravity, and unpaid for the iteration tax every "minor — shouldn't affect structure" revision actually levies. He likes Alec Romer genuinely. He also keeps a private tally of the Friday-afternoon revisions, and the tally is not small.

## What he's trying to do
- Get consulted in week one, when his "no" is cheap, not week forty, when it needs a crane
- Make the re-analysis tax visible — and chargeable
- Protect his firm's liability line absolutely: his advice's boundary is a legal perimeter

## What gets in his way
- Revisions arriving unflagged, their structural impact discovered by his juniors at cost
- Twelve meshes sharing one attention span
- Shared-platform proposals that blur where his firm's record ends and the architect's begins

## How he feels about AI
His firm runs the same governance conversation as Harewood Frame's. Personally: revision-impact triage would change his week more than any drafting tool ever could. His condition is verbatim from Persona 4 and non-negotiable: a shared project layer must provably leave his firm's exposure unchanged.

## In his own words
> “Flag the structural impact on issue, price the iteration honestly, and keep the liability lines crisp. Do that, and the bluntest man in the meeting becomes your best reference.”

## Win him by
Change-impact flagging at the moment of drawing issue, the iteration ledger his MD will fund on sight — and an exposure-neutrality story his directors and insurers can both sign.
