## His world
Tunde is the mesh at its rawest: revision N+1 arrives before his analysis of N completes, meeting recordings pile up from sessions he wasn't senior enough to skip, and the model truth everyone relies on is his work wearing other people's signatures. He's AI-native — he already summarises the meeting recordings himself — and slightly amazed the firm doesn't.

## What he's trying to do
- Get chartered on schedule — every task logged against competencies
- Build models fast and right, and have the *right* part noticed
- Learn the judgement layer: why Tomas says no, not just that he does

## What gets in his way
- Rebuild churn from unflagged revisions
- Catch-up archaeology after every rotation between projects
- Credit gravity: model truth flows up, attribution doesn't

## How he feels about AI
Native and already ahead of the firm's official posture. He'd wire his whole workflow in tomorrow; his only ask is that the system's record of who-did-what is honest.

## In his own words
> “The minutes mine themselves, the revisions flag their own impact, and the log shows my name on my models. That's not the future. That's Tuesday, please.”

## Win him by
