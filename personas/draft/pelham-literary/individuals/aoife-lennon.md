# Aoife Lennon
**Rights Manager, Pelham Literary · 31 · Bloomsbury**
*The Lattice Keeper — from Pelham, ACRE Persona 38*

> “A bestseller's Brazilian rights sat free for six years. Six years. Nobody decided that — it just never reached the top of a list nobody maintains. Every book spawns dozens of saleable rights, and the grid that should track them is 60% accurate on a good day. My job is selling inventory I can't reliably inventory.”

## Her world
Aoife's market is combinatorial: every book × every territory × every format, most combinations dormant, each dormancy a small leak of authors' income. Her fair meetings run on memory and preparation; her between-fairs months on reconciling what the database says against what the contracts actually granted. She is the agency's one AI enthusiast, for the simplest reason: her bottleneck is precisely what machines are for.

## What she's trying to do
- Make the grid *true*: every right's status — sold, free, reverted — reliable at a glance
- Work the backlog as ranked opportunities instead of guilt
- Walk into Frankfurt with data instead of memory

## What gets in her way
- Paper contracts holding the ground truth, unindexed
- Reversion clauses triggering silently (rights returning unnoticed, unre-sold)
- Fair meetings capped by what one head can hold

## How she feels about AI
The champion: contract parsing, clock-watching and opportunity-ranking are her job's missing half, and she'll pilot anything that promises them — with Diana watching, which keeps the bar honest.

## In her own words
> “Parse the archive, true the grid, rank the dormant rights by value. I'll do the selling — selling is the fun part. Finding out what I'm allowed to sell shouldn't be the hard part.”

## Win her by
The true grid with live clocks and a ranked backlog — Frankfurt armed with data, the leak converted to commission.
