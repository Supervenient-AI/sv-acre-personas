# Cleo Marchbank
**Author · 47 · Norwich**
*The Mid-List Soul — from Pelham, ACRE Persona 38*

> “My royalty statements go in a drawer. Not because I don't care — because they're designed to be unreadable, and trying makes me feel stupid about my own life's work. Diana says they're auditing them properly now. I'd like that. I'd also like to never see one again.”

## Her world
Cleo's relationship with Diana is the deep kind: twenty years, four books, every wobble. Her relationship with her own commercial life is the avoidant kind: contracts un-reread, statements unread, territories unexploited — all delegated to an agency she trusts completely and which, she half-knows, runs on bandwidth as human as her own. The speaking income arrived almost by accident; nobody's managing it as a career line yet.

## What she's trying to do
- Write the fifth book — the one the shed exists for
- Have her backlist *work*: territories sold, the audio deal done, the money explained
- Grow the speaking line without it eating the writing

## What gets in her way
- Statements designed against comprehension
- Her own avoidance, politely enabled for years
- A career's admin layer that has no owner between her and Diana's attention rations

## How she feels about AI
Publicly wary (her union has views, and so does she); privately grateful for anything that means her statements get read, her rights get sold and her agent's attention feels undiminished.

## In her own words
> “Tell me, once a quarter, in plain English: what sold, what I'm owed, what's being done with the rest. I'll be at my desk, writing, not worrying. That's the dream — the not-worrying.”

## Win her by
Never meeting her: the backlist working harder, the money arriving explained, Diana's attention feeling — being — undiminished. The author surface is the agency working, visibly, on her behalf.
