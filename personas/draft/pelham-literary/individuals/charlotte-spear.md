**Commissioning Editor (publisher) · 38 · London**
*The Counterparty Champion — from Pelham, ACRE Persona 38*

## Her world
Charlotte loves books and negotiates against the people who sell them — the publishing paradox, worn daily. Her house's contracts department grinds slowly; her board wants comparables for the incomparable; and her movement between houses every few years orphans authors mid-list, a wound she's inflicted twice and carries both times. Pelham's submissions are her safe ground: reliably good, honestly pitched, never machine-written.

## What she's trying to do
- Acquire books worth her capital and publish them as promised
- Keep her submission inbox navigable as the slop tide rises
- Move contracts faster than her own department's default geology

## What gets in her way
- Acquisition boards pricing literature in spreadsheet cells
- The slush pile's new AI-generated bulk
- Her own inevitable next move, and the authors it will orphan

## How she feels about AI
A policy exists; a working group exists; her actual position is simpler: tools that speed contracts and surface comparables, fine; machine-written pitches, instantly binned; anything touching editorial taste, never.

## In her own words
> “Keep the submissions human and the contracts fast. Curation is what's left when generation is free — invest there.”

## Win the edge by
Keeping Pelham's signal pristine: human pitches, efficient contract turns, rights clarity that makes her deals easier to board — the agency's tooling experienced by her only as reliability.
