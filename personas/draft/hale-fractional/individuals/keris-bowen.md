# Keris Bowen
**Virtual Assistant · 35 · Swansea**
*The Schedulable Layer — from Hale Fractional, ACRE Persona 29*

> “I run diaries for three portfolio executives. Margot's is the hardest: five companies' calendars braided into one woman's week, and when one client has a crisis, I re-weave the whole thing by nine p.m. I see the shapes of her five worlds. I'm not allowed the contents. That's correct, and it makes the job chess in the dark.”

## Her world
Keris is the human buffer between Margot's five worlds and the clock. Crisis-reflow — one client annexing a week, four others' commitments re-sequenced with graceful holding messages — is her signature move, currently performed manually at unsociable hours. Her information diet is deliberately thin (partition discipline applies to her too), which makes her effectiveness a small miracle of inference.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep three executives' impossible diaries possible
- See calendar collisions weeks out instead of the Sunday before
- Grow her own practice — a fourth executive, maybe an associate

## What gets in her way
- Five statutory and board calendars she can see only as shapes
- Crisis-reflow as a 9pm phone call instead of a workflow
- Holding messages she drafts cold, without knowing what she's holding

## How she feels about AI
Fluent and practical — drafts the chase emails already. A reflow engine with pre-drafted, partition-safe holding messages would be the best colleague she's ever had.

## In her own words
> “Flag the collisions early, draft the holding notes safely, and let me conduct. The diary is an instrument. I'd like to stop playing it with oven gloves on.”

## Win her by
Collision detection across the five-calendar braid and one-click crisis-reflow — the schedulable layer given actual machinery.
