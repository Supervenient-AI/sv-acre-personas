## Her world
Margot is senior in five organisations and employed by none — the library's purest single-user coordination problem. Her week is a timetable of identities punctured by cross-bleed ("quick question" from Thursday's client on Monday); her months interleave five statutory calendars and five board rhythms; her nightmare is specific and cold: the wrong company's numbers in the wrong board pack.

## What she's trying to do
- Be a full-power CFO five times over without the timeshare ever showing
- Make every context switch start warm: where she left off, what she promised, what moved
- Externalise her method — the difference between a career and a sellable business

## What gets in her way
- The switching tax: twenty unbilled minutes per identity change
- Five calendars' collisions discovered the week they collide
- "Just quickly" requests silently exceeding the days each client bought

## How she feels about AI
The most motivated user in the library: she *is* the coordination problem and the budget holder in one person. Heavy ChatGPT user already — with partition paranoia to match, because one cross-bleed ends a client relationship permanently.

## In her own words
> “Be my working memory, walled per client by architecture — not by my discipline at 11pm. Load me warm at every switch. And make cross-contamination *impossible*, not unlikely.”

## Win her by
Partition-locked context capsules, the unified five-calendar view, the days-ledger per client — and her method quietly accumulating into templates worth more than her day rate.
