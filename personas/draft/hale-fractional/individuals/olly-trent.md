## His world
Olly runs on velocity and conviction; Margot is his gravity — the person who converts his narrative into numbers investors trust and occasionally tells him the narrative is wrong, which is precisely what he's paying for and exactly what he argues with. His midnight "just quickly" messages are sincere: he genuinely doesn't track that each one is a withdrawal from a three-day account.

## What he's trying to do
- Hit the metrics that unlock the Series B
- Have a real CFO's judgement without a real CFO's salary (yet)
- Move fast with someone occasionally pulling the handbrake — the tension is the value

## What gets in his way
- Cash-flow questions that can't wait for the monthly day, or so it always feels
- His own optimism meeting her independence — the productive collision
- The invisible ledger of days bought versus attention consumed

## How he feels about AI
Maximal. He'll happily interrogate "Margot's AI surface" at midnight — and honestly prefers a fast, accurate holding answer to waiting for the human, most of the time.

## In his own words
> “I don't need her at midnight. I need an answer at midnight. If her system gives me the number and flags it for her morning, everyone wins — and I'll buy more days like a grown-up when the ledger shows I'm over.”

## Win him by
Fast scoped answers and honest holding replies that make the timeshare invisible — plus a days-ledger that converts his over-consumption into upsell instead of friction.
