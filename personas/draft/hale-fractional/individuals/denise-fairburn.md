# Denise Fairburn
**Financial Controller, manufacturing client · 56 · Stoke**
*The Hands On Site — from Hale Fractional, ACRE Persona 29*

> “Margot gets one day a month here. Whether that day is strategy or spent re-explaining the cost codes depends entirely on my other twenty. I prepare like it's an exam. It would be nice if anyone noticed that's why her day works.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 56 |
| Lives | Trentham, Stoke — husband (retired from the potteries), grown kids, a grandson on Fridays |
| Background | Thirty years in the same manufacturer: ledger clerk to financial controller; knows every cost code by heart and history |
| Role | The permanent finance presence; Margot's monthly day stands on Denise's other twenty |
| Style | Precise, sceptical, quietly proud; trusts what reconciles and nothing else |
| Channels | Email, Sage, the monthly day (prepared like a state visit), the phone for true anomalies |
| Devices | A desktop with two decades of spreadsheet archaeology, a printing calculator she defends |

## Her world
Denise has watched finance directors come and go; Margot is the first "fractional" one, and Denise was sceptical until the third visit, when Margot asked the one question about work-in-progress valuation that nobody had asked in a decade. Now there's mutual professional respect — and a structural inefficiency: Margot's context reload eats the morning of every monthly day, and Denise's meticulous preparation is the only thing that saves the afternoon.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the company's numbers true through every initiative that lands on finance
- Make Margot's day count — the strategy conversations help Denise's own standing too
- Retire in six years leaving a function, not a folklore

## What gets in her way
- The monthly reload: re-establishing context that should persist between visits
- Initiatives announced without finance consulted (then reconciled by Denise)
- Being the institutional memory nobody costs until she books a holiday

## How she feels about AI
Sceptical, precise, winnable: anything that reconciles, persists context and shows its working gets a fair trial. Anything that hallucinates a number gets one chance fewer than that.

## In her own words
> “If Margot arrives already knowing what moved since last month, her day starts at the strategic bit. That's better for her, the company — and, noted in passing, for me.”

## Win her by
Context persistence between visits (her preparation visibly feeding it, credited) and reconciliation she can audit — respect for the twenty days that make the one day work.
