*The Standard-Bearer — from Kindling Learning, ACRE Persona 18*

> “Compliance training people don't hate — that's the whole company. The threat to it isn't competitors. It's a subject-matter expert's diary and a launch date that doesn't care.”

## Her world
Bridget sells knowledge transformed: experts' heads → scripts → animation → modules → an LMS. Her margin doubled when AI accelerated drafting, and she runs that quietly because client policies haven't all caught up. Her schedule risk is always the same shape: a brilliant, unavailable expert whose three-week silences sit on the critical path of a launch tied to a regulation's commencement date.

## What she's trying to do
- Stop SME bottlenecks eating launches — the recurring, predictable, unsolved failure
- Govern her AI use before clients ask — transparency as a selling point, not a confession
- Grow past the size where she personally reviews everything

## What gets in her way
- Experts who sign off module one and contradict it reviewing module three
- Legal reviews arriving at the end with rewrite power
- The maintenance amnesia: year-two content updates done by archaeology

## How she feels about AI
Strategic adopter, ahead of her clients and aware of it. She wants provenance she can show: where AI touched the content, where humans verified it — turning her quiet advantage into an open one.

## In her own words
> “Make every claim's status visible — assumed, confirmed, signed off. If production never starts on an assumption again, my year has no bad surprises left in it.”

## Win her by
The verification pipeline (claims tracked from assumption to sign-off, gating media spend) plus client-facing AI-use transparency — her margin and her integrity, productised together.
