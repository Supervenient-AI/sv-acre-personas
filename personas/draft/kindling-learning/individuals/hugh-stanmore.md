# Dr Hugh Stanmore
**Head of Regulatory Affairs (client SME) · 57**
*The Eleventh Priority — from Kindling Learning, ACRE Persona 18*

> “I care that it's right. That's why my reviews are devastating — and why they're late: I won't skim something that carries my subject. Give me a fifteen-minute way to be thorough and you'll get me inside a week.”

## His world
Hugh knows everything the course must teach, including the unwritten institutional sensitivities ("we never phrase it that way since the incident"). He attended the kickoff workshop brilliantly, creating an illusion of availability the project has been paying down ever since. His late reviews devastate precisely because he's good: when he finally reads module three, he's right about what's wrong — including things he himself approved in module one, before his thinking refined.

## What he's trying to do
- Do his actual job, which this project is not
- Keep his name off anything technically wrong — his reputation is precision
- Help, genuinely, in the smallest possible units of time

## What gets in his way
- Review requests sized in afternoons when he has minutes
- Being asked things he's already answered, somewhere, to someone
- A project schedule that treats his diary as a resource nobody actually booked

## How he feels about AI
None for himself — but he'd dictate corrections to anything that made reviewing take fifteen minutes instead of an afternoon, and never know or care what processed them.

## In his own words
> “Ask me small, specific, pre-researched questions. Never the same one twice. I'm not difficult. I'm just busy being the thing you're trying to bottle.”

## Win him by
Shrinking every ask: batched micro-questions, draft-for-confirmation instead of compose-from-scratch, markup-light reviews — thoroughness made cheap enough for his diary.
