## His world
Sandeep is accountable for a date gated by people he can't instruct: Hugh's diary, legal's queue, IT's publishing windows. His weekly call with Bridget is half status, half mutual commiseration — they share a problem and a powerlessness. Under pressure he'll paste anything into anything; the ungoverned-AI risk in this constellation wears his lanyard, amiably.

## What he's trying to do
- Launch on the date — the regulation doesn't move and neither does the board slide
- Get Hugh's diary unlocked without spending career capital chasing a senior expert
- Look like the person who ran a tight programme, because mostly he is

## What gets in his way
- Accountability without authority — the embedded theme of his working life
- Status he assembles manually from emails the night before reporting
- The legal review's late ambush, every time, like a tradition

## How he feels about AI
Pragmatic enthusiast, zero governance instincts, maximum deadline pressure — the exact profile that needs a sanctioned surface before the unsanctioned habit causes an incident.

## In his own words
> “Give me an honest status I can forward, and make the expert-delay cost visible to the people who own the expert. Neutrally. With charts. Not my name on it.”

## Win him by
The forwardable truth: live programme status, SME-latency costs computed by a neutral system — escalation ammunition that protects the project without singeing anyone's career.
