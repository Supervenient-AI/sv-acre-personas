## Her world
Flo sits at the expensive end of the chain: by the time work reaches her, every upstream ambiguity has been inherited and priced into her hours. A script that unlocks after animation isn't a tweak — it's days of unbillable rework or an awkward amendment invoice. Meanwhile she watches AI tools encroach on her craft's lower rungs with professional unease, and uses them for storyboards anyway, because the deadline is the deadline.

## What she's trying to do
- Animate from genuinely verified scripts — the difference between craft and roulette
- Get changes arriving as priced amendments, not assumed favours
- Keep her client roster warm enough to choose projects, not just accept them

## What gets in her way
- "Locked" scripts that unlock
- Source-file requests that feel like expropriation (her project files are her working method)
- The pricing race to the bottom as AI floods the explainer market

## How she feels about AI
Uses it for storyboards and previz; watches it with one professional eye narrowed. Her bet: the craft layer survives, the middle gets eaten, and verified-input workflows are what keep her hours billable.

## In her own words
> “Tell me the script's verification status before I render a frame. Green means animate. Amber means wait. It's traffic lights. We can do traffic lights.”

## Win her by
Sequencing her work behind verification automatically, pricing changes at the moment they arrive — and an escrow arrangement for source files that respects her method while protecting the studio.
