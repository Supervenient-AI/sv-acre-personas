## His world
Dan's raw material is other people's expertise, available in fragments: a workshop here, a forty-minute call there, weeks of silence between. He's industrialised his own discipline — batched questions, mined transcripts, the verification doc — because every question asked twice spends expert goodwill the project can't spare. The pedagogy is the job he trained for; the extraction economy is the job he got.

## What he's trying to do
- Make every minute of expert time count triple
- Track each claim from assumption → confirmed → signed-off, so production never builds on sand
- Defend the learning design ("that's three modules of cognitive load in one") against cramming pressure

## What gets in his way
- Expert latency on the critical path
- Sign-offs that un-sign themselves at later review
- His verification system being a private doc instead of the company's spine

## How he feels about AI
Heavy daily user — transcription, structuring, drafting — with the pedagogy guarded. His dream feature is the one he's already half-built by hand.

## In his own words
> “Mine every interview so nothing gets asked twice. Draft the answers from the documents so Hugh confirms instead of composes. Expert minutes are the rarest currency we have — spend them like it.”

## Win him by
Productising his private system: claim-status tracking, transcript mining, batched micro-asks — with his craft visibly at the centre of it.
