# Naomi Webster
**Homeowner — Maple Road renovation · 38 · Walthamstow**
*The Accidental Hub — from Maple Road, ACRE Persona 35*

> “I commission NHS services for a living — I can see this is a coordination failure. I just can't see the system. Four thousand WhatsApp messages, three versions of the budget, and an electrician who needs socket positions by Thursday. I'm the project manager. Nobody hired me. I'd fire me if I could afford to.”

## Her world
Naomi holds the contracts, the budget and the decisions for the most expensive project of her life, in a domain where she can't name the trades. Every professional runs their fragment competently; the gaps between fragments arrive in her inbox at 9pm, dressed as questions she can't evaluate. She makes good decisions slowly and slow decisions cost — and nobody, anywhere, is paid to fix that.

## What she's trying to do
- Get the house built right with her marriage, savings and sleep intact
- Make decisions at a humane pace without idling the trades
- Know the real budget — one number, current, shared with Carl

## What gets in her way
- Decisions arriving un-sized, un-explained and deadline-loaded
- Version chaos she's the unwitting curator of
- The professionals routing through her because their contracts don't connect them

## How she feels about AI
A competent consumer who'd adopt tonight: a status, a decision queue and a budget truth would convert 4,000 messages into a manageable project — and her 3am catastrophising into mere concern.

## In her own words
> “Give me the system I can see: what's decided, what needs me by when, what it all costs. I'm actually good at this part. I've just been doing it blindfolded.”

## Win her by
The civilian hub console: sized decisions with consequences, one budget truth, version-current drawings — the professional integration layer the contracts never created, handed to the person holding them.
