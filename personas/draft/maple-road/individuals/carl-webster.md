# Carl Webster
**Homeowner — Maple Road renovation · 40 · Walthamstow**
*The Quiet Ledger — from Maple Road, ACRE Persona 35*

> “I teach maths. I keep a secret spreadsheet of the real spend because raising it mid-chaos causes rows. The secret spreadsheet says we're £14,200 over the version we discuss at dinner. I'm not hiding it from Naomi. I'm hiding the argument.”

## His world
Carl experiences the renovation as a slow-motion exam in applied probability: every variation a marginal cost, every "while we're at it" a compounding term. His secret ledger isn't deceit — it's conflict scheduling: he's storing the bad news for a calm moment that the build never supplies. The information asymmetry inside the marriage is the project's quietest risk.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep the family solvent through the most expensive thing they'll ever do
- Get the number — one, true, shared — onto the kitchen table without a row
- Support Naomi's hub role without becoming the house's auditor

## What gets in his way
- Three versions of the budget: Naomi's, Steve's valuations, his shadow ledger
- VAT hiding in quotes like a pedagogical trap
- His own conflict-avoidance, compounding at roughly the same rate as the overspend

## How he feels about AI
Wary of gadgets, devoted to anything that makes the number undeniable — a shared, reconciled, current budget would retire the secret spreadsheet and the secrecy with it.

## In his own words
> “One figure, updated automatically, visible to both of us. The truth at the same time, in the same place. Cheaper than counselling and, on current trajectory, necessary sooner.”

## Win him by
Budget truth as a shared surface: every variation priced in, VAT included, both signatures informed — the shadow ledger made obsolete by daylight.
