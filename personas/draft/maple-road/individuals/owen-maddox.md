# Owen Maddox
**Director, Forma Studio (architect) · 43 · Walthamstow**
*The Unpaid Integrator — from Maple Road, ACRE Persona 35*

> “I sold Naomi a design service. What she needed — what every domestic client needs — is an integration service nobody sells. So I do it unpaid, in the margins, because the alternative is watching a family drown in their own project. My accountant has opinions about this.”

## His world
Owen's twenty projects each contain a civilian hub doing badly what no one is contracted to do at all — and the overflow lands on him, because architects are the only professionals who see the whole design intent. He absorbs it for decency and reputation, and watches it eat the design hours that are his actual product and joy.

## What he's trying to do
- Do the thoughtful work Forma exists for — details, light, the rooms people live differently in
- Stop subsidising integration: either it's productised or it's priced
- Differentiate the practice: "our clients don't drown" is a brand, if he could deliver it systematically

## What gets in his way
- Client drowning he can't ethically ignore and can't economically absorb
- The Owen↔Steve gap: two professionals routing through a civilian because their contracts don't touch
- Queries reaching him after the trade improvised

## How he feels about AI
Professionally curious, commercially motivated: the overflow coordination is his margin leak, and a product that equips his clients is one he'd deploy on every project and bill into every fee proposal.

## In his own words
> “Give my clients the integration layer and give me my design hours back. I'll buy it for the practice, deploy it per project, and put it in the fee letter as a feature.”

## Win him by
The Account play, exactly as designed: Forma buys, every project deploys, clients arrive supported by default — his decency converted from margin leak to differentiator.
