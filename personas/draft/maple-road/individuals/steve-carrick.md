# Steve Carrick
**Builder · 50 · Chingford**
*The Fragment Sovereign — from Maple Road, ACRE Persona 35*

> “Third generation. My grandad built half of Higham's Park with a diary and a pencil, and so do I. The programme's in my head, the photos are on my phone, and if your system wants me to type, your system has never met a builder.”

## His world
Steve's programme exists in his head at a fidelity no document matches — which trade, which day, what's behind which wall. His frustrations at Maple Road are the domestic classics: decisions queued behind a civilian's overwhelm, drawings that answer yesterday's questions, and the architect routed through the client because their contracts never met. He's not anti-system; he's anti-*typing*. The Mick Tully condition, confirmed a third time.

## What he's trying to do
- Run four jobs without any of them idling on someone else's decision
- Get discoveries (the wet rot, the surprise drain) priced and agreed fast, before they're arguments
- Finish Maple Road with the family still waving at him in the street — the referral is the pension

## What gets in his way
- Thursday's pour waiting on Tuesday's unanswered question
- "The current drawing" being a matter of opinion
- Variations agreed verbally on a Saturday and disputed on an invoice in March

## How he feels about AI
He doesn't, and won't. But a WhatsApp number that takes his voice notes and photos and turns them into records, schedules and priced variations? That's not technology. That's finally somebody doing the paperwork.

## In his own words
> “I already send the photo and say what I found. If that can become the record and the price without me touching a keyboard, then fine — the future can stay.”

## Win him by
The WhatsApp bridge, total: voice-note-to-variation, photo-to-record, current-drawing-to-his-hand — zero new behaviour, every contribution captured.
