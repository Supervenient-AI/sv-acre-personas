# ACRE Persona 35 — The Civilian Hub
### The Maple Road renovation — a homeowner running a six-figure constellation with no training, seen from the professionals who serve her

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #27 (Family 7, consumer-as-hub); ◆ the hub is a private individual. The Account is the architect's practice — we enter through the professionals; the persona models the civilian at the centre.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Practice Whose Clients Are Amateurs
**Instance:** Forma Studio, Walthamstow — a 6-person residential architecture practice (director, three architects, a Part II, a practice manager). Twenty live projects, mostly extensions and whole-house renovations, £150k–£700k builds. The Account buys; the constellation it sells into is run — by default and disastrously — by its clients.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Every project embeds the practice in a constellation whose hub is an untrained civilian: the homeowner hires the architect, the builder, the engineer and the kitchen company separately, holds all the contracts, and performs — badly, anxiously, at 11pm — the coordination none of them is paid to own. |
| Tooling | The practice: proper drawing and project tools. The constellation: the homeowner's inbox, a WhatsApp group titled "HOUSE!!!", a spreadsheet of doom, and a folder of PDFs she's not sure are the latest. |
| Who carries coordination cost | Contractually: the homeowner. Actually: whoever cracks first — usually the architect (unpaid, scope-creeping) or the homeowner's marriage. |
| Buying behaviour | The practice buys tools normally; the homeowner buys nothing twice — a once-per-decade civilian with no learning curve to amortise. Anything client-facing must need zero training. |
| What failure costs | For the practice: fee erosion (unpaid PM), blame absorption, and reviews written by people judging an experience nobody was responsible for. For the homeowner: the most expensive project of her life run on vibes — overruns measured in school fees, and a kitchen-less Christmas. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A six-figure construction constellation whose hub seat is occupied by an amateur: the homeowner holds the contracts, the budget and the decisions, while the professionals each own a fragment — and the coordination between fragments belongs, by terrifying default, to the person least equipped to do it.

**Cast:**
- **Naomi Webster, 38** — homeowner; NHS commissioning manager; the accidental hub (Maple Road, Walthamstow)
- **Carl Webster, 40** — homeowner; secondary-school teacher; the budget conscience and occasional second front
- **Owen Maddox, 43** — director, Forma Studio; the architect
- **Steve Carrick, 50** — builder; main contractor, three crews across four jobs
- **Plus:** the structural engineer, the kitchen company (its own sub-constellation), the party-wall surveyor, building control, and Maple Road's neighbours

**External immovable nodes:**
- **Building control & the party-wall award** — statutory gates the homeowner barely understands until they bite
- **The mortgage lender's drawdown schedule** — money arrives in stages against milestones; a sequencing constraint nobody told the WhatsApp group about
- **The kitchen company's lead times** — twelve weeks, ordered against a build programme that will move
- **School terms and the family's life** — the renovation happens *around* the constellation's own children; September is a deadline with a uniform

**Topology:** ◆ Hub-and-spoke with an amateur hub: every professional runs their own competent fragment; the integration layer — sequencing, budget truth, decision flow — exists only in Naomi's evenings. The professionals' direct edges (Owen↔Steve) are thin and slightly territorial; everything important routes through a civilian who doesn't know what's important.

**Lifecycle (18 months: 6 design, 9 build, 3 limping):**
- *Design & dreaming:* Forma's competent phase — drawings, planning, Pinterest reconciliation; the budget is a hopeful fiction everyone politely maintains.
- *Tender & the first reckoning:* builders' quotes land 30% over the fiction; scope bleeds; the engineer's steel arrives as a line item nobody romanticised.
- *The build (the long crunch):* the family decamps to Naomi's mother's; daily WhatsApp photos of holes; decisions demanded faster than civilians can make them ("the sparky needs socket positions by Thursday or we lose him a week").
- *Midpoint reset:* the discovery (wet rot, a surprise drain, the 1930s wiring) — budget contingency consumed, scope re-negotiated through gritted teeth, trust between all parties repriced in one bad fortnight.
- *The limp:* snagging, the kitchen company's missing plinth, the final invoice dispute conducted over a half-finished utility room.
- *Dissolution:* the professionals leave; the family moves back in; the constellation survives only as folklore and a fat lever-arch file.
- *Trust curve:* Naomi starts trusting everyone (she chose them) and ends trusting whoever communicated — outcomes mattered less than explanations, the Wrenfield finding in domestic form.

**Cadence:** Daily during the build — site questions, photo updates, decision demands — against a hub who has a job, two children and a 9am commissioning meeting. Decision latency at the hub is the build's biggest hidden cost, and it's nobody's fault.

**Boundary objects:** The drawings (gospel to Owen, "guidance" to Steve, wallpaper to Naomi), the build programme (Steve's, optimistic, never shared in full), the budget spreadsheet (Naomi's, three weeks stale, missing VAT somewhere), the WhatsApp group (the de facto record: 4,000 messages, zero structure), the variations list (existing mostly as resentment), the snagging list.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On Naomi, catastrophically: sequencing trades she can't name, relaying messages between professionals who could but don't talk directly, converting "RSJ" into decisions. Secondary load on Owen, unpaid, because watching a client drown is bad for business and worse for sleep.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Owen holds design intent and regulatory truth. Steve holds site reality and the programme's real state. The engineer holds why the steel matters. The kitchen company holds its own dates and tells no one. Carl holds the actual remaining budget (he tracks it secretly to avoid arguments). Naomi holds everything else — decisions made, promises heard, versions received — in a memory built for NHS commissioning, not construction integration. *Nobody holds the whole*, and the hub knows it, at 3am.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Hub decision latency: professionals stalled on a civilian's overwhelmed inbox — **eliminate** the overwhelm (decisions arriving sized, explained, with consequences and deadlines); the homeowner's right to deliberate is the point of the project
2. Version chaos at the hub: superseded drawings forwarded to the builder — **eliminate** (Wildfen's failure mode 1, with an amateur curator)
3. The professional bypass-gap: Owen and Steve not talking directly, routing through Naomi — **eliminate** the relay; **contain** the territorial caution underneath it (their contracts genuinely don't connect them)
4. Budget truth fragmentation: three versions of "what's left" — **eliminate**
5. The discovery fortnight: contingency consumed in surprise and blame — **contain**: old houses surprise; the eliminable part is the un-priced, un-recorded scramble that follows
6. Steve's "I can't pour Friday if the engineer's detail isn't here Wednesday" — **preserve**: sequencing candour is the build's quality system; it just shouldn't arrive as the hub's panic attack
7. The marriage as load-bearing infrastructure — **contain**, gently: the product can't fix a relationship, but shared truth removes the arguments that were really about information asymmetry (Carl's secret spreadsheet should not need to be secret)

**Conflicting interests:**
- Naomi wants the house in the photos; Carl wants the number in the spreadsheet; both are the client
- Steve's margin lives in variations; Owen's reputation lives in containing them; Naomi pays either way
- Each professional optimises their fragment; integration risk lands on the amateur
- The kitchen company's schedule serves the kitchen company

*Contain-class: the professionals' fragment-optimisation is contractual reality. The product's job is giving the civilian hub the integration layer the contracts never created.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap a trade: Steve absorbs it. Swap *Steve* mid-build (insolvency — it happens): the catastrophic case — site knowledge, programme and sub-contractor loyalties leave; the half-built house is re-tendered at distress prices while the family pays rent and mortgage. Swap *Owen*: design intent survives in drawings; the diplomatic glue doesn't. Swap *Naomi* as hub (illness, or simple surrender): Carl inherits a system he was excluded from; the WhatsApp archaeology takes weeks. The deepest finding: ◆ the civilian hub *cannot be substituted at all* — no professional will take contractual ownership of integration — which is precisely the gap the product fills.

**Afterlife:** The family lives in the result for decades; the professionals' work outlives every relationship. The as-built record (what's in the walls, who installed it, which tile batch) is worth real money at every future repair and sale — and lives in a dead WhatsApp group. For Forma and Steve: the dinner-party verdict drives the next three commissions (Mosshouse's pattern, civilian edition). For the product: the homeowner is a once-a-decade user, but the *professionals* return constellation after constellation — the Account structure is the strategy.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Give the civilian hub a professional's integration layer: one decision queue (sized, explained, deadlined, consequence-tagged), one version-truth for drawings, one budget that reconciles Naomi's spreadsheet, Steve's valuations and Carl's anxiety. Bridge the professional gap — let Owen and Steve coordinate directly *on the record* without anyone's contract changing. Capture the build as it happens (photos, decisions, variations priced at utterance — the Mosshouse 48-hour rule) into an as-built archive the house keeps forever. Translate construction into civilian English, calmly, at 11pm. Enter through the professionals; belong to the family.

## R — Roles

**Archetype load** (3+ = structural overload):

| Seat | Archetypes held | Load |
|---|---|---|
| Naomi | Conductor (untrained) · Client Owner · Coordinator · Sponsor | **4 — the founder-hub pathology in a civilian: the library's most overloaded amateur** |
| Owen | Creative Lead · Gatekeeper (regs) · Coordinator (unpaid overflow) | **3 — the fee-eroding overflow is the practice's chronic complaint** |
| Steve | Production Realiser · Conductor (of trades) | 2 |
| Carl | Sponsor (budget) · Hidden Stakeholder | 2 — secretly running the real spreadsheet |

### Naomi Webster, 38 — Homeowner *(The Accidental Hub)*
**Walthamstow.** Commissions NHS services by day — genuinely good at structured decision-making, which makes the chaos more galling: she can see it's a coordination failure, she just can't see the system. Runs the project from a phone between meetings. **Attitude to AI:** competent consumer (uses it at work); would adopt anything that converts 4,000 WhatsApp messages into a status, a queue and a number. **Winning her looks like:** she makes good decisions at a humane pace, knows the true budget weekly, and gets her evenings — and her sleep — back.

### Carl Webster, 40 — Homeowner *(The Quiet Ledger)*
**Walthamstow.** Teaches maths; tracks the spend secretly because raising it mid-chaos causes rows. **Attitude to AI:** wary of gadgets, devoted to anything that makes the number undeniable. **Winning him looks like:** one shared budget truth, updated without him maintaining it — and the secret spreadsheet retired without a fight.

### Owen Maddox, 43 — Architect *(The Unpaid Integrator)*
**Walthamstow.** Founded Forma to do thoughtful domestic work; spends a fee-eroding fraction of every project doing project management he didn't sell because the alternative is watching clients drown. **Attitude to AI:** professionally curious, commercially motivated — the overflow coordination is his margin leak. **Winning him looks like:** clients arrive supported by default, his design time stops subsidising integration, and Forma sells the better experience as a differentiator.

### Steve Carrick, 50 — Builder *(The Fragment Sovereign)*
**Chingford.** Third-generation builder; good, gruff, allergic to apps (the Mick Tully condition, confirmed at hypothesis level). His programme lives in his head and his diary. **Attitude to AI:** none; will send photos and voice notes to a WhatsApp number forever. **Winning him looks like:** decisions arrive on time, the drawing in his hand is always current, and the variations he flags get priced and agreed before they're arguments.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Naomi ↔ Steve | Building contract | Warm → **discovery-tested**: repriced in the bad fortnight | WhatsApp + site Saturdays | He knows the house's truth; she learns it in invoices |
| Naomi ↔ Owen | Appointment | High → **dependency-deepening**: he's the only translator she has | Email + meetings | His unpaid overflow is her lifeline and his margin leak |
| Owen ↔ Steve | None (both contract to Naomi) | Professional-wary → **the routed-through-the-civilian gap** | Via Naomi, mostly | The Mosshouse triangle without the builder owning design-build integration |
| Naomi ↔ Carl | Marriage | Load-bearing → **information-asymmetry-strained** | Kitchen table | The secret spreadsheet is a symptom; shared truth is the treatment |
| Family ↔ kitchen company / lender / building control | Contracts & statute | Thin → calendar-shaped | Portals + letters | Sub-constellations and gates the hub discovers reactively |
| **Naomi ↔ AI** | — | Ready, relieved | The hub surface | The civilian gets the professional integration layer: the product's ◆ thesis |
| **Owen ↔ AI** | — | Commercially motivated | Practice-side | The Account: he buys it, deploys it per project, differentiates on it |
| **Steve ↔ AI** | — | Refusenik, bridgeable | WhatsApp/voice | The Mick Tully rule, third confirmation: meet the trades where they live |
| **Carl ↔ AI** | — | Number-focused | Budget view | One undeniable figure is his entire feature request |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- ◆ The civilian hub is the family's revelation: the most overloaded, least equipped, highest-stakes hub in the library — and structurally unsubstitutable. The product doesn't relieve this hub; it *equips* it. Entry through the professionals (Owen's practice as the Account), value centred on the amateur.
- The Owen↔Steve gap names a general pattern: professionals whose contracts don't connect them route through the client. An on-the-record direct channel that changes nobody's liability is the cheap, huge win.
- The as-built archive (the house's permanent memory) is the long-tail asset — the first persona where the *building itself* is the afterlife's beneficiary, surviving every human edge in the constellation.
