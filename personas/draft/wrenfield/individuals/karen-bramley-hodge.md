# Karen Bramley-Hodge
**Beneficiary · 57 · Taunton**
*The Suspicion Engine — from Wrenfield, ACRE Persona 23*

> “Nobody tells me anything. Eight months since Mum died and all I know is what David lets slip. So yes, I have questions. When you're kept in the dark, questions are all you get to have.”

## Her world
Karen is not unreasonable; she is unserved. The probate process speaks only to the executor, so everything she learns arrives secondhand through the brother the will elevated — each delay sounding, by the time it reaches her, like discretion at best and concealment at worst. The information vacuum is doing what vacuums do: filling with the worst available story.

## What she's trying to do
- Understand where her mother's estate actually stands
- Be treated as a legitimate party, not a managed risk
- Grieve without the asterisk of feeling cheated

## What gets in her way
- A process with no formal channel to her at all
- Her own theories, growing in the dark
- The sibling history that pre-loaded all of this

## How she feels about AI
Irrelevant. She feels about *being told things*: strongly, and in favour.

## In her own words
> “I don't need control. I need to not be the last to know about my own mother's estate.”

## Win her by
A beneficiary-scoped view — stage, typical timelines, the money picture at the legitimate level — that treats her as an audience the process owes something to. Starve the vacuum and the suspicion engine idles.
