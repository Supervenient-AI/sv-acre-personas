**Case Manager, Wrenfield Estate Services · 30 · Exeter**
*The Thread Runner — from Wrenfield, ACRE Persona 23*

> “Forty estates, each with twenty-odd institutional threads. A thousand little clocks. My spreadsheet has conditional formatting that tells stories — but it only knows what I remembered to tell it.”

## His world
Liam shepherds a thousand parallel threads through institutions that answer when they answer. His discipline — what's the next action on every thread, always — is the operational craft of probate, performed manually at a scale where something is always quietly stalled. He's good enough that the stalls are rare; the vigilance cost is his evenings' background hum.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep every thread moving: chase fired, response logged, next action set
- Catch the bank that's gone quiet in week three, not month three
- Build expertise (reliefs, edge cases) instead of just velocity

## What gets in his way
- Institutions whose every chase starts from zero ("can you confirm the deceased's date of birth again?")
- The chase volume crowding out the interesting law
- His trackers' fidelity depending entirely on his own discipline

## How he feels about AI
Enthusiastic without reservation — chasing institutions deserves no one's creativity. The routine 80% can go today; he'll keep the judgement 20% and finally enjoy it.

## In his own words
> “Run the broom-head: every thread's age, owner and next action, chases firing on schedule. Hand me the exceptions with the history attached. That's the job I actually trained for.”

## Win him by
The next-action engine at full scale — batch chasing, stall detection, evidence filing — promoting him from thread-runner to the estate professional he's becoming anyway.
