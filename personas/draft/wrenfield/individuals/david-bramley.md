## His world
David is doing his last duty for his mother and discovering it's a part-time job in an unfamiliar language. He annotates everything, double-checks everything, and still feels permanently behind — because the process's pace is set by institutions, while its blame, in Karen's eyes, is set by him. The IHT liquidity trap genuinely keeps him up: tax due on assets the grant hasn't yet unlocked.

## What he's trying to do
- Do this properly — provably, defensibly properly
- Keep the peace with Karen, or at least keep the record that peace was offered
- Finish, grieve, and plant the spring garden he's deferred

## What gets in his way
- Institutional latency he can neither speed nor explain
- A sister whose information vacuum fills with theories
- His own reluctance to ring with "silly questions"

## How he feels about AI
Cautiously capable — he ran a school's timetabling, he can use a status page. He'd check it nightly rather than bother anyone, which is precisely the point.

## In his own words
> “A page that says what stage we're at, what's normal, and what needs me. I'd look every evening with a cup of tea, and I'd sleep.”

## Win him by
Self-serve reassurance plus a record that protects him: every step documented, every Karen-question answerable with evidence rather than memory.
