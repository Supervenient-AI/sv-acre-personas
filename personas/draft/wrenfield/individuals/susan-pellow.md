# Susan Pellow
**Director, Wrenfield Estate Services · 54 · Exeter**
*The Steady Hand — from Wrenfield, ACRE Persona 23*

> “Families don't remember how fast the estate settled. They remember whether they understood what was happening while it didn't. Communication is the product. The law is just the machinery.”

## Her world
Susan stands between grieving families and institutions that answer in weeks. Her practice's excellence is two-layered: the legal work (IHT reliefs, contested-will instincts, distributions beyond reproach) and the communication discipline that stops a family's grief curdling into suspicion. She's seen estates break families and families break estates; the monthly update letter is her quiet technology against both.

## What she's trying to do
- Run estates so well documented that a suspicious beneficiary's solicitor finds nothing to feed on
- Keep the update discipline as the firm grows past her ability to personally enforce it
- Protect her team from absorbing forty estates' worth of secondhand grief unsupported

## What gets in her way
- Institutional silence she didn't cause but must answer for
- Threads stalling invisibly among the forty-estate broom-head
- The fixed-fee clock ticking against the thoroughness her name requires

## How she feels about AI
Measured: chasing and assembly, gladly; tax judgement and distributions, never without her; and the tone of anything sent to the recently bereaved gets her eyes until proven, repeatedly, kind.

## In her own words
> “Every letter we send may land on someone's worst year. Get the tone right and I'll consider the rest. Get it wrong once and we're finished.”

## Win her by
Bereavement-grade tone as a proven capability, stalls surfacing themselves, estate accounts assembling continuously — her discipline, institutionalised.
