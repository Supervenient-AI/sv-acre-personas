## His world
Alan operates at the altitude where projects are narratives: he frames AURELIA superbly to the funder, opens doors no SME could, and is unreachable between performances. His group's real AURELIA work happens through Chen Wei, whose career anxieties and contract expiry register with Alan as distant weather. He is not careless — he is *allocated*, across more commitments than any diary survives.

## What he's trying to do
- Convert AURELIA into papers, impact narratives and the next, bigger grant
- Keep his name attached to success and unattached to missed deliverables
- Place Chen Wei somewhere good (he does care, in the abstract, sincerely)

## What gets in his way
- A diary that triple-books itself
- Project realities that need decisions between quarterly meetings
- The gap between his framing and the lab's findings, awkward when wide

## How he feels about AI
Enthusiastic in keynotes, delegating in practice. If the project's record briefs him brilliantly in nine minutes before each meeting, he'll perform the value of the whole system without ever logging in.

## In his own words
> “Give me the one-page truth before the meeting and the impact narrative after it. Between those, you have my blessing and my absence.”

## Win him by
Servicing the absence: pre-meeting briefs distilled from the live record, his strategic moments maximally leveraged, his name never on a surprise.
