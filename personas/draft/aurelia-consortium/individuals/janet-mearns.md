# Janet Mearns
**Research Grants Administrator, University A · 49**
*The Claims Machinery — from AURELIA, ACRE Persona 26*

> “I have outlasted four funding-body rebrands and every academic who ever called my timesheet reminders ‘bureaucracy.' The money arrives because the paperwork is right. The paperwork is right because of me.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 49 |
| Lives | A market town near the university — husband, grown sons, a caravan and a National Trust membership both used properly |
| Background | Thirty years in research finance administration; institutional memory in human form |
| Role | Grants administrator: cost statements, timesheet collection, claim reconciliation across the consortium's university end |
| Reputation | Deadlines not negotiable; chasing legendary; spreadsheets correct |
| Channels | Email (sternly formatted), the research finance system, phone calls that academics dread and need |
| Devices | A desktop with two decades of folder discipline, a wall calendar of claim deadlines in red |

## Her world
Janet's quarterly ritual is the reconciliation of academic chaos into auditable claims: timesheets extracted from people who consider time-recording beneath science, costs evidenced to standards the funder's auditors will test years later. She is unloved at deadline minus five days and indispensable at deadline plus zero, every ninety days, forever.

## What she's trying to do
- Submit claims that are right, on time, every quarter, audit-proof
- Extract timesheets without the chase consuming her week and her charm
- Retire, eventually, leaving a system rather than a séance

## What gets in her way
- Academics' relationship with administrative time, which is theological
- Four organisations' costs converging on one deadline through email
- Funder rule changes arriving mid-grant with retroactive enthusiasm

## How she feels about AI
None whatsoever as a category. As a means to complete, on-time, evidenced cost data? She'd call it the first sensible use of a computer she's seen this decade.

## In her own words
> “If the timesheets arrive complete without me chasing, I genuinely don't care if a robot did it. I'll send the robot a Christmas card.”

## Win her by
Continuous claims-readiness: costs and timesheets accumulating evidenced through the quarter — her standards met by machinery, her chasing retired before she is.
