## His world
Chen Wei does the work the proposal promised, knows precisely where the data diverges from its promises, and holds his scientific honesty against the funder-narrative pressure with quiet firmness — "the data doesn't show what the proposal said" is a sentence he's had to say twice. His real clock isn't the Gantt; it's his contract, his publication record and the IP review standing between his paper and his next job.

## What he's trying to do
- Publish: first-author papers are the currency his career runs on
- Do honest science inside a project with three different definitions of success
- Leave well — knowledge handed over, name attached, bridges intact

## What gets in his way
- IP reviews that take months while his job applications take weeks
- Knowledge-harvest gestures that arrive as extraction rather than attribution
- Being the load-bearing node with the least institutional standing

## How he feels about AI
Native — ELN, pipelines, drafting. His condition mirrors Viktor Hajek's exactly: harvest with attribution (authorship, credited contributions) and he'll feed the system generously; harvest anonymously and the knowledge walks out with him, politely.

## In his own words
> “Run the IP review in days and put my name on what I built. Do both and I'll document everything like my career depends on it — because it does.”

## Win him by
A fast, tracked publication/IP pipeline and attribution-first knowledge capture — the scheduled substitution catastrophe converted into a credited, orderly handover.
