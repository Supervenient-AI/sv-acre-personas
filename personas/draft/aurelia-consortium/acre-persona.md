# ACRE Persona 26 — The Funder-Governed Mesh
### The AURELIA Consortium — a battery-materials SME inside a university–industry research project, seen from the SME's seat

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #18 (Family 4, peer mesh); ◆ adds the funder-governed mesh. The Account is the SME — the seat most likely to buy.*

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Grant-Spliced SME
**Instance:** Voltaic Edge Ltd, Coventry — a 12-person battery-materials company. Lead partner in AURELIA, a 30-month, £1.9m Innovate UK collaborative R&D project with two universities and another SME. The grant is a quarter of Voltaic Edge's runway; the project is half its R&D capacity.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | A work-package mesh across four organisations with incompatible admin systems, plus a funder's reporting regime on top of all of them. Nobody is anybody's client; everybody is the funder's. |
| Tooling | Voltaic Edge: Google Workspace + an ELN (electronic lab notebook). The universities: their own M365 tenancies, their own data policies, SharePoints nobody external can reach. The consortium: email, quarterly meetings, and a shared folder that violates at least one partner's policy. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The SME's R&D director, as project lead — coordinating partners she cannot instruct, against deliverables only the funder can enforce. |
| Buying behaviour | Grant-funded frugality with deadline spikes; tools chargeable to the project budget clear easily, anything else doesn't. Data security matters doubly: trade secrets *and* academic openness in one project. |
| What failure costs | A failed quarterly claim freezes cash flow (the SME pre-funds and reclaims). A missed deliverable threatens the grant. An IP leak — a post-doc's conference preprint with the secret sauce in figure 3 — costs the company its future product. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A multi-organisation research mesh governed not by a client or a lead consultant but by a *funder's regime* — work packages, deliverables, quarterly claims and monitoring officers — stretched across academic and commercial cultures that measure time, success and disclosure in incompatible units.

**Cast:**
- **Dr Sofia Brandt, 42** — R&D director, Voltaic Edge; consortium project lead (Coventry)
- **Prof. Alan Whitcombe, 58** — principal investigator, University A; eminent, elsewhere
- **Dr Chen Wei, 31** — post-doc, University A; does the actual work-package research
- **Janet Mearns, 49** — research grants administrator, University A; the claims machinery
- **Plus:** the second university's PI and post-doc, the other SME's engineer, Voltaic Edge's lab team, the funder's monitoring officer (quarterly, decisive)

**External immovable nodes:**
- **The funder (Innovate UK)** — the regime itself: claim deadlines, deliverable schedule, change-request bureaucracy; can claw back money and does
- **The monitoring officer** — quarterly reviews; the project's de facto judge, working from the paperwork alone
- **The collaboration agreement** — IP, publication rights and background-knowledge clauses negotiated by lawyers two years ago, now half-remembered and fully binding
- **University ethics, finance and REF machinery** — internal gates that outrank the consortium's needs every time

**Topology:** ◆ A mesh with an absent sovereign: the funder governs but doesn't coordinate; the "lead partner" coordinates but can't govern. Authority and articulation live in different places — the defining pathology. Work packages create sub-meshes that barely communicate until integration reveals their divergence.

**Lifecycle (30 months):**
- *Kickoff:* genuine excitement, a Gantt chart of fiction, partner cultures politely colliding.
- *The quarterly pulse:* claims and reports — Janet's machinery vs. Sofia's deadline — punctuating everything.
- *The long middle:* work packages drift apart at their own institutional speeds; the academic clock (papers, terms, REF) and the commercial clock (product, runway) tick at different rates.
- *Midpoint review (built-in):* the funder's mid-term review — the mesh's mandated frame-reset, where scope gets formally re-negotiated through change requests that take longer than the changes.
- *Integration crunch:* month 24 discovers what month 9 assumed; the deliverables converge by heroics.
- *Dissolution:* final report, exploitation plan, the post-doc's contract ends — and with it, most of the project's actual knowledge.
- *Trust curve:* high at kickoff (grant-winning bonhomie); eroded by claim-cycle friction and the slow discovery that partners' real priorities differ from their proposal prose; the funder's trust is paperwork-shaped and非 negotiable.

**Cadence:** Quarterly meetings (the only time the whole mesh assembles), monthly work-package calls (sparsely attended), email threads that die in university inboxes, and the claims-deadline scramble every 90 days.

**Boundary objects:** The Gantt/deliverable schedule (fiction, ritually maintained), the quarterly claim (where four finance systems must agree), the collaboration agreement (consulted only in anger), the shared results (data crossing IP boundaries with every transfer), the conference paper draft (the leak surface), the final report.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On Sofia — chasing partners she can't instruct, translating between lab reality and funder formats, pre-digesting everything for the monitoring officer. Janet carries the claims articulation inside University A; the post-docs carry the actual inter-partner science coordination, unrecognised.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Chen Wei holds the experimental reality of WP2 — and he leaves when his contract ends. Sofia holds the project's true state vs. its reported state (the gap is managed, not dishonest). Alan holds the funder relationships and strategic narrative; he attends quarterly. Janet holds the claims rules. The collaboration agreement's actual meaning lives with lawyers no one wants to wake. *The project's integrated knowledge exists nowhere* — each partner archives their fragment under their own policies.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. The claims scramble: four organisations' timesheets and invoices reconciled by deadline heroics, quarterly, forever — **eliminate**
2. Work-package divergence discovered at integration — **eliminate** the late discovery
3. Post-doc knowledge evaporation at contract end — **eliminate** (the academic version of Northbeam's tenant-trapped knowledge)
4. The preprint leak: academic publication culture vs. commercial IP, colliding in a figure caption — **contain**: publication is the university's legitimate mission; the product's job is making the IP review *fast and certain*, not suppressing the paper
5. Reported-state vs. true-state divergence growing until the mid-term review — **contain**: optimism in funder reports is structural; the internal record must stay true regardless
6. Chen Wei's "the data doesn't show what the proposal promised" — **preserve**: scientific honesty is the project's actual value; funder-narrative pressure must never sand it down
7. Change-request paralysis: the regime's process costing more than the change — **contain**: the regime is immovable; pre-assembled evidence is the available speedup

**Conflicting interests:**
- The universities want papers and REF returns; the SMEs want IP and product; the funder wants impact narratives — three success metrics, one project
- Alan's name is on it; Chen Wei's career needs it; neither incentive matches the Gantt
- Each partner's finance department optimises its own claims position
- Sofia needs partner output she has no authority to compel

*Contain-class: the metric conflict is the family's ◆ feature — it cannot be aligned, only made visible and traded explicitly (a paper delayed for a patent filed, recorded as a deal, not a grudge).*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap Janet: claims chaos for one cycle, then recovery — her machinery is institutional. Swap *Chen Wei* (guaranteed — his contract ends before the project does): WP2's tacit knowledge leaves unless deliberately harvested; the mesh's most predictable, most ignored substitution event. Swap Alan: the funder narrative and academic gravitas wobble; the work barely notices. Swap *Sofia*: the project loses its only articulation engine and its true-state memory simultaneously — the mesh would visibly degrade within one quarter. The funder substitutes monitoring officers without notice; each new one re-reads the paperwork cold — another argument for the record being self-explanatory.

**Afterlife:** The grant ends; the exploitation plan begins (in theory). What persists: IP (per the agreement, if records support the claims), publications, the relationships that seed the *next* consortium bid — grant-world is a repeat game played over decades — and the funder's institutional memory of whether this team delivers. What evaporates: the integrated technical knowledge, with the post-docs. The final report is written for the funder; nobody writes the report the partners themselves need.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Run the quarterly claim as a continuous process, not a deadline event — timesheets, costs and evidence accumulating per partner, reconciled weekly. Keep the true-state record (lab reality, decisions, inter-WP dependencies) alongside the reported-state documents, with the gap visible to Sofia alone. Harvest post-doc knowledge continuously (ELN-integrated, interview-the-work) against the guaranteed departure. Run the IP/publication review as a fast, tracked pipeline. Pre-assemble change-request and review evidence from the live record. Respect every partner's perimeter — the Northbeam architecture, with a funder watching.

### Dr Sofia Brandt, 42 — R&D Director & Project Lead *(The Unempowered Conductor)*
**Coventry.** Ex-automotive battery engineer; co-founded Voltaic Edge on a patent and a grant. Runs the consortium with diplomacy because she has nothing else — no partner reports to her. **Attitude to AI:** pragmatic and security-conscious; already uses it for funder-report drafting, keeps it far from the secret chemistry. **Winning her looks like:** claims stop being quarterly heroics, partner state is visible without chasing, and the project's knowledge survives its post-docs.

### Prof. Alan Whitcombe, 58 — Principal Investigator *(The Eminent Absence)*
**University A.** Three consortia, two editorial boards, one sabbatical on the horizon. Brilliant in the quarterly meeting, unreachable between them. **Attitude to AI:** enthusiastic in keynotes, delegating in practice. **Winning him looks like:** the project produces papers and impact narratives without consuming attention he doesn't have — and his name never lands on a missed deliverable.

### Dr Chen Wei, 31 — Post-doc *(The Departing Knowledge)*
**University A.** Runs WP2's experiments; first-author ambitions, eighteen months left on contract, job applications already open. **Attitude to AI:** native — ELN, analysis pipelines, drafting. **Winning him looks like:** the knowledge harvest credits him visibly (authorship, attribution) and the IP review answers in days, not months — his paper and the patent both ship.

### Janet Mearns, 49 — Grants Administrator *(The Claims Machinery)*
**University A.** Thirty years of research finance; has outlasted four funding-body rebrands. Her spreadsheets are correct; her deadlines are not negotiable; her timesheet chasing is legendary and hated. **Attitude to AI:** none — attitude to *complete, on-time cost evidence*: devotional. **Winning her looks like:** the consortium's claims arrive reconciled, and she stops being the project's least popular email.

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- ◆ The funder-governed mesh splits authority from articulation: the product serves the articulator (Sofia) while satisfying the authority (the regime) — continuous claims-readiness is the killer feature because it serves both at once.
- The post-doc evaporation is a *scheduled* substitution catastrophe — unique in the library for being predictable to the day. Continuous knowledge harvest with visible attribution is the design answer.
- The true-state/reported-state duality is one-truth-per-audience under governance pressure: the internal record must stay honest precisely because the external one is curated. Both, honestly labelled, beat one diplomatic blur.
