*The Process Owner-To-Be — from Calder Wealth, ACRE Persona 5*

## Her world
Sarah is the practice's operational memory: every chase's status, every file's location, every client's paperwork quirk. The processes she runs exist nowhere in writing — they live in James's habits and her reverse-engineering of them. She wants the handover to happen properly, with her on the owning end; what she will not accept is tooling that reads like a stopwatch on her day.

> “I don't want my employees to feel like they're being watched.” — James, in the interview. Sarah heard that, and holds him to it.

## What she's trying to do
- Take whole processes off James — officially, with the authority to run them
- Kill the chase: track every letter of authority without re-deriving its status from her inbox
- Grow into the practice the second hire will make real

## What gets in her way
- Processes that were never written down by the only person who knows them
- Providers who respond in weeks, partially, in PDF
- Tools that help the boss by monitoring the staff

## How she feels about AI
Practical and willing — it's staff for her staff-less role. One bright line: it reports *to* her, never *on* her.

## In her own words
> “Give me the chase board — every provider, every status, every next action — and watch what I do with it.”

## Win her by
Making her the operator of the automation, visibly in charge of more than before. The product's posture matters as much as its features: assistant to Sarah, not auditor of Sarah.
