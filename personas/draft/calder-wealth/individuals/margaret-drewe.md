# Margaret Drewe
**Client, Calder Wealth Planning · 67 · Harrogate**
*The Reason It All Matters — from Calder Wealth, ACRE Persona 5*

> “James explains things without making me feel foolish. That's the entire reason I'm here. The folder of paperwork? I file it. Filing is not reading.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 67 |
| Lives | Harrogate — recently widowed; two daughters, three grandchildren, a garden that gets her through |
| Background | Retired headteacher; capable with money in the everyday sense, at sea in the pensions sense |
| Situation | Consolidating three pensions she half-understands into a retirement she's anxious about |
| Money | Comfortable if managed well; the stakes are her whole later life |
| Channels | Face-to-face meetings, the phone, letters she files unread but keeps forever |
| Devices | An iPad for the grandchildren's photos; online banking, used carefully |

## Her world
Margaret chose James because he talked to her like a person. Since Geoffrey died, the finances that were “his department” have become a source of low-level dread, and James's annual review meeting is the hour where the dread gets smaller. She will never read a suitability letter. She will never forget a wrong name in one.

## What she's trying to do
- Feel safe about money for the first time in three years
- Do right by the girls — inheritance done properly, no mess left behind
- Understand just enough to feel in charge, and delegate the rest to someone she trusts

## What gets in her way
- Jargon, forms, and processes that take months for reasons nobody explains
- Her own reluctance to “bother” anyone with questions
- The quiet fear of being patronised, or worse, processed

## How she feels about AI
She doesn't, and she mustn't have to. If she ever learned a machine drafted the letter that named her daughters, what she'd hear is: *he doesn't really know us.* The product's job in her life is total invisibility.

## In her own words
> “He remembered Lily's graduation. That's worth more than any percentage point.”

## Win her by
Never appearing. She experiences the product only as James seeming less rushed, the paperwork never needing chasing twice, and every name in every letter being exactly right.
