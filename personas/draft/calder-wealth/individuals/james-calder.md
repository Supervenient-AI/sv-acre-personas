*The Excel Romantic — from Calder Wealth, ACRE Persona 5*

> “The job is talking — making people comfortable with their financial lives. The regulator's job is making sure I write it all down for a reader who, in 130 of 140 households, does not exist.”

## His world
James left a big firm to run his own book and discovered the second job hiding inside the first: letters of authority, suitability reports, review letters — weeks of process latency none of which is the talking that clients actually value. He quietly uses AI ahead of his network's approval list because an hour-long letter in ten minutes, times 140 households, isn't a marginal gain. Then a draft invented a child called Riley for a family with three differently-named daughters, and now he checks everything twice and trusts conditionally.

## What he's trying to do
- Spend his hours on the conversations only he can have
- Hand whole processes to Sarah — which first requires writing them down (ongoing, going badly)
- Stay inside the network's good graces while moving faster than its approval list

## What gets in his way
- Provider relay chains: chase, partial data, chase again
- His own delegation reflexes (he was the whole process for fifteen years)
- The Riley problem: AI speed with regulated-trust stakes

## How he feels about AI
Convinced and scorched at once. The philosophy is settled — AI makes employees more productive, it doesn't replace them — but every output now needs a verification layer he can see.

## In his own words
> “Show me every name and number checked against the source file before I sign. Do that, and you can have the whole back office.”

## Win him by
Verification as a visible surface — every fact traceable before signature — plus the LOA chases gone from his evenings. Bonus: be the product his network approves, and he'll introduce you to 2,000 colleagues.
