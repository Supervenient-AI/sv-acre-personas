# ACRE Persona 5 — The Regulated Satellite
### Calder Wealth Planning — a solo financial adviser and PA operating inside a national network's compliance perimeter

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Franchise Satellite
**Instance:** Calder Wealth Planning, York. A newly formed one-adviser practice (adviser + PA, second staff member arriving within months) operating under a large national wealth-management network, serving ~140 client households.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Constrained: every workflow is a relay through institutions the practice doesn't control — the network above, product providers alongside, the client at the centre. |
| Tooling | The network approves the software list: its own messaging app (encrypted docs, digital signatures), Salesforce, a cashflow-modelling platform, a client-portal product. Microsoft 365 underneath everything. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The PA, increasingly — chasing providers, assembling data, running the document mill. The adviser carries it wherever delegation hasn't happened yet, which is most places. |
| Buying behaviour | The network has approved some AI (meeting transcription) and not others — which the practice quietly uses anyway, on the reasoning that the value is too large to wait: AI-drafted review letters take 10 minutes instead of an hour, across 140 households. The risk calculus is personal. |
| What failure costs | Trust is the product. One confabulated detail in a client letter, one compliance incident, and the network's reputation engine lands on the practice, hard. There is no brand to hide behind — the adviser *is* the brand. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A tiny regulated practice whose every project is a relay chain through institutions it doesn't control — the network above, product providers alongside, the client at the centre — with trust as the actual product.

**Cast:**
- **James Calder, 43** — adviser and founder (York)
- **Sarah Whitlow, 34** — PA (York)
- **The network's compliance function** — faceless, decisive
- **Product providers** — pension and investment companies on the other end of letters of authority ("Aegon-equivalents")
- **The client households** — e.g. **Margaret Drewe, 67**, retired headteacher, recently widowed, consolidating pensions

**External immovable nodes** (outside the working team, veto power over it):
- **The FCA** — Consumer Duty, suitability requirements; the reason the review letter exists at all
- **The network's compliance function** — sits in the cast *and* above it: approves tools on its own schedule, audits files, punishes incidents without appeal
- **HMRC's calendar** — tax year end turns March into a constellation-wide crunch nobody scheduled
- **Provider back offices** — institutional counterparties who respond in weeks, in PDFs, incompletely, by design of nobody in particular

**Topology:** Satellite-and-relays. The practice orbits the network (which sets the rules and takes a cut of everything) while running horizontal relay chains to providers: client signs a letter of authority → adviser submits to provider → provider returns incomplete information → clarification round-trips → data lands in the practice's template → analysis → suitability letter. Weeks of latency, none of it value-adding.

**Lifecycle:** This constellation never dissolves — it pulses:
- *Per household, annually:* the review cycle — data gathering, the meeting (the part that matters), the letter (the part the regulator requires), across 140 households in a rolling wave.
- *Per new client:* the onboarding relay — LOA chains at their worst, six-to-twelve weeks of chasing before any advice can be given. The client's enthusiasm decays measurably across this gap.
- *Annually, for everyone:* tax year end — allowances expire on a fixed date; every procrastinated decision lands in the same six weeks.
- *The practice's own arc:* newly independent, hiring its second staff member, building toward a sellable book — every undocumented process is a debt against that future.
- *Trust curve:* with clients, slow-building and decades-long (the opposite of the temporary-team pattern) — but spent instantly by error: one wrong child's name in a letter outweighs three good years.

**Cadence:** Meeting-driven with clients (annual or semi-annual, human, unhurried — deliberately so); bursty and chase-driven with providers; submission-and-silence with the network.

**Boundary objects:** The review letter (regulatorily required, written for a reader who mostly doesn't exist — perhaps 10 of 140 households read them), the letter of authority, the suitability report, the cashflow model, the meeting transcript.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** Currently on James, because the processes live in his head; structurally it belongs to Sarah, who is inheriting the LOA misery without the documentation. The transfer of articulation work *is* the practice's current project, and it's going slowly.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** James holds every client's story, family and anxieties — and the un-written processes. Sarah knows where everything is filed and what state every chase is in. The providers hold the actual policy data, behind weeks of latency. The network holds the rules, published on its own schedule. Nothing holds the processes: the practice's operating manual is an oral tradition with two speakers.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged: eliminate / contain / preserve)*:
1. AI confabulation in client-facing documents — a review letter recently invented a child ("Riley") for a family with three differently-named daughters; sent unchecked, that destroys the only asset that matters — **eliminate**, via verification against source records, never via abstinence
2. The letters-of-authority relay: chase, receive partial data, chase again — drudgery that is "no good for anybody" — **eliminate**
3. Delegation failure: a founder used to doing everything, now needing the PA to absorb whole processes that exist nowhere in writing — **eliminate**
4. Tool schism: the network approves slowly; capability arrives quickly; the practice quietly lives in the gap — **contain**: a genuine conflict between the network's institutional risk and the practice's competitiveness; it cannot be resolved by the practice, only managed visibly enough to be defensible
5. Compliance review slowing everything down — **preserve**: the network's awkward "no" is the client-protection system working; the AI must never help route around it
6. James spending "unproductive" hours in unhurried client conversation — **preserve**: this is the product; any efficiency drive that compresses it destroys value

**Conflicting interests:**
- The network wants control and auditability; the adviser wants speed and his evenings
- Sarah wants real responsibility without becoming the one watched by monitoring software ("I don't want my employees to feel like they're being watched")
- The providers are structurally indifferent to everyone's timelines
- The client wants a human who knows their children's actual names

*Contain-class: the network/practice tension especially — it is permanent, structural, and the practice's quiet workarounds are themselves a risk surface the product must make legitimate rather than hide.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap Sarah and the practice loses the only map of every in-flight chase and filing decision — there is no handover document because there is no documentation; James absorbs her work back and the delegation project resets to zero. Swap *James* (illness, the regulatory nightmare) and the business halts entirely: clients are contractually the practice's but relationally his, and the network would reassign them within weeks. Swap a provider contact and nobody notices, because the provider edge was never personal — which is exactly why it's automatable.

**Afterlife:** Client relationships run for decades and outlive any single product cycle — each household is a perpetual constellation that periodically activates. The practice is building a sellable book: at exit, the documented client records *are* the asset, and every process that lives only in James's head is a discount on the sale price. The network relationship persists regardless; its memory of incidents is long and unforgiving.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Run the relay chains — track every letter of authority, chase providers, flag what came back incomplete, assemble the data into the practice's templates. Draft review and suitability letters with a *verification layer* that surfaces every factual claim (names, figures, products) against source records before anything human-signs. Brief Sarah into processes James has never written down — by observing them. Operate inside the approved perimeter — or be the product the network itself approves.

## R — Roles

**Archetype load** (count of collaboration archetypes per seat; 3+ signals structural overload):

| Seat | Archetypes held | Load |
|---|---|---|
| James | Conductor · Specialist Maker (the advice) · Coordinator · Sponsor | **4 — the solo-practice pathology: the rainmaker is also the back office** |
| Sarah | Coordinator (inheriting) · Production Realiser | 2 — rising as delegation lands |
| The network | Gatekeeper · Hidden Stakeholder | 2 — a weather system with both roles |
| Providers | Gatekeeper (of their own data) | 1 |
| Margaret (client) | Client Owner | 1 — and must never feel like a "seat" in anything |

The product's first job: move Coordinator from James to Sarah-plus-AI, and keep the Specialist Maker hours for the conversations only James can have.

### James Calder, 43 — Adviser & Founder *(The Excel Romantic)*
**York.** Ex-rail demand modeller; builds beautiful spreadsheets nobody asked for. The actual job is talking — making people comfortable with their financial lives — and he's clear-eyed that most clients never read the paperwork the regulator requires him to produce. Philosophy: AI makes employees more productive, it doesn't replace them. Currently teaching himself to delegate, which is going about as well as it usually does. **Attitude to AI:** quietly ahead of his network's approval list, because an hour-long letter in 10 minutes times 140 households is not a marginal gain — but the invented-child incident haunts him. **Winning him looks like:** the letters draft themselves *with a verification pane he can trust*, the LOA chases vanish from his evenings, and nothing AI-touched ever reaches a client unverified.

### Sarah Whitlow, 34 — PA *(The Process Owner-To-Be)*
**York.** Poached from his previous firm; knows where everything is. About to inherit the letters-of-authority misery and would pay personally to have it automated. **Attitude to AI:** practical and willing — with one bright line: anything that reads like monitoring software changes her relationship with the job. **Winning her looks like:** she owns whole processes end-to-end with the AI as her staff, the chase-status of every provider visible at a glance — and the tool reports to *her*, not on her.

### The Network *(The Weather System)*
Not a person; a compliance function. Approves tools on its own schedule; punishes incidents without appeal. Every product decision routes around or through it. **Attitude to AI:** institutional — slow, liability-shaped, eventually decisive. **Winning it looks like:** a product so auditable the network approves it network-wide, converting 1 practice into 2,000.

### Margaret Drewe, 67 — Client *(The Reason It All Matters)*
**Harrogate.** Retired headteacher, recently widowed, consolidating three pensions she half-understands into a retirement she's anxious about. Chose James because he explained things without condescending. Will never read a suitability letter; will never forget a wrong name in one. **Attitude to AI:** none, and it must stay that way — she's buying a human who knows her. **Winning her looks like:** she never encounters the product at all; she just notices that James seems less rushed and nothing ever needs chasing twice.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Practice ↔ network | Franchise/appointed-rep agreement | Dependent → **stable but absolute**: autonomy exists at the network's pleasure. Sustained by: clean audits. Eroded by: one incident, permanently | Portal + bulletins | All the structural power one way; all the daily ingenuity the other. Product strategy may mean selling to the network, not the satellite |
| James ↔ Margaret (clients) | Advice agreement | Deep, decades-long → **slow-building, instantly spendable**: one clanger is unrecoverable | Meetings + phone; paper she files unread | The edge AI must never visibly touch — fully human by design |
| Practice ↔ providers | LOAs, institutional | None — pure process → flat | Letters, PDFs, hold music | Weeks of latency, zero relationship at risk: the single best-ROI edge to automate |
| James ↔ Sarah | Employment | High, growing → **the delegation edge**: trust exists, documentation doesn't | Office + Teams | James holds undocumented processes; Sarah holds operational state. The product is the medium through which handover finally happens |
| Sarah ↔ providers | Process | Transactional → flat | Phone + email | Where the chase actually lives, invisible to everyone upstream |
| Practice ↔ FCA / HMRC calendar | Statutory | N/A | Deadlines | The regulator's dates create the constellation's only true synchrony |
| **James ↔ AI** | — | High but scorched once | In-product | The "Riley" incident defines this edge: trust is conditional on verification being visible, every time |
| **Sarah ↔ AI** | — | Eager, with a surveillance tripwire | In-product | Adopts as her own staff; rejects on first sign of being its subject |
| **Network ↔ AI** | — | Unapproved → the gap the practice lives in | Approval process | The product either fits the perimeter or becomes the perimeter |
| **Margaret ↔ AI** | — | Must remain non-existent | None | An anti-edge: its quality is measured by her never noticing |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- The provider edges are the beachhead: high pain, zero relationship risk, instantly demonstrable value (LOA tracking and chasing).
- The James↔AI edge requires a verification layer as a first-class surface — every name, figure and product claim traceable to a source record before signature. This is the difference between the product and the incident.
- The network edge is the scaling decision: build defensibly inside the approved list, or build the thing the network approves. Both start with auditability.
- Sarah's tripwire generalises: in two-person practices, "tool that helps the assistant" and "tool that watches the assistant" are one design decision apart.
