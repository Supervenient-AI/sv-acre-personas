# Carmen Núñez
**Founder & MD, Linden Translations · 47 · London**
*The Orchestrator — from Linden Translations, ACRE Persona 19*

> “Machine translation was supposed to kill us in 2017. Instead it killed the commodity layer and left us the judgement, the accountability and the network. AI is the third restructuring of my industry I've survived. I plan to enjoy this one.”

## Her world
Carmen runs a company whose entire production capacity goes home every night to four hundred different homes. Her margins survive on orchestration: the right linguist, the fast answer, the consistent term, the payment that lands on the nail. She watched MT eat per-word rates and repositioned the firm on what machines can't do alone — and she's clear-eyed that the same logic now applies to her own operations layer.

## What she's trying to do
- Sell judgement and accountability at margins that survive per-word arithmetic
- Keep the network loyal — payment punctuality and respect are the only durable glue
- Handle the TM/AI question honestly before it handles her

## What gets in her way
- Query latency across timezones turning 30-second answers into 24-hour delays
- Client expectations set by MT prices and native-quality demands simultaneously
- The industry's harvest problem: linguists' work training the systems that compress linguists' rates

## How she feels about AI
A veteran's calm: neither saviour nor enemy, just the next restructuring. She'll adopt deeply and contract honestly — the agency that's straight with its network about what trains on what wins the network.

## In her own words
> “The network is the company. Treat four hundred freelancers well at scale — that's not sentiment, that's the entire balance sheet.”

## Win her by
Operations that compound (query auto-answering, living style guides, network health visible) and harvest terms she can publish to her linguists without flinching.
