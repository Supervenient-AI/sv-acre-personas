## Her world
Becca's day is a continuous dispatch problem: jobs land, linguists get picked (the decision the whole business rides on, made on memory and availability), queries flow back, deadlines compress. Her head holds client preferences ("this client hates formal German") that should live in style guides and don't, and her inbox is the routing layer between four hundred specialists and forty clients.

## What she's trying to do
- Hit every deadline without burning the linguists who make hitting deadlines possible
- Kill query latency — the single dumbest source of delay in her week
- Get the preference folklore out of her head and into the system

## What gets in her way
- Routine queries waiting a day for thirty-second answers
- Selection by recall when the evidence (scorecards, domain history) exists but isn't at hand
- Terminology drift across split jobs surfacing at review

## How she feels about AI
Pragmatic maximalist: auto-answer the routine queries from the TM and style guide, surface the selection evidence, chase the deliveries — and leave final delivery judgement with her.

## In her own words
> “Answer the translator's question from what we already know, instantly, and wake me only for the genuinely new ones. That's half my job done right there.”

## Win her by
Query auto-resolution and evidence-at-dispatch — her twenty-five jobs running on system memory instead of hers.
