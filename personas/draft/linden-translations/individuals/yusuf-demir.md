## His world
Yusuf's value is knowing where the machine is wrong: the fintech register, the legal nuance, the Turkish that reads as written-by-a-human-who-knows-banking. He drafts with MT and AI daily — his craft moved up a layer years ago. What he watches warily is the harvest: his TMs, his corrections, his expertise feeding systems whose owners then negotiate his rate down. Agencies that are honest about this get his loyalty; the others get his invoice terms.

## What he's trying to do
- Stay first-call in a compressing market by being un-substitutable in his domain
- Get queries answered in minutes — his day is timezone-sensitive and silence is expensive
- Know, in writing, what his work trains and what it doesn't

## What gets in his way
- Query latency: a London afternoon question costs his Istanbul working day
- Briefs that disrespect his expertise while depending on it
- The unspoken harvest economics of the whole industry

## How he feels about AI
Fluent, unsentimental, professionally invested in its limits. The tools are his; the terms of their feeding are the open question he wants answered like an adult.

## In his own words
> “Pay on the nail, answer in minutes, and tell me honestly what trains on my work. Do those three things and I'll move your deadline mountains.”

## Win him by
Instant query resolution, payment punctuality made visible, and published, honest linguistic-asset terms — respect, operationalised.
