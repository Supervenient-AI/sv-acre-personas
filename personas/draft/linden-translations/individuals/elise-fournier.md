# Elise Fournier
**Localisation Manager (client-side) · 36 · Paris/London**
*The Continuous Client — from Linden Translations, ACRE Persona 19*

> “I'm judged on release-day quality in fourteen languages. I speak three. My whole job is choosing whom to trust and being able to prove the trust was earned.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 36 |
| Lives | Between Paris and London (the Eurostar knows her) — partner in Paris, product team in London |
| Background | Translation studies, then in-house localisation at two software firms; technically deep for the role |
| Role | Owns fourteen languages for a scaling software product; Linden carries eight of them |
| Pressure | In-country reviewers (employees, distributors) who judge after release, with politics attached |
| Channels | The TMS portal, email, quarterly vendor reviews she runs with spreadsheets and standards |
| Devices | Laptop, a phone with string-freeze dates in the calendar |

## Her world
Elise ships software in languages she can't read, on dates engineering compresses, through vendors she must evaluate on evidence rather than instinct. She runs her own MT-evaluation pilots, knows exactly where AI sits in the quality stack, and respects vendors who are honest about their chain. Her nightmare is specific: a terminology inconsistency a Korean distributor finds on release day, with a screenshot, in an email to her boss.

## What she's trying to do
- Ship release-day quality in fourteen languages, provably
- Get terminology consistency she can demonstrate, not just hope for
- Keep her vendor mix honest, evaluated and slightly competitive

## What gets in her way
- Strings arriving late while release dates hold
- In-country reviewer verdicts arriving after the only moment they could help
- Vendors whose AI use is a secret she's expected not to ask about

## How she feels about AI
Sophisticated and unsentimental — she evaluates engines for a living. Vendors who document their human/machine chain earn trust; vendors who hide it earn audits.

## In her own words
> “Show me the terminology compliance per release and the process behind each deliverable. Evidence is the relationship.”

## Win her by
Demonstrable consistency (term-compliance reports she can forward) and a transparent process chain — turning Linden from a supplier she manages into the benchmark she measures others against.
