# ACRE Persona 11 — The Maker Between Studios
### Aldercroft Joinery — a 6-person bespoke joinery workshop serving interior designers, architects and their clients

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #3 (Family 1, hub-and-spoke); not yet interview-grounded.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Craft-Proud Designer-Maker
**Instance:** Aldercroft Joinery, near Stroud. Founder-maker, three bench joiners, an apprentice, and a part-time bookkeeper. £450k turnover; order book 4–6 months deep; one CNC machine bought with a grimace.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Permanently mid-chain: upstream are designers and architects who specify; downstream are finishers, fitters and sites they don't control. The workshop almost never meets the person who'll live with the piece. |
| Tooling | Email and phone, genuinely. Drawings printed and pencil-marked. A whiteboard with the order book. Xero, operated monthly under duress. The CNC has better software than the office. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The founder — quoting, sequencing the workshop, chasing specs, booking the spray shop, arranging deliveries, all between bench hours he refuses to give up. |
| Buying behaviour | Buys machinery after years of deliberation and software never. Would pay for protected bench time, if anyone sold it. |
| What failure costs | A piece made to a superseded drawing is days of material and labour, unrecoverable. A missed install window at a site they don't control can orphan a finished piece for months — and the invoice with it. |

---

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A small workshop as the making-node between multiple specifying studios and the sites where work lands — every project arriving through someone else's constellation, with specs that evolve upstream while timber gets cut downstream.

**Cast:**
- **Rhys Penhale, 51** — founder and master joiner, the hub (Stroud)
- **Jamie Dunmore, 26** — apprentice; the only person who likes computers
- **Imogen Sorrel, 47** — interior designer client (Sorrel & Gray, Bath) — a recurring specifier *(see Persona 9)*
- **Gavin Leach, 47** — spray-shop owner; the finishing bottleneck (Gloucester)
- **Plus:** three bench joiners, architect specifiers, timber merchants, site fitters, main contractors whose programmes set install dates

**External immovable nodes:**
- **Timber supply** — kiln schedules, FSC chains, a walnut shortage nobody can negotiate with
- **The spray shop queue** — Gavin's lead time is two weeks until it's five, discovered at booking
- **Site programmes** — install windows set by builders the workshop has never met, moving without notice

**Topology:** A spoke that is itself a hub: Aldercroft sits at the periphery of five or six client constellations simultaneously (the Halyard & Vance condition), while running its own internal hub of benches, machines and suppliers. The order book is the collision zone where everyone else's timelines meet.

**Lifecycle (per commission, 6–16 weeks, ~10 live at once):**
- *Quote:* drawings arrive incomplete; Rhys prices the gaps from experience and hope.
- *Spec-settling:* the dangerous overlap — design still moving upstream while material gets ordered. The version that reaches the bench is the version that gets built.
- *Making:* heads-down weeks; upstream silence interpreted as approval.
- *Finishing & install crunch:* the spray queue, the site's shifting readiness, and delivery converge; the piece is finished but homeless, or the site is ready and the piece is in Gavin's queue.
- *Dissolution:* invoice, retention quibbles, photographs for Instagram; the spec archaeology that made it buildable is binned with the offcuts.
- *Trust curve:* specifiers extend trust on craft reputation; it erodes not through quality (rare) but through *dates* — and the dates are mostly hostage to nodes upstream and downstream of the bench.

**Cadence:** Phone calls at 7am and 6pm; email in batches when Rhys surfaces; weeks of silence mid-make that designers experience as risk.

**Boundary objects:** The workshop drawing (Rhys's redraw of the designer's intent — where translation errors live), the quote, the order book whiteboard, the sample/finish swatch, the delivery note.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On Rhys, entirely: sequencing ten commissions across four benches, one CNC, one spray shop and six other people's site programmes — in his head, at 5am.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Rhys holds every spec's real state and every client's tolerances (dimensional and personal). The bench joiners hold make-state. Jamie holds the photos. Gavin knows his true queue. Designers hold intent-changes they assume were communicated. The whiteboard knows the plan as of last Tuesday.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Superseded-drawing builds — the workshop executes version 3 while the designer is on version 5 — **eliminate**
2. Install-window roulette: finished pieces orphaned by site delays nobody relayed — **eliminate**
3. Quote-gap absorption: pricing incomplete specs, then eating the difference — **contain**: spec evolution is legitimate; the silent absorption of its cost is not
4. The spray-shop discovery: Gavin's queue learned at booking, not at planning — **eliminate**
5. Rhys's bench time consumed by coordination — **eliminate**
6. Rhys's "that veneer will cup in underfloor-heated rooms" — **preserve**: the maker's no, again, is the product's quality system

**Conflicting interests:**
- Designers want late flexibility; the workshop needs early freezes — the central, permanent trade
- Rhys wants bench hours; the business needs his phone hours
- Gavin sequences his queue for his margin, not Aldercroft's deadlines
- Sites want delivery exactly when ready and never before; the workshop needs the space cleared

*Contain-class: the AI prices and timestamps flexibility instead of letting craft pride absorb it silently.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap a bench joiner: weeks of ramp, manageable. Swap Gavin (he retires soon — known risk): the finishing knowledge of every recurring spec leaves the constellation; reproducible finishes become unreproducible. Swap *Rhys*: the business is over — the order book, every spec's true state and thirty years of specifier trust are one person. Designers know this; it caps the order book at what one head can hold.

**Afterlife:** Pieces outlive everyone — and come back: repairs, matching additions, "can you do the same in oak" requests years later, answerable only if the spec archaeology survived. Specifier edges are decades-long repeat games; one missed install window with a new designer ends a relationship that took years to win. Today's archive: a shed of paper drawings, Jamie's phone, and Rhys.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Hold one live spec per commission and broadcast supersessions to the bench the hour they happen. Track every commission against spray queue, site programme and delivery window — and flag the collisions a month out. Turn upstream changes into priced, dated variations automatically. Give specifiers ambient progress (photos, milestones) so silence stops costing trust. Answer "can you do the same in oak" from the archive. Cost Rhys nothing at the bench.

## R — Roles

**Archetype load** (3+ = structural overload):

| Seat | Archetypes held | Load |
|---|---|---|
| Rhys | Conductor · Specialist Maker · Coordinator · Gatekeeper (buildability) | **4 — the maker-founder pathology: the best pair of hands chained to the phone** |
| Jamie | Newcomer · Coordinator (accidental, digital) | 2 |
| Imogen (specifier) | Client Owner · Creative Lead | 2 — from this seat, the Persona 9 hub is a demanding spoke |
| Gavin | Production Realiser · Gatekeeper (the queue) | 2 |

### Rhys Penhale, 51 — Founder *(The Hands and the Phone)*
**Stroud.** Thirty years at the bench; can read a board's tension before the saw does. Email in monosyllables, phone in paragraphs. Quietly proud, quietly drowning in coordination. **Attitude to AI:** none — but he had none about the CNC either, and now defends it in the pub. Adopts tools that respect the craft and produce fewer surprises. **Winning him looks like:** the bench gets him back two days a week and no piece is ever built to a dead drawing again.

### Jamie Dunmore, 26 — Apprentice *(The Accidental Digitiser)*
**Gloucester.** Second-career apprentice (ex-retail). Photographs every build stage for the workshop Instagram, which is accidentally the firm's best archive. **Attitude to AI:** native; already drafts Rhys's awkward emails on his phone. **Winning him looks like:** his photos and notes become the official record, and he's visibly the reason the workshop modernised.

### Imogen Sorrel, 47 — Specifier *(The Upstream Hub)*
**Bath.** *(Persona 9's principal, seen from the workshop's side.)* Sends beautiful, incomplete drawings; changes her mind with charm and consequence. **Attitude to AI:** whatever shows her commission's progress without phoning. **Winning her looks like:** she sees the piece growing weekly and her changes come back priced in hours, not arguments.

### Gavin Leach, 47 — Spray-Shop Owner *(The Bottleneck)*
**Gloucester.** One booth, two staff, every joinery shop in the county in his queue. Schedules by phone and favouritism. **Attitude to AI:** amused hostility. **Winning him looks like:** bookings arrive complete (spec, sheen, date) and nobody rings to ask where things are — because the answer's already visible.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rhys ↔ Imogen | PO per commission | Deep, years → **date-fragile**: craft trust total, schedule trust thin | Phone + email | She holds intent and the end-client; he holds buildability and the real timeline |
| Rhys ↔ Gavin | Informal, decades | Warm → **queue-opaque** | Phone | His bottleneck is invisible until it's everyone's problem |
| Rhys ↔ sites/contractors | None — via specifiers | Thin → **the orphaned-piece edge** | Relayed messages | Install windows move without anyone owing Aldercroft a phone call |
| Rhys ↔ bench team | Employment | High, craft-bonded → stable | Workshop air | Make-state lives here; spec-state lives in the office; the gap is failure mode 1 |
| Rhys ↔ timber merchants | Trade account | Long → **supply-shocked** | Phone | They know about shortages first; he learns at order time |
| **Rhys ↔ AI** | — | Indifferent until it saves a build | Email/phone bridge | Like Dave Hartley (Persona 1): meet him in his habits, win him with one prevented disaster |
| **Jamie ↔ AI** | — | Native, keen | In-product | The workshop's adoption route runs through the apprentice |
| **Imogen ↔ AI** | — | Already courted (Persona 9) | Cross-constellation | If both hubs adopt, this edge becomes the library's first **product-to-product edge** |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- This persona is the library's purest *multi-membership* case after Halyard & Vance: one workshop inside six client constellations. The product must give Rhys one order book across all of them, while each specifier sees only their slice — one truth per legitimate audience, applied to a craftsman's diary.
- The Imogen↔Rhys edge is the strategic one: when a specifier's constellation and a maker's constellation both run on the product, the spec, the variation and the install window stop being relayed and start being shared. Design for the join.
- Adoption enters through Jamie and lands as relief for Rhys: the champion and the beneficiary are different people — price and pitch accordingly.
