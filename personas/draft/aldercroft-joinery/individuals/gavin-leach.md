# Gavin Leach
**Spray-Shop Owner · 47 · Gloucester**
*The Bottleneck — from Aldercroft, ACRE Persona 11*

> “Everyone thinks their job's next in the booth. The booth has opinions. The booth has a queue. The queue is in my head, where it's safe.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 47 |
| Lives | Gloucester — divorced, remarried, a caravan in Pembrokeshire for the August shutdown |
| Background | Twenty-five years of finishing: car bodies first, then furniture when the money moved |
| Business | One spray booth, two staff, every joinery shop in the county in his queue |
| Power | His lead time is "two weeks" until it's five — discovered at booking, never before |
| Channels | Phone, exclusively; scheduling by memory and favouritism |
| Devices | A diary by the compressor, a phone with paint on it |

## His world
Gavin is the county's finishing bottleneck and he knows it — the queue's opacity is partly his margin and wholly his power. He schedules by relationship: the shops that book complete (spec, sheen, dates) and don't ring him twice a day get the good slots. Aldercroft is mostly one of those, which is why Rhys's jobs jump the queue more often than Rhys realises.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep the booth full but never jammed — the difference is his profit and his blood pressure
- Reward the easy customers, quietly punish the chaotic ones
- Retire in eight years with the goodwill sold to someone who'll keep the standard

## What gets in his way
- Jobs arriving incomplete: no sheen spec, no date, no idea
- The where's-my-job phone calls that interrupt the spraying that would answer them
- Everyone's deadline being his emergency

## How he feels about AI
Amused hostility. He's not adopting anything. But bookings that arrive complete and a status page that quiets his phone? He'd accept that the way he accepts good weather — without crediting anyone.

## In his own words
> “Send the job complete and leave me alone. You'll get it back beautiful. Ring me daily and you'll get it back eventually.”

## Win him by
Never asking him to use anything: complete bookings flow to him, status flows back from one weekly grunt, and his phone goes blessedly quiet. The bottleneck stays sovereign; the queue stops being a mystery.
