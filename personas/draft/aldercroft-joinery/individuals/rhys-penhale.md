# Rhys Penhale
**Founder & Master Joiner, Aldercroft Joinery · 51 · Stroud**
*The Hands and the Phone — from Aldercroft, ACRE Persona 11*

> “I can read a board's tension before the saw touches it. What I can't read is which of my ten commissions just had its drawings changed by an email I haven't opened.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 51 |
| Lives | Outside Stroud — wife, two grown kids, a workshop that's the real family home |
| Background | Thirty years at the bench; apprenticed at sixteen, founded Aldercroft at thirty-five |
| Role | Founder, master joiner, and — resentfully — coordinator of ten live commissions across four benches, one CNC and other people's site programmes |
| Money | £450k turnover, order book 4–6 months deep, margin eaten quietly by spec changes absorbed for free |
| Channels | Phone at 7am and 6pm; email in batches when he surfaces; pencil on printed drawings |
| Devices | An office laptop he visits, a phone he answers, a CNC with better software than the business |

## His world
Rhys is the best pair of hands in the building, chained to the phone. Every commission arrives through someone else's constellation — a designer's project, an architect's site — and his order book is where everyone's timelines collide. He deliberated for three years before buying the CNC and now defends it in the pub; that's his adoption curve in miniature: glacial, then absolute.

## What he's trying to do
- Get back to the bench two days a week — the work only he can do
- Never again build a piece to a drawing that died upstream four days earlier
- Keep thirty years of specifier trust by hitting dates he doesn't fully control

## What gets in his way
- Specs that evolve while timber's being cut
- Gavin's spray queue and other people's install windows, discovered late
- Coordination that lives entirely in his head at 5am

## How he feels about AI
None — yet. Like the CNC: he'll resist on principle, adopt on evidence, and evangelise on results. Tools that respect the craft and prevent one dead-drawing build earn a hearing.

## In his own words
> “Every drawing in the workshop, current, the hour it changes. Do that one thing and you can call yourself whatever you like.”

## Win him by
One prevented disaster — a supersession broadcast to the bench before the cut. Then give him his bench days back and let Jamie run the screens.
