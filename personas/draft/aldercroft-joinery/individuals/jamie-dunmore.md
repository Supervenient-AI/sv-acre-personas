## His world
Jamie came for the craft and found a second job nobody advertised: being the only person under fifty who likes computers. His build-stage photos pull in commissions from designers who found the workshop on Instagram; his drafts of Rhys's awkward emails keep clients warm. He's learning dovetails by day and accidentally building the firm's institutional memory by phone.

## What he's trying to do
- Become a proper joiner — the apprenticeship is the point, not the phone
- Make his digital instincts *count*: the archive, the shopfront, the systems the workshop needs
- Be visibly the reason the workshop modernised, without becoming "the IT lad" instead of a craftsman

## What gets in his way
- Apprentice standing: he can see the broken process and can't yet say so
- His photo archive living on a personal phone with no backup
- The bench-versus-screen tension in his own week

## How he feels about AI
Native. Already drafts emails with it, would wire up the whole workshop tomorrow if asked. The asking is the missing part.

## In his own words
> “Everything I already photograph could be the official record. Just give it somewhere to land.”

## Win him by
Making his existing habit the system — photos and notes become the commission archive, credited — and keeping his hands on the timber. He's the adoption route; the product should make him look prescient.
