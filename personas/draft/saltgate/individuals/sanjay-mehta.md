# Sanjay Mehta
**Importer — furniture wholesale · 48 · Leicester**
*The Cash-Flow Hostage — from Saltgate, ACRE Persona 22*

> “Three hundred containers a year means my working capital is permanently somewhere on the South China Sea. When a box sticks at the port, that's not logistics. That's my retailers' Christmas and my overdraft having a conversation.”

## His world
Sanjay sells certainty to retailers who plan floor space months out — and buys uncertainty by the container. Arrival estimates wobble with tides, duty surprises land in paragraph four of message twelve, and the gap between what he promised his customers and what the sea delivers is bridged with his margin and his apologies. He doesn't need the chain faster. He needs it *legible*.

## What he's trying to do
- Promise retailers dates he can keep — his brand is reliability in an unreliable trade
- Know landed cost per container before it lands, not after
- Stop financing the chain's surprises out of his overdraft

## What gets in his way
- Arrival truth scattered across portals, threads and Marta's phone
- Duty changes and demurrage discovered as invoices
- Warnings that were technically sent and practically buried

## How he feels about AI
Entirely indifferent to the mechanism. One screen: where are my containers, when do they truly arrive, what will each cost. If that exists, Saltgate keeps his business forever.

## In his own words
> “Tell me bad news early and in plain English. I can manage bad news. I cannot manage surprises.”

## Win him by
The importer's one-screen truth — positions, honest ETAs, landed costs, warnings that arrive as warnings — turning the forwarder from a supplier into infrastructure he'd never switch.
