# Trevor Mott
**MD, Saltgate Freight · 56 · Felixstowe**
*The Port Whisperer — from Saltgate, ACRE Persona 22*

> “A container went missing into the port's system for nine days last month. I made two phone calls. It was on a truck by Thursday. You can't write down whose numbers those were — except, well, maybe now you can. Maybe you'd better.”

## His world
Trevor's firm pilots documents through institutions while the cargo sails on its own clock. His personal asset is folklore: whom to ring when a box vanishes, which shipping line's Tuesday cut-off is really Wednesday, how the port behaves in a strike week. He jokes about AI in the pub and lies awake about succession — the two are the same problem and he's beginning to suspect it.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep demurrage bills boring and clients calm
- Get the folklore out of his head before his retirement makes it archaeology
- Hand the staff a firm that runs on systems, not séances

## What gets in his way
- Margins per shipment thin as manifest paper
- The third "digital freight revolution" pitch this year (the first two shipped portals)
- His own indispensability, lovingly maintained for three decades

## How he feels about AI
Breezy dismissal in public, private arithmetic in the evenings: the firm's sale price is a function of how much of Trevor is written down.

## In his own words
> “Bank what I know with my name on it, and prove it to me one stuck container at a time.”

## Win him by
Folklore capture framed as succession (and valuation), demonstrated through the daily wins: divergence alarms that beat his instincts to the punch, occasionally — and credit his instincts when they win.
