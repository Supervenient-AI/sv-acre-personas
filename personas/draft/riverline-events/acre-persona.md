### Riverline Events — a 6-person mass-participation events company putting 8,000 runners on closed public roads

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Civic-Pulse Producer
**Instance:** Riverline Events, York. Race director/founder, ops manager, events coordinator, partnerships manager, marketing, admin — six people producing nine events a year: a 10k series, two half marathons, a triathlon. Each event: 2,000–8,000 participants, hundreds of volunteers, public roads borrowed for a morning.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | The Hern & Salt pulse shape on civic infrastructure: every event assembles councils, police, medics, timing companies, charity partners, volunteer marshals and thousands of participants — onto roads that belong to everyone — for four hours, nine times a year. |
| Tooling | Race-entry platform (registrations, the data spine), course-mapping tools, the council's event-application portals, email, WhatsApp marshal groups, radios on the day, and **the risk assessment** — the document the whole constellation orbits (the universe doc's flag, confirmed). |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A pulse constellation on public infrastructure: statutory permissions, medical cover, traffic management, timing tech and a volunteer army assembled around thousands of self-selected civilian participants — with the risk assessment as the central boundary object through which every party's obligations are negotiated.

**Cast:**
- **Tessa Whitlam, 41** — founder & race director (York)
- **Rob Sinfield, 36** — ops manager; courses, closures, contractors (York)
- **Dr Saira Hussain, 44** — clinical lead at the event-medical provider (Leeds)
- **Neil Garrod, 49** — timing company owner; chips, mats, results (Harrogate)
- **Plus:** the events coordinator, ~250 volunteer marshals per event, the council's Safety Advisory Group, police, the traffic-management contractor, charity partners (who bring runners and want data), sponsors, 8,000 participants

**External immovable nodes:**
- **The SAG, council and police** — road closures exist at their pleasure; the event safety plan and risk assessment are the application's beating heart (the Hern & Salt licence edge, on public highways)
- **The date** — published, entered, trained-for by thousands; immovable
- **The weather** — heat is the medical model's biggest variable; ice cancels
- **The participants themselves** — 8,000 civilians of unknown fitness who signed a waiver and brought their own cardiac risk

**Topology:** The Hern & Salt breathing organisation, smaller and more statutory: a six-node core inflating per event with professional suppliers (medics, traffic, timing — role-grammared, reliable) and a volunteer marshal layer (mass, fragile, the 30% evaporation pattern confirmed). The course itself is the site plan: a linear venue twelve miles long with junctions for doors.

**Lifecycle (per event, ~9 months gestation, 4 hours of existence):**
- *Application & design:* course drawn, closures negotiated, the risk assessment begun — a living document that will pass through every party's hands.
- *The SAG gauntlet:* iterations with council, police, highways; every junction's plan interrogated; the medic ratio set by the model.
- *Entry build & the long middle:* registrations climb; charity partners recruit; marshals sign up (and silently un-sign in their hearts).
- *The final fortnight (inflation):* marshal briefings, signage plans, results setup, the weather watch begins in earnest.
- *Race morning (the four-hour crunch):* 4am course build, marshal check-ins (the no-show scramble), the start horn, the medical channel's quiet tension, the sweep vehicle, the roads reopened by 1pm as if nothing happened.
- *Dissolution by teatime; results by 6pm; the debrief maybe.*
- *Trust curve:* the SAG's trust is documentary and cumulative (the Hern & Salt pattern, confirmed); participants' trust is experiential and reviewed on social media by lunchtime; marshals return next year in proportion to how valued they felt at junction 14.

**Boundary objects:** **The risk assessment** (the constellation's constitution — every party negotiates through it), the course map and junction schedule, the medical plan (Saira's ratios), the traffic-management plan, the marshal roster and briefing card, the entry list with its medical-flag data, the results feed.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On Rob — junctions × marshals × closures × contractors, converging on one morning; on Tessa across the statutory bodies; on race morning, on the control vehicle's radio discipline.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Rob holds the course's folklore (which junction's marshal needs to be unflappable, where cars leak through). Saira holds the medical model and the morning's live clinical picture. Neil holds the timing tech's failure modes. The council's events officer holds the institutional memory of every past incident in the city. Marshals know their junction for one morning. The risk assessment holds what everyone agreed; the gaps between its revisions hold what they haven't.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Risk-assessment version chaos: the SAG reviewing v7 while police hold v5 — **eliminate**; on this document, version control is a safety control
2. Marshal evaporation (the 30% pattern, confirmed) discovered at 6am check-in — **eliminate** the surprise (confirmation pipelines, predictive over-recruitment); **contain** the volunteerism itself
3. Junction-instruction failure: a marshal at a critical point with a laminated card they half-read — **eliminate** the format failure (the zero-onboarding bar: one junction, one phone, ninety seconds)
4. Statutory-application drag: each council's portal and pace re-learned per event — **eliminate** the re-learning
5. Medical-data fragility: entry health flags not reaching the course medics — **eliminate**; this is the data path with a life on it
6. Saira's "the heat model says reduce the start waves" and the police's junction veto — **preserve**, absolutely: the safety hierarchy's friction is the entire point
7. Series amnesia: each event re-solving last year's junctions — **eliminate** (the pulse-memory pattern, third confirmation)

**Conflicting interests:**
- Participants want a fast, scenic course; police want minimal closure; residents want their Sunday; the council wants both the event's economy and no complaints
- Charity partners want runner data; GDPR and runner goodwill say carefully
- The entry platform owns the data the operator needs to own
- Sponsors want brand moments at exactly the junctions safety wants clear

*Contain-class: the civic bargain — disruption traded for civic good — is renegotiated through the risk assessment annually; the AI serves the negotiation's paperwork, never its judgement.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap a marshal: the design absorbs it ×250 — unless it's junction 14. Swap *Neil* race-week: results fail publicly; timing suppliers are a thin national market booked years out. Swap *Saira's company*: the medical plan re-derives; the SAG re-reviews; the date wobbles. Swap *Rob* mid-gestation: the course folklore and junction map leave (the Mack/Mitch pattern — third confirmation that pulse businesses store risk models in one operations head). Swap the council's events officer: the statutory relationship resets to the paperwork's quality — which is the argument for the paperwork being impeccable.

**Afterlife:** Per event: results stand forever (runners' PBs are sacred), the debrief feeds the next risk assessment (or doesn't), marshal goodwill compounds or evaporates by junction. Per series: the SAG relationship, the residents' tolerance and the city's institutional memory of "that race" accumulate across years. The safety case's documentary trail is also the legal defence file for the incident everyone hopes never comes — afterlife as liability armour, the Harewood golden-thread pattern on tarmac.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Hold the risk assessment as a versioned, single-truth document with every party's review state visible — the safety case as living infrastructure, not a Word attachment lineage. Run the marshal pipeline: recruitment, confirmation-decay detection, junction-matched briefings delivered to phones in ninety seconds, race-morning check-in with gap alerts while there's still time to re-deploy. Carry the medical flags from entry form to course medics intact. Bank the series memory: junction folklore, council preferences, incident learnings resurfacing at next year's matching moment. Pre-assemble each council's application from the last one. And on race morning: radios and humans run the show; the product's job is that nothing surprising remains to be discovered.
