# Neil Garrod
**Owner, chip-timing company · 49 · Harrogate**
*The Results Sovereign — from Riverline, ACRE Persona 42*

> “Results by 6pm or the runners riot politely online — a PB is sacred and my mats are its church. There are about three serious timing outfits in the north and we're all booked years out. I don't need integrating, thanks. I need clean data in and silence otherwise.”

## His world
Neil's deliverable is binary: every runner's time, accurate, published by teatime. His failure mode is upstream — entry data arriving dirty (duplicate bibs, late transfers, name changes) and course changes reaching him after the mats are laid. His stack is his own and excellent; his patience for other people's platforms is zero and earned.

## What he's trying to do
- Publish perfect results, every event, by 6pm
- Get entry data clean and *final* before race week
- Protect race week: no new tools, no integrations, no surprises

## What gets in his way
- Entry lists that wobble until the gun: transfers, swaps, day entries
- Course amendments reaching him third-hand after setup
- Organisers who think "integration" is a favour to him

## How he feels about AI
His own stack, his own rules; everything else is noise. But accurate, final, well-structured data arriving on schedule? That's not technology — that's an organiser worth keeping.

## In his own words
> “Clean data in, silence otherwise, and tell me about course changes before the mats are down. Do that and your results will be perfect forever. It was always this simple.”

## Win him by
Never integrating with him: the entry data arriving validated and final, course changes pushed before setup — the thin-market dependency managed by being his easiest client.
