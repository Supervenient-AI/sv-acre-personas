# Dr Saira Hussain
**Clinical Lead, event medical provider · 44 · Leeds**
*The Safety Spine — from Riverline, ACRE Persona 42*

> “Heat is my biggest variable: a warm morning re-prices eight thousand people's cardiac risk before the start horn. When my model says reduce the start waves, that call outranks everything — the sponsors, the schedule, the horn itself. Everyone agrees in the planning meeting. The system has to make sure they agree at 8:40am too.”

## Her world
Saira's planning converts statistics into staffing: entry numbers, course profile, weather bands → ambulances, medics, water points. Her recurring operational fury is the data path: medical flags declared by runners at entry (cardiac history, diabetes, allergies) reaching her course teams late, partial or not at all — the library's first genuinely life-critical data flow, treated by the industry as an export-to-spreadsheet afterthought.

## What she's trying to do
- Cover every event to her standard: the ratios right, the response times real
- Get the entry-form medical flags to her teams intact, every time, automatically
- Keep the clinical veto's authority absolute and its exercise rare

## What gets in her way
- Medical data arriving as a CSV emailed the Thursday before
- Heat-model decisions needing re-litigating at 8:40am with adrenaline in the room
- Event organisers (not Tessa) who treat the medical plan as a procurement line

## How she feels about AI
Clinically rigorous: decision support welcome, decision substitution never. The data-path reliability matters more to her than any model — a flag that doesn't arrive is a model that doesn't matter.

## In her own words
> “The runner told us about their heart condition at entry. That fact reaching my medic at kilometre nine, intact, is the whole system. Build that pipe to a clinical standard and I'll champion the rest.”

## Win her by
The life-critical data path, engineered like one: entry flags to course teams, verified, every event — and her veto's channel kept singular and sovereign.
