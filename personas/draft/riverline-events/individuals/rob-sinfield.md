# Rob Sinfield
**Ops Manager, Riverline Events · 36 · York**
*The Course in His Head — from Riverline, ACRE Persona 42*

> “I walk every course twice and dream in junction numbers. Junction 14 needs an unflappable marshal — live traffic leaks through if the closure crew's late. That fact isn't in any document. It's in me, from the year it nearly went wrong. There are forty facts like that per course, and nine courses.”

## His world
Rob converts a public road network into a race venue and back in nine hours, nine times a year. His marshal pipeline suffers the 30% evaporation the pulse family knows well — discovered, traditionally, at the 6am check-in with two hours to re-deploy. His junction folklore is the series' real risk model, held in the format the pulse family always holds it: one operations head.

## What he's trying to do
- Land every race morning with zero surprises that aren't weather
- See marshal confirmation decay *before* the 6am scramble
- Bank the junction folklore — credited — against the day he's promoted, poached or asleep

## What gets in his way
- Yes-in-March, silence-in-July volunteer physics
- Briefing cards half-read by marshals holding live-traffic junctions
- The course knowledge living in his head and his laminates

## How he feels about AI
Sceptical of anything that doesn't work in rain; converted instantly by anything that fills marshal gaps at 6:15am. His test is specific, recurring and unforgiving.

## In his own words
> “Predict the no-shows, brief each junction in ninety seconds on a phone, and flag the gaps while there's still time to re-deploy. Race morning should be execution, not improvisation.”

## Win him by
The marshal pipeline (decay detection, junction-matched phone briefings, live check-in gaps) and the folklore bank — the 6am scramble retired, his knowledge outliving his alarm clock.
