## Her world
Tessa's events borrow public infrastructure for four hours at a time, nine times a year — a privilege renewed annually through paperwork that must be impeccable and relationships that must never be surprised. Her February is application season across multiple councils' bespoke processes; her race mornings are the four-hour crunch where every plan meets 8,000 civilians of unknown fitness.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep nine licences' worth of statutory trust compounding
- Make the risk assessment one living, versioned truth across every reviewing party
- Grow the series without the application drag scaling linearly with it

## What gets in her way
- Each council's portal and pace, re-learned per event
- The RA's Word-attachment lineage breeding versions
- Series amnesia: last year's junction lessons re-solved annually

## How she feels about AI
Pragmatic and February-motivated: document assembly, version truth and application pre-filling are her quarter's misery, automated. The race-morning judgement stays human, on radios, where it belongs.

## In her own words
> “One true version of the safety case, every reviewer's status visible, and next year's application pre-built from this year's record. The SAG complimenting the paperwork — that's the win condition.”

## Win her by
The living risk assessment and the application engine — statutory trust fed by document quality, at a fraction of the February.
