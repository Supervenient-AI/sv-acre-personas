# ACRE Persona 30 — The Deadline-Tendril Hub
### Ledgerline Bookkeeping — a 9-person firm living inside forty small companies' Xeros, inboxes and payrolls

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #22 (Family 5, embedded workers); not yet interview-grounded.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Outsourced Finance Tendril
**Instance:** Ledgerline Bookkeeping, Wigan. Founder, five bookkeepers, a payroll specialist, a junior, an admin. ~40 clients (builders, salons, cafés, consultancies, a haulier) on monthly bookkeeping, VAT, payroll and credit-control packages.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Embedded ×40 at the operational layer: inside every client's Xero, bank feeds, receipt apps, payroll and (for some) actual inbox — deeper into each client's daily reality than the clients themselves, with none of the authority. |
| Tooling | Xero/QuickBooks ×40, Dext for receipts, BrightPay for payroll, HMRC's portals, WhatsApp with the clients who only do WhatsApp, a master deadline spreadsheet that is the firm's true beating heart. |
| Who carries coordination cost | Each bookkeeper across their client list; the founder across the whole deadline matrix; the *chasing of clients for their own paperwork* is half the labour and none of the glamour. |
| Buying behaviour | App-stack fluent (the cloud-accounting ecosystem normalised tooling) and margin-precise: fixed monthly fees mean every extra chase erodes the packet. MTD has made software adoption non-optional for their clients — Ledgerline is often the one dragging them. |
| What failure costs | A missed VAT or PAYE deadline is the client's penalty and the firm's fault — the deadline matrix is zero-tolerance ×40. Below that: the eternal grind of incomplete records ("what's this £400 to 'CASH'?") and clients who treat their bookkeeper as therapist, debt collector and miracle worker. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A hub with deep operational tendrils into many small companies — processing their financial reality on HMRC's calendar, dependent on each client's chaotic cooperation for the raw material, and sitting permanently between the client, the taxman and the client's accountant.

**Cast:**
- **Tracey Mullan, 46** — founder; keeper of the deadline matrix (Wigan)
- **Josie Pratt, 34** — senior bookkeeper; twelve clients, infinite patience (Leigh)
- **Bev Onslow, 53** — payroll specialist; forty payrolls, zero tolerance for late timesheets (Wigan)
- **Gary Thwaite, 44** — client: plumbing firm owner, eight staff, receipts in the van (Bolton)
- **Plus:** the other bookkeepers, the junior, clients' external accountants (year-end), HMRC, the receipt-app and bank-feed plumbing

**External immovable nodes:**
- **HMRC** — VAT quarters, PAYE RTI (every payday, by law), MTD mandates, CIS returns: the calendar that runs the firm; penalties automatic, appeals miserable
- **Companies House** — filing deadlines per client entity
- **Clients' external accountants** — year-end arrives with queries about decisions made eleven months ago
- **The banks and app ecosystem** — feed breakages and API changes that silently stop the data the whole model runs on

**Topology:** Hub with forty tendrils, each tendril deeper than any client realises: Ledgerline often knows a client is in trouble before the client does (the bank feed doesn't lie). The reverse asymmetry of Bellhaven — operational authority absent, operational knowledge total.

**Lifecycle:** Perpetual, run on the tax calendar:
- *The monthly cycle per client:* records in (the chase), processing, queries out (the second chase), reconciliation, reports nobody reads.
- *The VAT quarter:* the deadline matrix's drumbeat — forty staggered quarters, each needing the month's chaos resolved by a fixed date.
- *Payroll's relentless pulse:* weekly/monthly ×40, RTI filed on or before payday, by law — Bev's world has no slack in it anywhere.
- *Year-end (the annual reckoning):* every unanswered query from the year resurfaces via the accountant; January is folklore.
- *Client lifecycle:* onboarding (archaeology of the previous bookkeeper's habits), the settling (training the client to cooperate), and offboarding (handing the books over intact — the professional-courtesy test).
- *Trust curve:* clients' trust is near-total and oddly passive ("Tracey sorts all that") — spent only by a penalty or a payroll error, both unforgivable; the firm's trust in each *client* is the real variable, priced in chase-hours.

**Cadence:** Month-end crescendos, VAT-quarter peaks, payday pulses, and the daily drip of WhatsApp photos of receipts, questions, and "quick favours."

**Boundary objects:** The bank feed (truth, when it works), the receipt pile (Dext-ed or van-bound), the VAT return (the artefact with a penalty attached), the payslip (sacred — an error here reaches a human's rent), the query list (the firm's eternal sisyphean artefact), the year-end pack.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On each bookkeeper as client-chaser ("any chance of those statements, Gary?") and on Tracey across the matrix. The chase is the hidden majority of the work: the books are easy; the *getting of the books* is the job.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Each bookkeeper holds their clients' quirks (what "CASH" means for Gary, which café's Saturday takings are always missing). Bev holds the payroll edge-cases (who's on a court order, whose maternity overlaps). Tracey holds the matrix and the client-risk instincts. Gary holds his own business's reality in fragments he doesn't know matter. The accountant holds the year-end view and arrives annually like a returning comet.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. The records chase: clients not supplying their own paperwork until the deadline makes it everyone's crisis — **eliminate** (automated, warm, channel-native chasing — the Compass/Gemma pattern at ×40 scale)
2. The query graveyard: "what's this payment?" lists dying unanswered, resurrecting at year-end — **eliminate**
3. Deadline-matrix fragility: forty calendars in one spreadsheet and Tracey's vigilance — **eliminate**
4. Feed and app breakage silently stopping data — **eliminate** (detection, not prevention)
5. Scope creep: "while you're in there" credit-control, forecasting and therapy on a bookkeeping fee — **contain**: the client's need is real; the unpriced absorption is the problem
6. Bev's refusal to run payroll on an unconfirmed timesheet — **preserve**: her zero-tolerance is the system that means nobody's wages are ever wrong
7. The bad-news edge: Ledgerline seeing insolvency coming before the client — **contain**: a duty-of-candour tension (when to speak, to whom) that judgement must own, with the record's support

**Conflicting interests:**
- Clients want it all handled invisibly and cheaply; the handling depends on their own cooperation, which is the scarce input
- The accountant wants clean year-end files; the client wants minimal fees; Ledgerline absorbs the gap
- Bev's payroll rigidity vs. clients' "can you just run it, I'll confirm later"
- Tracey's fixed fees vs. the chase-hours each client actually costs

*Contain-class: the cooperation economy is the heart of it — the product's job is making each client's true cost (chase-hours, query-debt) visible enough to price honestly.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap the junior: nothing. Swap *Josie*: twelve clients' quirk-knowledge (the real meaning of their chaos) leaves; the chase-relationships reset and two clients wobble — quirks live in heads, the Bellhaven-Lily pattern at trade scale. Swap *Bev*: forty payrolls' edge-cases are a genuine operational emergency; payroll knowledge is the firm's least documented, highest-stakes asset. Swap *Tracey*: the matrix survives (it's a spreadsheet) but the client-risk radar and the firm's judgement layer don't. Swap a client's accountant: year-end queries double while the new one re-learns the file. Clients themselves churn rarely — inertia is the industry's moat and its complacency.

**Afterlife:** Clients leave by exit (business sold/closed) or upgrade (in-house finance); the handover file's quality is the firm's reputation in the accountant community — which is also its top referral source. The query graveyard's unanswered entries become the year-end's billable archaeology, then the next bookkeeper's inheritance. The matrix's deadline history (never missed, ever) is the firm's whole brand, invisible until broken.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Run the chase: per-client, channel-native (WhatsApp for Gary), warm, persistent, automatic — and a chase-hours ledger that makes each client's true cost visible. Kill the query graveyard: questions raised in-context, chased to resolution, dying only by answer. Hold the deadline matrix as infrastructure: forty calendars, staggered, escalating, vigilance-free. Watch the plumbing: feed breakages detected the day they happen. Draft the bad-news brief when the numbers turn, for Tracey's judgement to deliver. And respect the payslip: payroll automation proposes, Bev disposes.

## R — Roles

**Archetype load** (3+ = structural overload):

| Seat | Archetypes held | Load |
|---|---|---|
| Tracey | Conductor · Coordinator (the matrix) · Strategist (client risk) · Sponsor | **4 — founder-matrix pathology** |
| Josie | Production Realiser · Coordinator (×12 chases) | 2 |
| Bev | Specialist Maker · Gatekeeper (payroll integrity) | 2 — rigid where rigidity is the feature |
| Gary | Client Owner · (and unwitting) Production Realiser of his own records | 2 — the chase's target and the model's weakest input |

### Tracey Mullan, 46 — Founder *(The Matrix Keeper)*
**Wigan.** Twenty years from AAT evening classes to forty clients; the deadline spreadsheet is her life's actual work. Sleeps badly in VAT-quarter weeks. **Attitude to AI:** practical and price-aware; already lets Dext read receipts, sees the next step clearly. **Winning her looks like:** the matrix watches itself, the chases run themselves, and she finally prices clients on their true cost instead of her optimism.

### Josie Pratt, 34 — Senior Bookkeeper *(The Quirk Holder)*
**Leigh.** Twelve clients who all think they're her only one. Translates chaos into ledgers daily; her WhatsApp is 40% receipt photos. **Attitude to AI:** comfortable — anything that chases Gary so she doesn't have to. **Winning her looks like:** the quirks get encoded (credited to her), the queries stop piling, and her month-end stops depending on other people's glove boxes.

### Bev Onslow, 53 — Payroll Specialist *(The Zero-Tolerance Pulse)*
**Wigan.** Twenty-five years of payroll; has never paid anyone wrongly and intends to die that way. Knows every client's staff by name and court order. **Attitude to AI:** deeply suspicious near the payslip; open to anything that gets timesheets in on time. **Winning her looks like:** confirmations arrive before she asks, edge-cases are documented somewhere other than her head, and nothing — nothing — files RTI without her nod.

### Gary Thwaite, 44 — Client *(The Van-Based Filing System)*
**Bolton.** Runs eight plumbers and a healthy business he understands by feel; receipts live in the van, invoices in his head, and "CASH £400" means something different every time. Loves Ledgerline the way you love a good dentist. **Attitude to AI:** none whatsoever; answers WhatsApp photos at red lights (don't) and 9pm (fine). **Winning him looks like:** snapping a photo is the whole job, questions arrive one at a time in plain English, and the VAT bill never surprises him again.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ledgerline ↔ each client | Monthly fixed fee | Passive-total → **penalty-fragile**: "she sorts all that" until the day she visibly didn't | WhatsApp/email per client | Total operational knowledge, zero authority; the cooperation economy runs this edge |
| Josie ↔ Gary | Working | Warm, chase-shaped → stable | WhatsApp photos | His chaos is her raw material; her patience is his unpriced subsidy |
| Bev ↔ clients' staff | None (payroll duty) | Invisible-critical → one error from collapse | Payslips | The humans behind the numbers; the edge with rent and groceries on it |
| Ledgerline ↔ clients' accountants | Professional courtesy | Mutual, wary → **year-end-tested** | Email, annually intense | Referral source and quality judge in one |
| Ledgerline ↔ HMRC | Agent authorisation | Procedural → zero-tolerance | Portals + RTI | The calendar sovereign; penalties automatic |
| Firm ↔ feeds/apps | API plumbing | None → silently fragile | Integrations | Breakage detection is cheap insurance for the whole model |
| **Tracey ↔ AI** | — | Ready, ROI-eyed | In-product | Matrix + chase + client-cost ledger is the trifecta |
| **Josie ↔ AI** | — | Comfortable | In Xero + WhatsApp | Chase automation with her tone, not a robot's |
| **Bev ↔ AI** | — | Payslip-protective | Propose-only | Hard human gate on payroll, by design |
| **Gary ↔ AI** | — | Unaware, ideal | WhatsApp | One photo, one question at a time, plain English: the whole UX |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- The cooperation economy is the design centre: the client is the supply chain (the Compass-Gemma insight as a business model), so chase design — warm, channel-native, one-thing-at-a-time — is the core product, and the chase-hours ledger is the pricing revolution.
- The payslip edge sets a hard automation boundary the product should advertise, not apologise for: propose-everything, file-nothing-unconfirmed — Bev's rigidity productised.
- The deadline matrix ×40 is the same artefact as Quill & Marsh's CPR calendar and Saltgate's demurrage clocks: the cross-family "zero-tolerance clock infrastructure" pattern, here at its highest multiplicity.
