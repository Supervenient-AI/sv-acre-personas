# Gary Thwaite
**Owner, plumbing firm (client) · 44 · Bolton**
*The Van-Based Filing System — from Ledgerline, ACRE Persona 30*

> “The receipts are in the van. They're always in the van. The van is the system. Josie texts me a photo request, I send it from the merchant's car park, job done. Don't ask me what ‘CASH £400' was in March. March was a long time ago and so was the van it was in.”

## His world
Gary's business genuinely thrives — eight wages paid, customers happy, the VAT bill always somehow covered. The financial paperwork is the one part of his world that doesn't bend to graft, so he's outsourced it to Ledgerline and his cooperation runs on charm and lateness. He's not careless; he's *busy*, and a boiler in Horwich will always outrank a receipt photo until the receipt photo takes ten seconds.

## What he's trying to do
- Run the jobs, pay the lads, keep the customers — the actual business
- Never be surprised by a VAT bill again (it's happened twice; both times ruined a fortnight)
- Do the paperwork in moments that fit between boilers

## What gets in his way
- Filing systems that assume a desk; he has a dashboard
- Question lists that arrive nine at a time in accounting language
- His own optimistic relationship with "I'll sort it Sunday"

## How he feels about AI
No opinion, no interest, no objection. If "the Ledgerline thing" texts him one plain question at a time and takes photo answers, that's not technology, that's just Josie getting easier to help.

## In his own words
> “One question, plain English, photo answer, done. And tell me the VAT number a month early so I can have the sulk in advance.”

## Win him by
Ten-second cooperation: single questions, photo-native answers, the VAT bill forecast before it lands — chaos met where it lives, in the van, at red lights (parked, ideally).
