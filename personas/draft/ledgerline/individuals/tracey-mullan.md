**Founder, Ledgerline Bookkeeping · 46 · Wigan**
*The Matrix Keeper — from Ledgerline, ACRE Persona 30*

> “Forty clients, forty VAT quarters, forty payrolls, Companies House dates, CIS returns. The deadline matrix is a spreadsheet and my vigilance. We have never missed one. I would like, just once, to sleep through a VAT-quarter week.”

## Her world
Tracey's firm is embedded in forty small businesses' financial bloodstreams — deeper into each client's reality than the clients themselves, with none of the authority. Half the labour is the chase: getting clients to supply their own paperwork before their deadline becomes everyone's crisis. Her fixed fees price the bookkeeping; the chase-hours are priced on optimism, and the optimism is wrong by client and by season.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the never-missed record without it running on her cortisol
- Price clients on their true cost — the chase-hours ledger she's never had
- Build a firm that survives her holidays and, someday, sells

## What gets in her way
- The matrix living in one spreadsheet and one woman's vigilance
- Clients' glove-box filing systems meeting HMRC's calendar
- Scope creep: credit control, forecasting and therapy on a bookkeeping fee

## How she feels about AI
Practical and price-aware — Dext already reads the receipts; she can see the next step clearly and wants it costed. Anything touching the matrix must be more reliable than she is, which she knows is a high bar and says so.

## In her own words
> “Watch the matrix better than I do, run the chases warmer than we can, and show me what each client truly costs. Then I'll price like a business instead of a favour.”

## Win her by
Matrix-as-infrastructure with escalation she can trust, channel-native chase automation, and the chase-hours ledger that turns her quiet subsidies into pricing truth.
