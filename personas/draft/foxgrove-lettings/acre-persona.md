# ACRE Persona 39 — The Perpetual Three-Sided Constellation
### Foxgrove Lettings — a 13-person lettings and property management agency between landlords, tenants and the law, forever

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #31 (Family 8, matchmakers); ◆ adds the perpetual, no-end-date constellation — there is no "project", only an ongoing state.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Standing Intermediary
**Instance:** Foxgrove Lettings, Reading. Director, two lettings negotiators, four property managers, an accounts/client-money clerk, maintenance coordinator, admin. ~480 managed properties for ~210 landlords; roughly 700 live tenancies' worth of humans on the other side.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | ◆ Permanently triangular: landlord (the client), tenant (the counterparty who must also be served), and a contractor bench — coordinated continuously, with statutory deadlines (gas safety, deposits, EPC, licensing) threading through every triangle, forever. There is no completion date. The business is an ongoing state, not a pipeline of projects. |
| Tooling | A property management platform (decent at rent, poor at everything relational), a maintenance-reporting app tenants half-use, email and phone, WhatsApp with contractors, a compliance spreadsheet that is the firm's true nervous system, client-money accounting under regulatory rules. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The property managers — 120 properties each, every one a live triangle with a boiler. |
| Buying behaviour | Fee-compressed (online agents undercut), regulation-buffeted (every Renters' Rights change is an operational rewrite), buys what reduces compliance risk and call volume. |
| What failure costs | A missed gas-safety certificate is a criminal offence with the landlord's name on it and the agency's negligence behind it. A deposit mis-protected voids possession rights. A maintenance issue mishandled is a family in a cold home and a tribunal later. The downside is legal, human and reputational at once. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** ◆ A matchmaker constellation that never dissolves: the agency stands permanently between parties with structurally opposed interests (rent vs. yield, repairs vs. costs) and a rotating contractor cast, while statutory clocks run on every property — the purest "ongoing state" in the library, where lifecycle means *seasons*, not stages.

**Cast:**
- **Michelle Grover, 48** — director; ex-negotiator, compliance-haunted (Reading)
- **Jordan Eaves, 27** — property manager; 120 triangles (Reading)
- **Geoff Tanner, 63** — landlord: seven flats, his pension, opinions about "tenants today"
- **Dami Adewale, 29** — tenant: NHS physio, good payer, mould in the bathroom, three unanswered reports
- **Tony Brace, 52** — gas engineer; the contractor bench's load-bearing member
- **Plus:** negotiators, the client-money clerk, ~209 other landlords, ~700 tenants, the contractor bench, deposit schemes, the council's licensing team

**External immovable nodes:**
- **The statutory calendar** — gas safety annually per property, EICRs five-yearly, EPC thresholds, deposit protection windows, licensing renewals: hundreds of interleaved legal clocks, zero tolerance
- **The deposit schemes and tribunals** — adjudicators who read the file cold, years later
- **The courts** — possession timelines measured in quarters; the last resort that reprices every negotiation
- **The property itself** — boilers fail on Christmas Eve; the physical estate is an actor with no phone number (the Sorrel & Gray physical-world pattern, perpetual edition)
- **Legislation in motion** — the Renters' Rights regime rewriting the rules mid-game, nationally

**Topology:** ◆ A standing lattice of triangles: agency at the centre of 480 landlord–tenant–property triads, sharing one contractor bench and one compliance calendar. Each triangle is a miniature matchmaker (two parties, opposed interests, scoped truths) — but unlike Bowline's deals, these never close. The relationship *is* the steady state; events (a leak, an arrears, a renewal) are perturbations of it.

**Lifecycle:** No project arc — a perpetual state with pulses:
- *Per tenancy:* marketing → referencing → move-in (inventory, deposit, certificates — the legally dense 72 hours) → the long middle (rent, repairs, inspections) → renewal or notice → check-out and deposit negotiation (the adjudication file's moment of truth) → re-let. Then again. Forever.
- *Per property:* the compliance year — certificates, inspections, licensing — cycling regardless of who lives there.
- *Per landlord:* acquisition → honeymoon → the first big repair bill (the midpoint reckoning: yield dreams meet a £3,800 roof) → portfolio maturity or the sell-up.
- *Seasonal pulses:* September student churn, Christmas boiler season, spring legislation commencements.
- *Trust curve:* the landlord's trust is yield-and-silence based (a quiet property is a good agency); the tenant's trust — rarely considered, commercially decisive — is responsiveness-based and starts low (the industry's reputation precedes it). Both erode through the same event handled badly; the triangle means every failure has two audiences.

**Cadence:** Inbound, relentless, interrupt-driven: maintenance reports, arrears flags, certificate expiries, renewal dates — 480 properties generating events on no schedule, against a compliance calendar that forgives nothing.

**Boundary objects:** The tenancy agreement, the inventory (the deposit dispute's decisive evidence, photographed badly), the gas/EICR certificates (criminal-liability paper), the maintenance ticket (one event, three audiences, three truths), the rent statement, the section notices (the relationship's legal endgame), the compliance spreadsheet.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On the property managers, perpetually: every maintenance event is a four-party coordination (tenant access, contractor availability, landlord authorisation, agency margin) with a household's comfort hostage to it. On Michelle: the compliance calendar's integrity and every escalation's judgement.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Jordan holds 120 properties' quirks, 120 landlords' authorisation thresholds and 120-plus tenants' situations — the quirk-knowledge pattern (Bellhaven's Lily, Ledgerline's Josie) at maximum census. Tony knows the boilers personally. Geoff knows his mortgage pressure (shared with no one). Dami knows the mould's history better than the file does. The compliance spreadsheet knows the clocks; the platform knows the rent; *nobody* reliably knows the maintenance ticket's full three-sided story.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Compliance-clock fragility: hundreds of statutory dates held in a spreadsheet and vigilance — **eliminate**; the criminal-liability class (the Ledgerline matrix with prison at the end)
2. Maintenance-triangle latency: tenant reports → agency relays → landlord deliberates → contractor schedules → tenant unavailable → repeat — weeks per cycle, a household waiting — **eliminate** the relay friction; **contain** the landlord's authorisation right (it's his money)
3. The three-truths problem: the same event narrated differently to tenant ("chasing the landlord"), landlord ("managing the tenant"), and file (neither) — **contain** the legitimate scoping; **eliminate** the file's blindness — the record must hold the whole truth even where audiences see slices
4. Arrears drift: missed payments unaddressed until they're a possession case — **eliminate** the drift; **contain** the human judgement (Dami's universal credit delay is not Geoff's villainy)
5. Inventory/evidence weakness losing deposit adjudications — **eliminate**
6. Jordan's burnout at 120 triangles — **eliminate** the load, or the churn (endemic in the role) continues
7. Michelle's "we don't serve notice on a family for a first late payment" — **preserve**: the agency's ethical line is its licence-to-operate with both sides and its staff
8. Legislation shocks — **contain**: unchangeable; the product's job is portfolio-wide impact analysis in days (the Cobalt Ridge blast-radius pattern)

**Conflicting interests:**
- Geoff wants yield and minimal spend; Dami wants a warm, working home; both are right and the triangle holds them
- The agency serves the landlord (the fee) and the tenant (the law, and decency) — the structural dual duty
- Contractors want volume and quick sign-off; landlords want three quotes; tenants want today
- The agency's margin wants fewer touches; the relationship wants more

*Contain-class at the core: landlord–tenant opposition is the constellation's permanent climate. The framework's matchmaker note applies fully: one truth per legitimate audience — and the tribunal eventually audits whether the agency's slices were honest.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap a contractor: the bench absorbs it (Tony excepted — his boiler-history knowledge is quietly load-bearing). Swap *Jordan*: 120 triangles' quirk-knowledge and relationship temperature vanish; complaints spike within a month — the industry's chronic churn makes this a *recurring* catastrophe, not a risk. Swap *Geoff* (sells the portfolio): seven tenancies change hands mid-term; the new landlord inherits files whose completeness is suddenly priced. Swap *Dami* (tenancy churn): routine — and each churn re-runs the legally dense 72 hours. Swap *Michelle*: the compliance conscience and the ethical line wobble together. ◆ The perpetual constellation's defining trait: substitution never stops — the same triangles persist while every human in them rotates.

**Afterlife:** ◆ There isn't one — there is only continuation: tenancies end but properties persist; landlords sell but portfolios transfer; the *file* is the only continuous member of every triangle. Which makes the record's quality compounding and existential: today's lazy inventory is next year's lost adjudication; this decade's maintenance history is the next sale's due diligence. The agency's reputation runs on two one-way streets — landlord referrals and tenant reviews — that never meet but both price the brand.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** The compliance calendar as infrastructure — every statutory clock on every property, self-watching, escalating, evidencing (the zero-tolerance-clock pattern, at its largest scale and highest stakes). The maintenance triangle run as a workflow: report triaged (with photos), landlord authorisation requested with costed options, contractor scheduled against tenant availability, every party seeing their legitimate slice while the file holds the whole. Arrears handled early and humanely — payment-plan drafts before possession drafts. Inventory and evidence made adjudication-grade by default. Portfolio-wide legislation impact analysis on commencement day. And the dual duty held visibly: a product that helps the agency be *fair* at scale is the only one both sides of 480 triangles can live with.

### Michelle Grover, 48 — Director *(The Compliance Conscience)*
**Reading.** Twenty-five years from Saturday viewings to ownership; carries the statutory calendar in her sleep and the ethical line in her spine. **Attitude to AI:** pragmatic, liability-first — anything that makes compliance provable gets a hearing; anything that automates a human judgement about a family's home does not. **Winning her looks like:** the clocks watch themselves with evidence attached, the triangles run without her firefighting, and the firm's fairness is visible enough to be a selling point on both sides.

### Jordan Eaves, 27 — Property Manager *(The Hundred-and-Twenty Triangles)*
**Reading.** Two years in; good at it, drowning in it, already eyeing the exit the role is famous for. Every day is forty interrupts about other people's homes. **Attitude to AI:** yesterday, please — triage, relay and chasing is 70% of his day. **Winning him looks like:** the maintenance workflow runs itself between judgement points, the quirk-knowledge gets captured (credited), and the job becomes the relationship work it was supposed to be.

### Geoff Tanner, 63 — Landlord *(The Pension in Bricks)*
**Wokingham.** Seven flats accumulated over thirty years; leveraged, anxious about legislation, secretly fond of his longest-standing tenants. Authorises repairs slowly because every email reads like money. **Attitude to AI:** none; attitude to costed options with photos and a recommendation: surprisingly fast. **Winning him looks like:** decisions arrive sized and priced, compliance never touches his name, and the yield statement explains itself.

### Dami Adewale, 29 — Tenant *(The Counterparty Who Must Be Served)*
**Reading.** NHS physiotherapist; pays on time, reports problems properly, and has learned that reporting changes nothing. The mould is in its third month. **Attitude to AI:** whatever works — she wants acknowledgement, a date, and a fixed bathroom. **Winning her looks like:** reports get answered in hours with honest status ("authorised, contractor booked Thursday 8–10, here's who's coming"), and being a good tenant finally feels like it counts for something.

### Tony Brace, 52 — Gas Engineer *(The Bench's Backbone)*
**Tilehurst.** Knows four hundred local boilers by their noises; schedules by phone, invoices late, shows up. **Attitude to AI:** none; WhatsApp and a diary. **Winning him looks like:** jobs arrive with photos, access pre-arranged and parking noted — and he stops doing the agency's coordination for free from a van.

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- ◆ The no-end-date constellation retires the project metaphor entirely: the product must model *standing state with statutory clocks and rotating humans* — closer to operations software than collaboration software, with the file as the only permanent member.
- The maintenance triangle is the universal wedge: highest volume, clearest pain on all three sides, and every cycle exercises the scoped-truth architecture (tenant slice, landlord slice, whole-truth file) the matchmaker family demands.
- The dual-duty visibility is the strategic differentiator: regulation is moving the industry toward tenant-side accountability — the agency that can *prove* fairness at scale wins both audiences, and the product that enables it defines the category.
