# Dami Adewale
**Tenant · 29 · Reading**
*The Counterparty Who Must Be Served — from Foxgrove, ACRE Persona 39*

> “I pay on time. I report things properly, with photos, through the app. The mould is in its third month. At work I'd escalate; here there's nothing to escalate to — just the app saying ‘received' and the silence saying everything else.”

## Her world
Dami treats her tenancy like the professional she is: documented, punctual, fair. What she gets back is the industry's deficit edge — an agency paid by the other side, a landlord she's never met deliberating somewhere about her bathroom, and a process whose silences she's too sensible to fill with theories and too human not to. The Renters' Rights regime is slowly arming her; she'd rather not need arming.

## What she's trying to do
- Get the mould fixed — it's her lungs, her deposit and her photographs
- Be treated as the counterparty she legally is, not an inconvenience to the fee-payer
- Stay: she likes the flat, the street, the commute — moving is the failure mode

## What gets in her way
- Reports that vanish into authorisation limbo without status
- A triangle whose other two corners never explain themselves
- Being a good tenant in a system that doesn't price that

## How she feels about AI
Whatever works: acknowledgement, honest status, a date, a fixed ceiling. If the mechanism is a robot, fine — the robot answers, which is more than the current arrangement does.

## In her own words
> “‘Authorised. Contractor booked Thursday 8–10. Here's who's coming.' Three sentences. That's the entire revolution I'm asking for.”

## Win her by
Honest status at her legitimate level — acknowledged, authorised, booked, done — making good tenancy finally feel like it counts. The tenant surface is the industry's reputational repair kit, and she's exactly who it repairs it with.
