# Jordan Eaves
**Property Manager, Foxgrove Lettings · 27 · Reading**
*The Hundred-and-Twenty Triangles — from Foxgrove, ACRE Persona 39*

> “A hundred and twenty properties. Every day: forty interrupts about other people's homes — a leak, an authorisation, a tenant who can't get an answer, a landlord who won't give one. I'm the relay in all of it. The industry knows this job burns people out in three years. I'm at two.”

## His world
Jordan's day is the maintenance triangle at industrial volume: tenant reports → his triage → landlord authorisation (slow) → contractor scheduling (negotiated) → tenant access (missed) → repeat. Every cycle's latency is a household waiting; every shortcut is a liability; every resolved job earns him zero visible credit and one less interrupt. The exit door the role is famous for is in his peripheral vision.

## What he's trying to do
- Get households' problems fixed fast — he genuinely cares, which is why it hurts
- Cut the relay work so the relationship work survives
- Stay — if the job becomes the one he signed up for

## What gets in his way
- Four-party coordination performed by phone, per incident, forty times a day
- Landlord authorisation latency he absorbs as tenant anger
- His quirk-knowledge being the system, uncredited and unbacked-up

## How he feels about AI
Desperate yes, today: triage, relay and chasing is 70% of his day and none of it needs his humanity. The workflow that runs itself between judgement points is the job description he thought he'd accepted.

## In his own words
> “Triage the report, get the landlord a costed yes/no, book the contractor against the tenant's actual availability — and let me do the human bits. There are human bits. I'd love to meet them again.”

## Win him by
The maintenance triangle as workflow: automated between judgement points, his quirk-knowledge captured and credited — the burnout role re-engineered into the relationship role it pretended to be.
