# Michelle Grover
**Director, Foxgrove Lettings · 48 · Reading**
*The Compliance Conscience — from Foxgrove, ACRE Persona 39*

> “Four hundred and eighty properties. Every one has a gas certificate that expires annually, an EICR on a five-year clock, deposit rules, licensing rules — and missing one isn't a fine, it's a criminal offence with a landlord's name on it and my firm's negligence behind it. The spreadsheet that tracks it all is magnificent. It is also a spreadsheet.”

## Her world
Michelle runs the perpetual constellation: 480 landlord–tenant–property triangles, none of which ever completes, all of which pulse with statutory clocks and human weather. Her dual duty — paid by landlords, bound by law and decency to tenants — is the job's spine and its strain. Every Renters' Rights amendment rewrites her operations overnight; every winter, the boilers audition for crisis.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the compliance record perfect: clocks watched, evidence filed, criminal liability nowhere
- Hold the dual duty visibly — fairness at scale as the firm's brand on both sides
- Stop Jordan's role burning through Jordans

## What gets in her way
- Hundreds of statutory dates on one spreadsheet and one woman's vigilance
- The maintenance triangle's four-party latency, with families waiting inside it
- Legislation rewriting the rulebook mid-game, portfolio-wide

## How she feels about AI
Liability-first: provable clocks and evidence trails get a fair hearing; anything automating a human judgement about someone's home does not. Her test is the tribunal's cold read.

## In her own words
> “Watch the clocks better than I do, evidence everything, and help us be *fair* at scale — provably. That product I'd buy twice.”

## Win her by
The compliance calendar as infrastructure (statutory clocks, evidence attached, escalation she trusts) and the maintenance workflow that serves all three sides of every triangle — her ethics, operationalised.
