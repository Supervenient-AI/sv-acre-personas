# Tony Brace
**Gas Engineer · 52 · Tilehurst**
*The Bench's Backbone — from Foxgrove, ACRE Persona 39*

> “Four hundred boilers in this town know me personally. I can tell the one in Crescent Road's about to go by the noise it made last winter. What I can't tell is whether the tenant's home Thursday, whether there's parking, or which key safe code the agency's changed — so I do their coordination for them, for free, from a van.”

## His world
Tony's job is gas; his unpaid second job is logistics — chasing access, discovering parking, ringing tenants who didn't know he was coming because the relay dropped a link. He shows up, which in his trade is a superpower, and the agencies that send him complete jobs (access arranged, codes current, fault described, photos attached) get his December availability. The others get his answerphone.

## What he's trying to do
- Get through the certificate calendar and the boiler season with his diary intact
- Stop doing agency coordination from a van at his own expense
- Keep the reputation: he shows up, it works, the paperwork's right

## What gets in his way
- Jobs arriving as addresses without access, context or parking
- Tenants unaware of bookings made on their behalf
- Invoices he submits late meeting agencies that pay later

## How he feels about AI
None, and none needed: WhatsApp and a diary. But jobs arriving *complete* — access confirmed with the tenant, fault photographed, key code current, parking noted? He'd call that the agency finally doing its half.

## In his own words
> “Send the job ready and I'll have it done by Thursday. Send me an address and a prayer and we're both having a bad week.”

## Win him by
Complete jobs over WhatsApp: tenant-confirmed access, photos, codes, parking — the bench's goodwill earned by removing the free labour, just in time for boiler season.
