# Geoff Tanner
**Landlord — 7 flats · 63 · Wokingham**
*The Pension in Bricks — from Foxgrove, ACRE Persona 39*

> “Seven flats, thirty years of accumulating them, and every email from the agency reads like money leaving. I'm not mean. I'm leveraged — the mortgages don't care about my sentimentality, and the legislation changes faster than I can read it. So yes, I sit on repair authorisations. I'm not proud of it. I'm doing maths.”

## His world
Geoff's flats fund his retirement at the mercy of interest rates, legislation and boilers. Each repair authorisation is a small fiscal event he deliberates like a board decision — while, invisibly to him, a tenant waits and an agency absorbs the friction. Give him a costed recommendation with photos and options, and he decides in an hour; give him "the boiler needs looking at," and he stalls for a week, asking around.

## What he's trying to do
- Protect the yield: the maths has to work or the pension doesn't
- Stay legal through a regime he half-understands and wholly fears
- Be, quietly, a decent landlord — within the maths

## What gets in his way
- Decisions arriving un-costed and un-explained
- Compliance obligations he learns about from newsletters and nightmares
- His own deliberation, costing him tenant goodwill he doesn't see depleting

## How he feels about AI
None whatsoever — but costed options with photos, a recommendation and a deadline? He answers those same-day. The format is the feature; the mechanism is irrelevant.

## In his own words
> “Tell me: here's the problem, here's the photo, here's two quotes and what happens if I wait. I'll decide before lunch. I'm slow because you make me slow.”

## Win him by
The decision-ready authorisation: costed, photographed, recommended, deadlined — his deliberation compressed from a week to an hour, the triangle's slowest edge quietly fixed.
