## His world
Robbie is the route in — the person who turns “the partners are nervous” into “the partners are curious.” He's spent internal capital on every tool he's championed, and his credibility is the practice's actual adoption mechanism. He knows he's a single point of failure for the practice's AI capability, and the practice doesn't, which worries him on its behalf.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep the practice ahead of its peers without ever triggering Sandra's veto
- Convert his internal credibility into an industry profile (and maybe, one day, a role that names what he already does)
- Find tools good enough that championing them is safe

## What gets in his way
- One overpromising vendor could cost him a year of internal trust
- The approval process: every pilot is a campaign
- Being indispensable in a role that doesn't exist on the org chart

## How he feels about AI
Evangelist with a mortgage on his credibility. Will trial anything; will recommend only what survives his own projects.

## In his own words
> “Make me look right. That's the whole partnership. I take you to the partners; you don't make me regret it.”

## Win him by
Being the tool that makes his next conference talk write itself — and never, ever shipping the overpromise he'd have to walk back internally.
