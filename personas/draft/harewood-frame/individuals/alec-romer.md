# Alec Romer
**Associate Director, Harewood Frame Architects · 44 · London**
*The Power User — from Harewood Frame, ACRE Persona 4*

> “I built a minute-taking agent and a tender-writing agent trained on three years of submissions. I'd build the next five products myself if I could code. I can't. It's infuriating.”

## His world
Alec is the practice's top consumer of AI credits and its most prolific internal toolmaker — agents for minutes, agents for tenders, ideas for five more (a brochure-assembly engine, a building-regs expert). The micro detail always wins his attention; he knows strategy is his weak spot and says so. Meanwhile the golden thread — what was decided, by whom, when — gets maintained by hand, by him, at night, because the regulator holds the practice liable for it.

## What he's trying to do
- Get the golden thread to assemble itself from the meetings he's already in
- Stop returners and new joiners costing him briefing days
- Build (or buy) the tools he can see so clearly and can't code

## What gets in his way
- Liability that concentrates on his chair while information scatters across firms
- The practice's perimeter: his best tools can't be shared with the engineers and contractor who need them
- Sandra's security review — necessary, unhurried, unimpressed

## How he feels about AI
Maximalist within the perimeter. He's past adoption and into invention; what he wants from a vendor is extensibility and respect.

## In his own words
> “Don't sell me a chatbot. Sell me the project's memory, inside our walls, that I can build on.”

## Win him by
Letting him extend it. Box him in and he'll resent it; give him rails to build on and he becomes the practice's product team for free.
