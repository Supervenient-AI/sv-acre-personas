# Diane Okafor
**Development Lead (client-side) · 39**
*The Date-Keeper — from Harewood Frame, ACRE Persona 4*

> “I read the minutes when something's gone wrong. Which tells you how often I read the minutes — and how I find out things have gone wrong.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 39 |
| Lives | Greenwich — partner, one small child, a calendar with no white space |
| Background | Surveying, then client-side development; this scheme is her biggest yet |
| Role | The client's development lead: owns the budget, the board updates and the dates — especially the dates |
| Pressure | A board that reads delay as incompetence and contingency as failure |
| Channels | Teams, monthly reports she skims, the phone call that actually informs her |
| Devices | Laptop, phone, a board pack due every fourth Thursday |

## Her world
Diane carries her board's expectations into a process built for accountability rather than speed, and translates between the two badly — not for lack of skill, but because nobody gives her a truthful programme view she can forward upward. So she asks for dates, gets caveats, and reads the gap as evasion.

## What she's trying to do
- Deliver the scheme on the dates she promised people more senior than her
- Know about problems before her board does
- Spend her contingency on the building, not on surprises

## What gets in her way
- A reporting cadence that surfaces issues a month after they were decidable
- Professional caution she experiences as fog
- Her own distance from the technical truth

## How she feels about AI
Consumer-grade enthusiasm, corporate-grade impatience. She doesn't care about the mechanism; she cares about Thursday's pack.

## In her own words
> “Give me one page that's actually true — dates, risks, what you need from me — and I'll stop ringing people at six.”

## Win her by
A truthful, forwardable programme view: honest dates with honest reasons, surfaced early enough that she's the one informing her board, never the last to know.
