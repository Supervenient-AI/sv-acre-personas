## His world
Craig is politely adversarial by role, not by nature. His employer's margin lives in the gaps between drawing issues, and his job is to find them, price them and win them — while keeping the project moving and the relationships workable, because he'll meet these same consultants on the next bid. He blames the engineers when artwork fails on site; they blame him back; everyone's half right.

## What he's trying to do
- Hit programme, protect margin, keep claims clean and defensible
- Get RFIs answered in days, not meeting cycles
- Walk away with a building, a profit and a reputation intact — in that order on bad days

## What gets in his way
- Design information arriving late and ambiguous
- A golden-thread record that reads, to him, like the architect's defence file
- Twenty-person meetings that produce three decisions and no instructions

## How he feels about AI
Indifferent — until the record it keeps starts settling variation disputes. At that point he'll adopt it or fight it, depending entirely on whose record it turns out to be.

## In his own words
> “I'll trust a shared record the day it costs the architect a claim too. Neutral means neutral.”

## Win him by
Being the neutral ledger both sides cite: instructions he can rely on, RFIs answered fast, and a record that's nobody's weapon — including his.
