## Her world
Sandra has watched two decades of vendors promise the moon and ship a data-processing agreement with gaps in it. Her job is simple to state and endless to do: nothing enters the practice that could leak a client's building, a consultant's design, or a safety record that courts may one day read. The AI wave hasn't changed her method; it's just made the queue longer.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the perimeter intact while the practice modernises around it
- Turn every approval into a precedent so the next review is faster
- Be the reason nothing ever appears in an incident report

## What gets in her way
- Enthusiasm arriving faster than documentation
- Vendors who answer her data-residency question with a marketing deck
- Being cast as the blocker when she's actually the enabler with standards

## How she feels about AI
Procedurally. It's a category of tool with a category of risk; the conversation is about data boundaries, retention and audit, or it doesn't happen.

## In her own words
> “Where does the data live, who can see it, when is it deleted, and can you prove all three? Answer those and we're friends.”

## Win her by
Answers she can file: residency, retention, scoping she can draw on a whiteboard for the partners. Treat her review as the product's proving ground, not its obstacle.
