# ACRE Persona 2 — The Agency-Led Account
### Loop Theory — a 3-person growth marketing agency running a client account with rotating freelancers

## A — Account
**Archetype:** The Post-Contraction Boutique Agency. Loop Theory, growth/performance marketing, 3 full-time staff plus roughly the same again in freelancers (two regulars, a rotating cast behind them). Recently shrank from a bigger team; the founders now do client work *and* run the company. Google Workspace + Slack; paid Claude subscription shared informally; dashboarding tools are the biggest software outlay. Buying behaviour: will trial anything that promises to relieve the founders, but has been burned — they adopted a reporting-automation tool last year that got better, then worse, then quietly died, and the team had stopped using it before the vendor folded. They will test a new product hard and tell you exactly why it broke.

## C — Constellation
**Definition:** An agency hub running a continuous client account — not a one-off project — with freelancers attached per-workstream and a client contact who can summon attention at any moment.

**Cast:** Agency founder (strategy + everything), second founder (delivery + content), account exec, two regular freelancers (paid media specialist, designer), client-side marketing manager, client-side finance contact (invoices only).

**Topology:** Agency-led hub, but with a live wire to the client: the client's Slack/email reaches straight into the agency's day and detonates priorities. Freelancers hang off individual workstreams and rarely see the whole account.

**Cadence:** Continuous, punctuated by the client's "throwaway" questions — quick to ask, expensive to answer, impossible to triage by urgency from the message alone.

**Boundary objects:** The monthly performance report (numbers + written narrative), the media plan, the dashboard, the timesheet (a work of collective fiction completed three weeks late), the brief per workstream.

**Failure modes:**
1. Client comms triage — every message treated as urgent because nobody knows which ones are
2. Each person disappearing into their own private AI chats; insight never returns to the team
3. Freelancer onboarding repeated from scratch per workstream
4. Resource whiplash: over-profitable with a burnt-out team, or comfortable and unprofitable — never level
5. Timesheets reconstructed from memory, "invariably wrong"

**Conflicting interests:** The client wants instant responsiveness; the founders want protected thinking time; the freelancers want clean briefs and to bill for the onboarding nobody scopes; the account exec absorbs the gap between all three.

**What the constellation needs from the AI:** Sit *in Slack* as a contributor, not in a separate app. Triage inbound client requests (urgent / needs thinking / can hold). Hold account knowledge so freelancers onboard themselves. Estimate timesheets from observable activity and ask for a 5-second confirm — "a quicker wrong is better than a slower wrong." Surface the resourcing picture before it becomes a crisis.

## R — Roles
**Marcus Bell, 41 — Founder (The Player-Manager).** Bristol; school-run hours, then a second shift at 9pm. Treats AI as "a fairly experienced mid-tier colleague — brilliant but often stupid": he'll delegate to it but interrogates every number. Wants the first 20% and last 20% of any deliverable to stay human, partly conviction, partly self-respect. Constantly slightly wrong about capacity. Has "try Claude's new features" on his list every week and never gets to it.
**Leila Fontaine, 38 — Co-founder (The Delivery Engine).** London. Has quietly vibe-coded a booking tool for a side project; the pragmatic experimenter of the pair. Would let the AI draft, never let it send.
**Jess Adeyemi, 26 — Account Exec (The Shock Absorber).** Remote, Sheffield. First responder to every client ping; decides forty times a day what's urgent with no information. The role that benefits most from triage and gets asked least.
**Tom Rourke, 33 — Freelance Paid Media (The Repeat Spoke).** Leeds, three other clients. Sets up campaigns in Meta/Google — a time-slog of pure configuration. Bills by the day; unscoped onboarding and "quick calls" eat his margin. Wary of anything that watches how long things take him.
**Client-side: Hannah Pryce, 35 — Marketing Manager (The Summoner).** Asks questions in Slack at 5.55pm that are either nothing or everything. Doesn't know which herself. Once rewrote the agency's long-form article in ChatGPT and sent it back "improved"; it wasn't, and saying so cost a week of diplomacy.

## E — Edges
The defining edges: **Hannah ↔ agency** (high power asymmetry; the channel is hers, the cadence is hers, and the AI must moderate the flow without ever appearing to gatekeep the client); **Marcus ↔ Tom** (warm, repeat, but every engagement re-negotiates scope from scratch — the edge needs a persistent memory neither party maintains); **Marcus ↔ Leila** (founder edge: shared everything, including the unexamined assumption that the other one is tracking capacity); **team ↔ AI** (the team will trust it as a colleague precisely to the degree it admits what it doesn't know — they've watched one tool die of overpromising).
