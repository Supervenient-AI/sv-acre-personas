*The Repeat Spoke — from Loop Theory, ACRE Persona 2*

## His world
Tom's craft is configuration: campaign structures, audiences, bid strategies — a time-slog of pure precision inside platforms nobody else on the account ever opens. Which makes him the only person who really knows what the media plan *is*. Every new workstream with Loop Theory starts warm and familiar — and then re-negotiates scope from scratch, because the edge between him and Marcus has no memory either of them maintains.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep four clients' campaigns sharp without any of them eating the others' days
- Get onboarding either scoped and billable, or eliminated
- Stay the *first-call* freelancer for the agencies he likes working with

## What gets in his way
- Context that arrives by archaeology: every workstream rebuilds the account picture from scratch
- “Quick calls” that are neither
- Anything that watches how long things take him — he sells outcomes priced in days, not minutes under a stopwatch

## How he feels about AI
Practical: uses it for campaign copy variants and analysis already. Structurally wary of observation — a tool that times him is a tool that renegotiates his rate.

## In his own words
> “Open the workstream, and the account context is just *there*. That's worth a day rate to me — mine.”

## Win him by
Day-one context with zero archaeology, and a hard, visible line between helping him and measuring him.
