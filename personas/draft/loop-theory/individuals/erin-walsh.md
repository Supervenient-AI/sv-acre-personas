# Erin Walsh
**Freelance Designer · 31 · Cardiff**
*The Per-Workstream Visitor — from Loop Theory, ACRE Persona 2*

> “Two days a month on this account, and I spend the first morning re-learning it. Every month. From scratch.”

## Her world
Erin sees Loop Theory two days a month, which means she experiences the account as a series of cold opens: who's the client again, what did we say last time, where's the latest logo lockup? Her work is consistently excellent and her context is consistently zero — a combination the agency keeps paying for without noticing.

## What she's trying to do
- Deliver work that looks like it came from someone embedded, in the hours of someone who isn't
- Keep four agencies' brand worlds straight in her head without cross-contamination
- Spend her booked hours designing, not excavating Drive folders

## What gets in her way
- Briefs that assume context she was never given
- The twenty-minute “quick context call” that opens every visit, unbillable in both directions
- Feedback that arrives as paragraphs when a Figma pin would do

## How she feels about AI
Uses it for ideation and moodboards; distrusts it near final artwork — the craft layer is hers. Neutral on client-side tools, provided they hand her context instead of homework.

## In her own words
> “Attach the brand files, the last three deliverables and the feedback history to the brief. That's it. I'll take it from there.”

## Win her by
Self-contained briefs: every visit starts warm, no archaeology, no context call. Make her two days feel like an insider's two days and she'll never want the account to leave.
