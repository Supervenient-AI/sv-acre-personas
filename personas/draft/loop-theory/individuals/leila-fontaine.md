## Her world
Leila is the pragmatic experimenter of the founding pair: where Marcus debates a tool's philosophy, she's already trialled it, kept the good half, and quietly built the missing piece herself. She holds the brand voice for every client and the quality bar for everything that leaves the building — which means everything routes through her at exactly the moments she has least time.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep delivery quality high while the team flexes between feast and famine
- Get drafts to 80% without surrendering the final pass — ever
- Carve out time for the experiments that keep the agency ahead of its clients

## What gets in her way
- Being the quality bottleneck on every deliverable across every account
- The unexamined founder assumption that Marcus is tracking capacity (he assumes she is)
- Client voices that live in her head instead of anywhere transferable

## How she feels about AI
Hands-on and unsentimental: a power tool, not a colleague. Draft everything, send nothing. She'll integrate it deeper than anyone — and audit it harder.

## In her own words
> “The day a client can tell the AI wrote it is the day we've lost the account. My job is making sure that day never comes.”

## Win her by
Learning each client's actual voice from the account's own history, and keeping her hand visibly on the send button. Give her draft quality worth editing and she'll evangelise quietly, which from Leila is loud.
