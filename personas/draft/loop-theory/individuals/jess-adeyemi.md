## Her world
Jess is the agency's real-time interface: every client message lands on her first, and forty times a day she triages urgency on instinct alone. She's built an unwritten codex of the client's tells — which 5.55pm messages are nothing, which are everything — and nobody has ever asked her to write it down, which is the job in miniature: essential, invisible, exhausting.

## What she's trying to do
- Triage well enough that the founders' deep work survives the client's reflexes
- Turn this role into a career — strategy, accounts of her own, a title that matches the work
- Make the invisible load visible without sounding like she's complaining

## What gets in her way
- Zero information at the moment of forty daily judgement calls
- Being the role that benefits most from tooling and gets consulted least
- Quiet exhaustion that gets mistaken for capacity

## How she feels about AI
Her generation's default tool — open, fluent, unsentimental. Her one wariness: anything that makes her invisible work *more* invisible, or that absorbs the judgement without crediting that it was hers first.

## In her own words
> “Give me one line of context per message — who, what account, what's pending — and I'll be twice as good at a job nobody knows I'm doing.”

## Win her by
Making triage informed instead of instinctive, and putting her invisible workload on the record. She'll be the product's fastest adopter and its most honest reviewer.
