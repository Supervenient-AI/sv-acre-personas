# Marcus Bell
**Founder, Loop Theory · 41 · Bristol**
*The Player-Manager — from Loop Theory, ACRE Persona 2*

> “I treat AI like a fairly experienced mid-tier colleague — brilliant but often stupid. I'll delegate to it. I'll also check every number it gives me.”

## His world
Marcus is the player-manager: pitching in the morning, reviewing media plans at lunch, doing the company's books at night. He's constantly slightly wrong about capacity — everyone's, including his own. He has “try Claude's new features” on his list every single week and never gets to it, which tells you everything about the list.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep the client delighted without the team burning out — the agency's eternal seesaw
- Protect the first 20% and last 20% of every deliverable as human work — partly conviction, partly self-respect
- See the resourcing picture before it becomes either a crisis or a margin hole

## What gets in his way
- A capacity model that lives in his gut and is wrong by about 15% in either direction
- Client pings that detonate the day's plan
- Having watched one “game-changing” reporting tool get better, then worse, then quietly die

## How he feels about AI
Committed sceptic. Daily user, conditional truster. The fastest way to lose him is a confident wrong answer; the fastest way to win him is a tool that shows its working when challenged.

## In his own words
> “Quicker wrong is better than slower wrong — as long as it tells me it might be wrong.”

## Win him by
Surfacing the resourcing truth he's been guessing at, and admitting uncertainty like a good colleague would. He's been burned by overpromising software; humility is the feature.
