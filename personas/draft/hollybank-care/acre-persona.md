# ACRE Persona 44 — The Rota That Holds Lives
### Hollybank Care — a 40-carer domiciliary care agency where the coordination problem visits people's homes four times a day

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #36 (Family 10, care & clinical); flagged in the universe doc as heavy — but "the social value and the urgency of the coordination problem are unmatched."*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Rota-Bound Lifeline
**Instance:** Hollybank Care, Barnsley. Registered manager, deputy, two care coordinators, a trainer, office admin — and ~40 carers delivering ~2,800 visits a week to ~160 clients in their own homes: medication prompts, personal care, meals, company. Mostly council-commissioned, partly private.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | A constellation whose spokes enter homes: rotating carers, anxious families, GPs and district nurses, council commissioners and safeguarding teams — coordinated around clients who are the most vulnerable principals in the entire library. |
| Tooling | A care-management system (rota, eMAR medication records, visit logging via carer phones), the council's commissioning portal, the office phones (which never stop), WhatsApp (officially discouraged, universally used), paper in clients' homes (the folder by the kettle). |
| Who carries coordination cost | The coordinators, brutally: the rota is a live, constantly breaking object — sickness, traffic, a hospital admission — re-solved hourly. The registered manager carries the regulatory weight personally (her name is on the CQC registration). |
| Buying behaviour | Margin-desperate (council rates vs. real costs), churn-plagued (carer turnover ~30%/year, the sector's wound), buys what provably reduces missed visits or office chaos. The care-management system is the incumbent spine. |
| What failure costs | A missed visit is a person on the floor, un-medicated, unfed — the library's most direct human cost. A safeguarding miss is the unthinkable with a registration-ending inquiry behind it. A CQC downgrade empties the council's referral pipeline. The stakes are asymmetric to the fees in a way no other persona matches. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A standing constellation (the Foxgrove perpetual pattern at human stakes): rotating carers delivering scheduled care into private homes, coordinated against constant disruption, watched by families, commissioned by councils, regulated by the CQC — where every coordination artefact has a person's day, dignity or safety on the other side of it.

**Cast:**
- **Angela Brafield, 49** — registered manager; her name on the registration, her sleep on the rota (Barnsley)
- **Becky Stowe, 31** — care coordinator; the rota's live solver (Barnsley)
- **Marius Ionescu, 38** — senior carer; eleven years, knows sixty clients' kettles (Wombwell)
- **Julie Cartwright, 54** — daughter of a client; POA, WhatsApp-anxious, lives in Reading
- **Edna Hartley, 88** — the client: four visits a day, fierce independence, a folder by the kettle
- **Plus:** the deputy, the second coordinator, ~39 carers, GPs and district nurses, the council's commissioners and safeguarding team, pharmacists, the CQC

**External immovable nodes:**
- **The CQC** — the registration is the business; inspections read the records cold (the golden-thread pattern at its most humane: the visit log is the defence and the truth)
- **The council** — commissioning rates, visit specifications and safeguarding referrals; payer and regulator-adjacent at once
- **The NHS edge** — GPs, district nurses, hospital discharges that arrive Friday 4pm with new care needs and no notice
- **The clients' conditions themselves** — dementia's progression, a fall, a UTI's sudden confusion: care needs change on no one's schedule

**Topology:** A standing hub with home-shaped spokes: each client is a micro-constellation (client, family, GP, pharmacy, their assigned carers) and the rota is the master object binding 160 of them to 40 rotating humans. The carers are the only nodes who see the clients; the office sees the carers' logs; the family sees neither and worries about both.

**Lifecycle:** Perpetual, pulsing on the rota's week and each client's arc:
- *Per client (months–years):* referral (council package or private) → assessment and care plan → the standing rhythm (the same carers, ideally — continuity *is* care quality) → the slow arc (needs increase, visits lengthen) → hospital episodes (the constellation's interrupts) → end of life or residential move — the only persona whose lifecycle's end is a person's.
- *Per carer (the churn cycle):* recruitment → training → the rounds (alone, in cars, in homes) → burnout or loyalty; 30% leave yearly, taking client knowledge with them.
- *Per week:* the rota built, broken and re-solved; weekend coverage as recurring crisis.
- *Per day:* four medication windows; the morning round's domino timing; the 7am sickness calls that re-solve everything.
- *Trust curve:* Edna's trust is in *faces* — Marius, not Hollybank; rota churn spends it directly. Julie's trust is in *information* — silence reads as neglect (the Wrenfield vacuum pattern, with her mother inside it). The council's trust is documentary; the CQC's is evidential. Four trust currencies, one rota.

**Cadence:** The rota's daily pulse, the office phones' constant ring (carers, families, GPs, the council), handover notes between visits, the monthly care-plan reviews that slip, the quarterly commissioning meetings.

**Boundary objects:** **The rota** (the master object — 2,800 weekly commitments to vulnerable people), the care plan (the visit's instructions, decaying against reality), the visit log and eMAR (the evidence layer: care's golden thread), the folder by the kettle (paper truth in the home), the handover note, the safeguarding referral (the bright line's paperwork), the CQC evidence trail.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On Becky, hourly — the rota under disruption is a live constraint-solver's job done by phone and instinct; on Angela for everything regulatory and every hard judgement; on *Marius and the senior carers*, invisibly — the inter-visit knowledge ("Edna didn't seem right; check tomorrow's lunch call") that the logs half-capture.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Marius holds sixty clients' real states — the kettle-level knowledge no care plan captures. Becky holds the rota's solvable space (who can cover, who's near, who Edna will open the door to). Angela holds the regulatory map and the safeguarding judgement. Julie holds her mother's history and her own dread. The care plans hold last review's truth. The visit logs hold what was recorded between door and car. *Edna holds her own preferences and increasingly cannot keep them* — the transactive memory question at its most poignant.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Rota fragility: sickness cascading into missed or late visits — **eliminate** the cascade's opacity (re-solving with full visibility of constraints and continuity); **contain** the workforce reality
2. Continuity churn: strangers at Edna's door — **contain** the churn economics; **eliminate** the *avoidable* discontinuity (the rota optimising for coverage when it could optimise for faces)
3. The inter-visit knowledge gap: "didn't seem right" observations dying in cars between calls — **eliminate**; this is the care-quality signal the whole system runs on
4. Family-information vacuum: Julie's silence-fed dread — **eliminate** (the Wrenfield update discipline, four times daily stakes)
5. Care-plan decay: instructions ageing against a progressing condition — **eliminate** the lag
6. Hospital-discharge ambushes: new needs landing Friday 4pm — **contain** the NHS edge; **eliminate** the scramble's blindness
7. The safeguarding bright line — **preserve**, absolutely: when a carer suspects harm, the referral happens, full stop; no tool may add a single step of friction to it, and the record must protect the carer who raised it
8. Carer isolation and burnout — **contain** structurally; **eliminate** the invisibility (lone workers whose entire day is unseen by colleagues)

**Conflicting interests:**
- The council pays for 30-minute visits; Edna's good day needs 40; the carer absorbs the gap unpaid or leaves early guilty
- Julie wants constant information; Edna wants privacy and dignity; both are right (the scoped-truth triangle with a POA)
- The rota wants efficiency; continuity wants the same faces; the carers want liveable rounds
- Angela's registration wants documentation; the carers' day has no slack to write it

*Contain-class with one absolute preserve: the safeguarding line. And the deepest contain in the library — care economics — which the product cannot fix but must never worsen.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Carer substitution is *daily life* — the constellation is built to survive it, and pays continuity costs every time (Edna's door opens slower for strangers). Swap *Marius*: sixty clients' kettle-knowledge leaves — the deepest tribal-knowledge concentration in the library, held by the lowest-paid expert in it. Swap *Becky*: the rota's solvable space collapses to the software's naive view; missed visits within days. Swap *Angela*: the registration itself must transfer — a regulated succession event (the Calder/Susan pattern with CQC stakes). Swap *Edna's GP*: the medical edge re-learns her from records. ◆ The finding: this constellation substitutes constantly at the edge and cannot substitute at all at three specific nodes — and the sector's churn means it's always mid-substitution somewhere.

**Afterlife:** Clients' endings are the constellation's solemn rhythm: the record closes (and must be impeccable — families grieve, sometimes litigate, the CQC reads), the family edge dissolves with a thank-you card or a complaint, the carers carry it personally and unsupported. The visit-log archive is the agency's evidence, its memory and its defence. Carer alumni spread the agency's reputation through the sector's tight labour market — the employer brand *is* the recruitment pipeline.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Re-solve the rota under disruption with continuity as a first-class objective — not just coverage, but *faces Edna knows*, computed in seconds when the 7am call comes. Capture the inter-visit signal: a carer's 20-second voice note in the car becoming structured observations, trends flagged ("third 'not herself' this week → prompt the district nurse"). Keep Julie informed by default — visit happened, mum was bright, lunch eaten — scoped to what Edna consents to share (dignity's version of one-truth-per-audience). Keep care plans live against observed reality. Assemble CQC evidence continuously from work already logged. Protect the safeguarding line absolutely: one tap, zero friction, carer shielded. And lighten the carers' load *first*: the sector's churn is the constraint everything else lives inside.

### Angela Brafield, 49 — Registered Manager *(The Name on the Registration)*
**Barnsley.** Twenty-five years from carer to registered manager; carries 160 vulnerable people's safety as a personal legal fact. Answers her phone at 2am because the alternative is worse. **Attitude to AI:** cautious, evidence-hungry — anything that strengthens the record and lightens the carers gets a fair hearing; anything that adds a step to a carer's day does not. **Winning her looks like:** the evidence trail builds itself from care already given, the rota's crises shrink, and she sleeps through a night.

### Becky Stowe, 31 — Care Coordinator *(The Live Solver)*
**Barnsley.** Solves a constraint-satisfaction problem with feelings in it, hourly, by phone. Knows which carer can take which client and which combination ends in tears. **Attitude to AI:** desperate yes — if it knows what she knows (the constraints the software doesn't hold). **Winning her looks like:** the 7am sickness call triggers a continuity-aware re-solve in seconds, with her judgement on the exceptions only.

### Marius Ionescu, 38 — Senior Carer *(The Kettle-Level Expert)*
**Wombwell.** Eleven years; sixty clients' preferences, moods and baselines in his head; the carer families ask for by name. Paid marginally above minimum wage to hold the constellation's deepest knowledge. **Attitude to AI:** phone-pragmatic — voice notes yes, forms no. **Winning him looks like:** his observations count (captured in seconds, visibly acted on), his knowledge is credited, and the round's admin stops eating the minutes that belong to Edna.

### Julie Cartwright, 54 — Daughter & POA *(The Distant Vigil)*
**Reading.** Rings the office twice a week; reads silence as decline; carries the guilt of distance. **Attitude to AI:** whatever ends the vacuum. **Winning her looks like:** the daily quiet reassurance — visit done, mum bright, lunch eaten — scoped to what her mother consents to, arriving without her asking.

### Edna Hartley, 88 — The Client *(The Principal)*
**Barnsley.** Taught primary school for forty years; tolerates help, treasures Marius, despises being discussed. **Attitude to AI:** none, and it owes her everything anyway. **Winning her looks like:** known faces at the door, her preferences honoured without re-stating, her dignity intact in every record written about her — the entire library's purpose, distilled to one kitchen.

**Product needs from the edge map:**
