# Julie Cartwright
**Daughter & Power of Attorney · 54 · Reading**
*The Distant Vigil — from Hollybank, ACRE Persona 44*

> “Mum's in Barnsley. I'm in Reading. Four visits a day happen that I know nothing about unless something goes wrong — so my brain fills the silence with everything that could. I ring the office twice a week and feel like a nuisance both times. I'm not a nuisance. I'm a daughter doing distance on guilt and fumes.”

## Her world
Julie holds legal authority and informational poverty: she can sign for her mother's care and cannot see whether Tuesday's lunch call happened. The silence is operationally normal — visits occurring, care given, nothing wrong — and emotionally corrosive: distance converts no-news into dread. Her phone calls to the office are the vacuum's only valve, and she rations them out of politeness the office never asked for.

## What she's trying to do
- Know her mother is okay — today, specifically, not in aggregate
- Honour Edna's dignity: information scoped to what her mother consents to share
- Convert the guilt into something useful: decisions made well, visits that are visits rather than inspections

## What gets in her way
- The information vacuum between incidents
- Her own restraint: "I don't want to be one of those families"
- Two hundred miles, applied daily

## How she feels about AI
Irrelevant as a category; precious as an outcome. A daily line — visit done, mum bright, lunch eaten — would change the texture of her every weekday.

## In her own words
> “Three sentences a day: she's seen, she's well, she ate. Scoped to what Mum's happy sharing — it's her life, not my dashboard. That's the whole product. It's also, currently, science fiction.”

## Win her by
The consent-scoped daily digest: quiet reassurance by default, escalation when true — the vigil relieved without the dignity invoiced.
