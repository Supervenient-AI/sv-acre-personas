# Edna Hartley
**Client · 88 · Barnsley**
*The Principal — from Hollybank, ACRE Persona 44*

> “Forty years I taught primary school. I can still spot who's done their homework. Marius has always done his — he knows the tea, the tablets, the order of things. The new ones mean well and ask where everything is, and I tell them, and the telling costs me more than they know. I tolerate help. I treasure being known.”

## Her world
Edna's day is architected around four arrivals: who comes, whether she knows them, whether they know *her* — the tea, the tablets, the unspoken rules of a house that has hosted half a century. Continuity isn't her preference; it's the difference between care and intrusion. She despises being discussed, notices being processed, and extends to known carers a warmth that is, in truth, the best part of some days.

## What she's trying to do
- Stay home — the entire strategic objective, against which everything is measured
- Keep her dignity: known faces, honoured preferences, records about her that she'd recognise as her
- Not worry Julie — a project she manages with a teacher's selective reporting

## What gets in her way
- Strangers at the door, however kind
- Repeating herself to every new face — the small tax that measures the system's churn
- A body increasingly at odds with a fully operational mind

## How she feels about AI
She doesn't, and owes it nothing — it owes her everything. Every feature in this constellation is measured, finally, at her doorstep: known faces, honoured preferences, dignity in every record.

## In her own words
> “I don't need much. The right person, at the right time, who knows how I take my tea and doesn't make me say it. That's not technology. That's manners — and if your machines can arrange more manners, they're welcome.”

## Win her by
Never appearing: continuity at her door, preferences honoured without re-stating, her consent governing what Julie sees — the entire library's purpose, distilled to one kitchen and judged there.
