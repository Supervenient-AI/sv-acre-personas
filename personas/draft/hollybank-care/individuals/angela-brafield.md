# Angela Brafield
**Registered Manager, Hollybank Care · 49 · Barnsley**
*The Name on the Registration — from Hollybank, ACRE Persona 44*

> “A hundred and sixty people get four visits a day because my rota holds. My name is on the CQC registration — not the company's, mine. When the 2am phone rings, I answer it, because the alternative is somebody's mother on the floor till morning. I'd like one unbroken night. I'd like the record to build itself from care we already give.”

## Her world
Angela holds the sector's defining asymmetry: stakes measured in human dignity, margins measured in council-rate pennies, staff churn at 30% a year. Her rota is a living object broken hourly by life; her evidence trail is her defence and her clients' protection; her carers' goodwill is the whole machine, and anything that adds one minute to their day is a cost she will not pay.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep 160 people safe, seen and dignified — the job, undecorated
- Make CQC evidence continuous: assembled from care already logged, never reconstructed
- Slow the churn: lighter carer loads, visible appreciation, rotas that respect lives

## What gets in her way
- Disruption cascades: one 7am sickness call re-solving forty visits
- The inter-visit signal ("Edna's not herself") dying in cars between calls
- Council rates that price 30-minute visits against 40-minute needs

## How she feels about AI
Cautious, evidence-hungry, carer-protective: anything that strengthens the record and lightens the carers gets a fair hearing; anything that adds a step to a carer's day does not — her red line, stated in every vendor meeting, rarely heard.

## In her own words
> “Zero added carer friction. Start there or don't start. Then show me the rota re-solving with continuity in it, and the evidence building itself — and I'll show you forty carers who stay.”

## Win her by
Continuity-aware rota re-solving, evidence-from-care-given, and the carer load *lightened first* — the registered manager's sleep, underwritten by architecture.
