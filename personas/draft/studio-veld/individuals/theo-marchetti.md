## His world
Theo is the studio's elasticity: booked when pitches land, gone when they don't. He produces excellent work inside other people's systems and leaves no trace by design — his rationale lives in his Figma files' logic, which dies with his contract. He's relaxed about most things except two: credit (his name stays on his work) and surveillance (his hours are his business; he sells outcomes).

## What he's trying to do
- Stay first-call at four studios without becoming furniture at any
- Start each booking productive — context on day one, not day three
- Build the reputation that converts day rates into project rates

## What gets in his way
- Re-onboarding from scratch, every project, everywhere
- His design rationale evaporating when contracts end — bad for the studio, worse for his legacy
- Tools that track time on a person who sells judgement

## How he feels about AI
Fluent and unbothered — it's in his workflow already. Alert to two failure modes: tools that harvest his style, and tools that time his day.

## In his own words
> “Hand me the project's memory when I arrive and keep my name on my work when I leave. Everything between those two is just craft.”

## Win him by
Day-one context without meetings, and attribution that survives his departure. The bench that's treated like memory-holding colleagues comes back faster and better.
