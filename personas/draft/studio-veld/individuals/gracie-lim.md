## Her world
Gracie runs the studio's reality layer: which rounds are left, which freelancer is free, which client relationship is one bad email from wobbling. The feedback ledger and the scope meter exist — in her head, maintained by vigilance, invisible until she's on holiday and everything quietly degrades. She spends her actual diplomacy budget on maybe three moments a week; the rest is consumed by mechanical chasing that deserves no diplomacy at all.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep six projects' scope, feedback and feelings reconciled
- Make round-creep visible *before* it becomes resentment or write-off
- Save her diplomacy for the moments that need a human

## What gets in her way
- Feedback arriving via five channels with no attribution or anchor
- "One more pass" with no unit cost
- Being the single human instance of the studio's operating system

## How she feels about AI
Enthusiastic — she is the person the product relieves first, and she knows exactly which 70% of her job she'd hand over by Friday.

## In her own words
> “Consolidate every note, attribute it, pin it to the frame it's about, and count the rounds. I'll handle the feelings. I'm good at the feelings.”

## Win her by
The ledger keeping itself: feedback consolidated and anchored, rounds counted and costed, freelancer status live. She becomes the studio's diplomat instead of its database.
