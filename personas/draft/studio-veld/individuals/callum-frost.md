## His world
Callum's Tuesday crit is a seminar: work pinned up, opinions earned, decisions made and — crucially — *reasoned*. He synthesises the room into direction better than anyone, and watches that synthesis evaporate the moment everyone returns to their desks. The rationale behind every design system the studio ships exists nowhere except in fading memories and his own head.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep the quality bar high as the studio scales past one room and one Tuesday
- Get crit decisions and their *reasons* captured without changing the crit's chemistry
- Develop the team: the crit is also his classroom

## What gets in his way
- Rationale evaporation: guidelines ship with the what, never the why
- Critique relayed second-hand arriving vague ("they want it more premium")
- Drudgery — file hygiene, asset prep — eating hours the craft deserves

## How he feels about AI
Open for the drudge layer, protective of judgement. His test: does the tool capture the crit or change it? Anything that makes people perform for the record instead of arguing for the work fails.

## In his own words
> “Record the room without making anyone talk *to* the recording. If the crit feels even slightly observed, it stops being a crit.”

## Win him by
Ambient capture: decisions and rationale harvested from the crit as it naturally happens, becoming the guidelines' "why" for free — the room's chemistry untouched.
