## Her world
Anneke sells judgement in a market drowning in execution. Her days split between concept reviews (where her eye earns the fees) and client politics (where the fees get defended): reading which stakeholder's opinion is load-bearing, which "small thought" carries a CEO behind it, and when a project's emotional weather is about to turn. She wins by taste and loses by gaps — scope gaps, feedback gaps, version gaps.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the studio's work at portfolio grade while keeping it profitable — they're different disciplines
- Walk into every presentation knowing exactly who said what about which frame
- Protect the crit culture as the studio grows past the room it fits in

## What gets in her way
- Round-creep with no ledger: "one more pass" priced at zero and costing thousands
- Feedback chaos across decks, DMs and meetings
- Tools that are capable and ugly — she'll reject them on sight, and she's not wrong to

## How she feels about AI
Aesthetically suspicious, practically curious. Proposals and admin, yes; near a concept, never. And the product itself must pass a designer's taste bar to exist in her building.

## In her own words
> “If the tool is ugly, what does that tell my clients about my judgement? Everything in this studio is the portfolio. Including the software.”

## Win her by
Being beautiful, first — then giving her the feedback ledger and the rounds meter. She'll adopt for the politics intelligence and stay for the margin recovery.
