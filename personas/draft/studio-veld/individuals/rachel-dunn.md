## Her world
Rachel is the membrane between two organisms: a studio that works in iterations and a scale-up that experiences iterations as indecision. Every presentation she relays upward gets her CEO's raw response relayed downward — and she does the softening in both directions, unpaid and unthanked. Her dread is specific: the week-nine CEO drive-by that reopens week-two decisions she'd reported as settled.

## What she's trying to do
- Land the rebrand and convert it into her next title
- Always have the "what changed and why" story ready before her CEO asks
- Keep both sides believing she's in control, which mostly makes it true

## What gets in her way
- Decision archaeology: she can't reconstruct why route B won in March
- Her CEO's taste arriving as texts with no anchor to any artefact
- Her own midnight ChatGPT habit producing feedback the studio experiences as ambush

## How she feels about AI
Daily, pragmatic, ungoverned. She's not going to stop pasting decks into chatbots — but she'd trade the habit instantly for a sanctioned surface that's better at it.

## In her own words
> “Give me the rationale trail — what we decided, why, who signed it — formatted for a board slide. That's my armour. I will love whoever gives me my armour.”

## Win her by
The forwardable why: every decision with its rationale, board-ready. Make her look unshakeable upward and the studio gains its best internal advocate.
