## His world
Pawel is where the project's paper meets its physics. Every morning he re-sequences reality: the plasterer's van broke down, the steel's late, the client moved a socket on Saturday. His head holds the site's true state — what's buried, what's bodged, what's promised — and the firm's systems hold a fraction of it, because every system so far has asked him to type at the end of a twelve-hour day.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep six trades sequenced through a building that changes daily
- Have the right drawing in his hand — the *current* one — every time
- Get the site's truth recorded without becoming a data-entry clerk in muddy boots

## What gets in his way
- Superseded drawings circulating like rumours
- Decisions pending at the hub while trades stand idle
- Documentation systems designed by people who've never held one in the rain

## How he feels about AI
Fine — if it speaks WhatsApp and never slows the morning. His photos and voice notes *are* the record; the product's job is to receive them and do the typing.

## In his own words
> “I already send the photo and say what it is. If that's not enough for your system, your system's wrong, not me.”

## Win him by
Making his existing habit — photo, voice note, send — become the official as-built record automatically. He does nothing new; the firm gains everything; his knowledge finally counts as the asset it is.
