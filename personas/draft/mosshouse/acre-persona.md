# ACRE Persona 10 — The Builder-Hub Home
### Mosshouse Build — a 12-person design-and-build firm running high-end residential projects with the client on site weekly

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #2 (Family 1, hub-and-spoke); not yet interview-grounded.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Accountable Builder
**Instance:** Mosshouse Build, Surrey. MD/owner, a contracts manager, two site foremen, office manager, and a rotating bench of 20–40 subcontractors. Two to three live projects, £400k–£1.5m each, 9–18 months.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | The firm is a coordination shell: nearly all hands on site are subcontractors; the architect and engineer belong to other firms; the client is a daily presence. |
| Tooling | Office 365 + a construction management app the foremen half-use, WhatsApp groups per project (the real system), paper drawings in the site cabin with coffee rings on the critical dimension. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The contracts manager officially; the MD actually; the foreman absorbs whatever lands on site by 7:30am. |
| Buying behaviour | Sceptical, referral-driven; bought the management app because a rival firm had it. Will pay for fewer disputes, not for dashboards. |
| What failure costs | A change that ripples through six trades costs real money within days. A client relationship that sours mid-build is a year of misery — you can't unbuild. Defects and retention disputes bleed margin for years after handover. |

---

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A builder as hub between an emotionally invested client, external designers, and six-plus trades whose sequenced work amplifies every change — with statutory inspectors gating progress.

**Cast:**
- **Daniel Moss, 49** — MD/owner, the hub (Godalming)
- **Sera Achebe, 43** — the client; barrister, visits site every Saturday with a list
- **Verity Lamb, 39** — architect, external practice, protective of her design
- **Pawel Adamski, 38** — site foreman, the firm's real interface with reality
- **Plus:** contracts manager, structural engineer (external), electricians/plumbers/roofers/plasterers, building control inspector

**External immovable nodes:**
- **Building control** — staged inspections that stop work until passed; their diary, not yours
- **The structural warranty provider** — photographic evidence requirements at stages nobody remembers until asked
- **The party-wall surveyor and the neighbours** — veto power over working hours and crane oversail, exercised by complaint

**Topology:** Hub-and-spoke around the builder, with a triangle at the top (client ↔ builder ↔ architect) that carries all the politics. Trades are sequential spokes — each one's start date is the previous one's finish, so the topology is also a chain in disguise.

**Lifecycle:**
- *Assembly:* tender won on a price everyone privately knows is optimistic; trades booked against a programme that is already fiction by week three.
- *Groundworks-to-watertight:* weather-hostage phase; trust spends fast when the site looks the same for a month.
- *Midpoint reset:* first-fix walkthrough — the client *sees* the rooms at true size for the first time and re-opens settled decisions ("the kitchen window should be wider") exactly when changes are most expensive.
- *Crunch:* second fix and finishes — every trade back on site simultaneously, sequencing collapses into negotiation.
- *Dissolution:* handover, snagging, retention; the relationship's final memory is formed by how the last 2% goes.
- *Trust curve:* the client's trust is highest at contract signing and erodes with every Saturday list that takes a week to answer; the trades' trust in Daniel is about one thing — being paid on time — and is the firm's true asset.

**Cadence:** 7:30am site phone calls, Saturday client walkarounds, WhatsApp all day, a monthly valuation that everyone argues about, formal letters only when things have already gone wrong.

**Boundary objects:** The drawings (intent to Verity, instructions to Pawel, ammunition to everyone in a dispute), the programme (aspiration), the monthly valuation, the variations log (where money and resentment accumulate), the snagging list.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On Pawel daily (which trade, where, with what materials, after whose work) and Daniel weekly (client expectations vs site truth vs cash flow). The contracts manager documents what survives.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Pawel knows what's actually behind the plasterboard. Verity knows the design intent and which details matter. Sera knows her own non-negotiables, revealed incrementally. Daniel knows the money and which subbie is reliable in rain. The variations log knows the truth of who asked for what — when it's filled in, which is after the argument.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Change-ripple blindness: a moved doorway re-sequences six trades and nobody prices the ripple until it's happened — **eliminate** the blindness; **contain** the change negotiation itself
2. Saturday instructions: the client directs a trade directly; the record never hears about it — **contain**: she may walk her own site; the bypass of the record is what must go
3. Drawing vs site-reality divergence discovered at inspection — **eliminate**
4. The variations log as post-hoc weapon instead of live ledger — **eliminate**
5. Pawel as single point of site knowledge — **eliminate** (the dependency, not the man)
6. Verity's design-protection objections and building control's staged "no" — **preserve**: both are the quality system

**Conflicting interests:**
- Sera wants her home perfect and her contingency intact — these are the same money
- Daniel's margin lives partly in variations; his reputation lives in not seeming to
- Verity defends design intent against value-engineering she isn't paid to police
- Trades want clean runs and hate working around each other and precious finishes

*Contain-class: anchor every change to a priced, signed variation within 48 hours — the dispute-prevention window is short and closes silently.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap a trade — routine, days of slippage. Swap Pawel mid-build and the project's physical memory (what's buried, what's bodged, what's promised) leaves in a van; the firm pays in re-opened walls. Swap Verity and design intent must be re-derived from drawings that never contained all of it. Sera cannot be swapped; if her trust fails, every remaining month is fought in writing.

**Afterlife:** Retention and defects run 12–24 months past handover — the constellation half-lives long after dissolution. Reputation is hyperlocal and dinner-party-borne; Sera's verdict prices the next three tenders. The trades carry forward; the as-built record (what's really in the walls) is worth real money at every future renovation — and today it lives in Pawel's head and a dead WhatsApp group.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Catch every change at the moment of utterance — site, Saturday, or email — and turn it into a priced variation before it's a dispute. Keep one live programme that re-ripples when reality moves. Make the as-built record automatic (photos, dates, decisions). Give Sera a truthful weekly picture so the Saturday list shrinks. Live in WhatsApp, because the site does.

### Daniel Moss, 49 — MD *(The Accountable Hub)*
**Godalming.** Carpenter by trade, builder by ascent; runs the firm from a Hilux. Trusts handshakes, documents reluctantly, regrets it annually. **Attitude to AI:** sceptical until it wins him one variation dispute; evangelical after. **Winning him looks like:** the variations ledger fills itself and the monthly valuation stops being an argument.

### Sera Achebe, 43 — Client *(The Saturday Inspector)*
**Guildford.** Barrister; reads contracts for a living and her building contract twice. Decisive, fair, allergic to vagueness. **Attitude to AI:** professional-grade comfort; would prefer a clear weekly digest to her own Saturday forensics. **Winning her looks like:** she always knows status, cost position and what needs her decision — before Saturday.

### Pawel Adamski, 38 — Site Foreman *(The Reality Interface)*
**Woking.** Fifteen years on UK sites; speaks four languages, all fluently about concrete. Photographs everything on his own phone — the firm's best record, privately held. **Attitude to AI:** fine, if it speaks WhatsApp and never slows the morning. **Winning him looks like:** his photos and voice notes *become* the site record without extra typing, and the right drawing is always the one in his hand.

### Verity Lamb, 39 — Architect *(The Intent Guardian)*
**Farnham.** Small practice, this is her flagship project; fee long since consumed by attentiveness. **Attitude to AI:** cautiously positive; her bar is whether the record protects design decisions or launders their erosion. **Winning her looks like:** every detail-drawing query reaches her before the trade improvises, and value-engineering happens to her face.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Daniel ↔ Sera | JCT building contract | High at signing → **erodes by surprise**: every cost shock and silent week spends it | Email + Saturday walkarounds | He knows site truth daily; she discovers it weekly — the gap is where trust dies |
| Daniel ↔ Verity | None (both contract to client) | Professional → **wary on money**: allies on quality, opponents on variations | Email + drawings | Classic triangle: each can blame the other to the client |
| Daniel ↔ trades | Subcontracts, often verbal | Loyalty-based → **payment-fragile**: one late payment run resets years | Phone + WhatsApp + cash rhythm | His real capital; invisible on any balance sheet |
| Pawel ↔ trades | Daily direction | Earned, gruff → stable | Site + WhatsApp | Sequencing power lives here, not in the programme document |
| Sera ↔ trades | None | Friendly → **the bypass edge** | Saturday chats | "While you're here, could you just…" — undocumented scope, weekly |
| Firm ↔ building control | Statutory | Procedural → gate-shaped | Inspections + portal | Their diary gates the programme; no relationship can hurry them |
| **Daniel ↔ AI** | — | Sceptical, dispute-converted | Phone-first | One won argument buys adoption |
| **Pawel ↔ AI** | — | Conditional on zero friction | WhatsApp bridge | The record must assemble from what he already sends |
| **Sera ↔ AI** | — | High, transparency-hungry | Client digest | Wants the truth; must get the truth — a sanitised feed will be detected and is fatal |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- The Daniel↔Sera edge needs *symmetrical truth at different resolutions*: she gets honest status without the firm's internal chaos; any whiff of a curated reality destroys the edge faster than the chaos would.
- The change-capture window is the product: utterance → priced variation in 48 hours, from any channel, including a Saturday conversation reported after the fact.
- The trades will never onboard: every trade-facing behaviour must work over WhatsApp and voice notes, or the site routes around the product the way it routes around the app it already ignores.
