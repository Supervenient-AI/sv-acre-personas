# ACRE Persona 3 — The Conscripted Spoke
### Halyard & Vance — a 2-person PR consultancy living inside its clients' constellations

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Micro-Consultancy as Periphery
**Instance:** Halyard & Vance — two co-founders, one PR, one sales, who left agency leadership roles to splice the two disciplines, plus one overflow freelancer used a couple of days a month. Five clients, five invoices, no finance team.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Inverted: **this account is almost never the hub.** In every engagement, Halyard & Vance is a spoke in someone else's constellation, conscripted into each client's channel of choice — Teams for one, Slack for another, WhatsApp for a third. |
| Tooling | Macs, Microsoft 365 (chosen because clients live in Teams), Google Drive for storage, ChatGPT business. Plus whatever each client imposes. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The founders, at midnight — proofing, desk research and invoice-chasing with no junior to lean on. |
| Buying behaviour | Fast to trial, allergic to upfront annual payment (a transcription app lost them on exactly that). Tools must earn their monthly fee against a visible subscription line. |
| What failure costs | Five clients is the whole business: one lost retainer is 20% of revenue; one late-paying client is a cash-flow event. Red lines: nothing touches the bank account; nothing exposes what people are paid. |

---

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A micro-supplier simultaneously embedded at the periphery of several client constellations, each with its own channel, cadence and culture — while running its own tiny business in the gaps.

**Cast:**
- **Nat Calloway, 41** — co-founder, PR (Stockport)
- **Ash Devine, 43** — co-founder, sales (Brighton)
- **Priti Rao, 36** — freelance PR, overflow (Bristol)
- Per client: a **marketing contact** (day-to-day), a **finance contact** (invoices vanish here), occasionally a **founder/CEO** — e.g. **Dominic Shaw, 48**, founder of a B2B tech client (opinions arrive from here)

**External immovable nodes** (outside the working team, veto power over it):
- **Journalists and editors** — the actual gate on the deliverable: coverage happens on the news cycle's schedule, not the retainer's, and no amount of coordination can compel it
- **HMRC** — VAT and self-assessment deadlines tracked on vigilance alone; misses are personal, not corporate
- **Client finance departments** — payment runs on their calendar; the invoice enters "like a coin into a vending machine that sometimes pays out"

**Topology:** Inverted hub-and-spoke. The pair are spokes five times over; the only place all five engagements meet is in the founders' heads and a spreadsheet.

**Lifecycle:** Five overlapping engagement lifecycles plus the business's own:
- *Conscription (per client, week 0):* the client's channel, cadence and culture are imposed; the agency adapts or grates. The early weeks set the responsiveness expectations the retainer will be judged by forever.
- *Monthly pulse (per client):* activity → coverage report → invoice → silence → chase.
- *Midpoint equivalent:* around month 4–6 of any retainer, the client's memory of the original scope blurs and "while you're at it" requests creep in; nobody owns re-stating the boundary, so it erodes instead.
- *The business's own calendar:* HMRC quarters and the pipeline's feast-famine wave, running underneath all five engagements and visible to no client.
- *Trust curve:* presumed high at signing (they bought senior people); spent by every missed nuance in a channel the founders didn't choose and can't monitor well. No client ever sees the other four constellations competing for the same two heads.

**Cadence:** Five clients' cadences at once, none of them the agency's own. Slack "never notifies you of what's going on"; WhatsApp blurs work and life; Teams is where documents go to multiply.

**Boundary objects:** The article/press release (which the client may unilaterally "improve" with ChatGPT), the coverage report (the retainer's monthly justification), the media list, the invoice.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** Entirely on Nat — cross-client sequencing, deadline tracking, remembering which conversation lives in which channel. Ash carries the pipeline's articulation. Nobody carries the business's own ops, which is why it happens at midnight.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Nat holds every client's context, voice and media relationships — the business *is* her head. Ash holds the pipeline and the commercial instincts. Priti holds fragments that expire between engagements. The spreadsheet holds an approximation of the money. No one holds a unified picture of commitments across all five clients; surprises are structural.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged: eliminate / contain / preserve)*:
1. Channel fragmentation — five clients, four platforms, zero unified inbox — **eliminate**
2. Invoice-chasing loops: client contact → finance contact → silence → restart ("the bane of my life") — **eliminate**
3. Workslop flowing *downstream*: the client redoes agency work with AI and sends it back worse, with feelings now attached — **contain**: the client's right to give feedback is legitimate; what must go is the silent rewrite that destroys provenance and the diplomatic tax of proving what was delivered
4. The founders proofreading and desk-researching at midnight because there's no junior — **eliminate**
5. Cash-flow blindness — money in, money out, HMRC deadlines on vigilance alone — **eliminate**
6. Nat's editorial "no" — telling a client their announcement isn't a story — **preserve**: this judgement is what they're paying senior people for; any tool that helps clients route around it damages the work

**Conflicting interests:**
- Each client wants the agency present in *their* channel at *their* cadence, as if exclusive
- The founders need one consolidated view of everything, and some evenings
- Priti wants the interesting work, not the update reports
- Client finance departments are structurally indifferent to the supplier's cash flow

*Contain-class throughout: none resolve. The AI's job is to absorb the cost of the asymmetry — five channels consolidated agency-side, chasing done politely by something with no relationship capital to lose.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap Priti and little breaks — her context was fragmentary by design (which is its own indictment: the overflow valve can't hold pressure). Swap a client's *marketing contact* and that engagement resets: new channel preferences, new cadence, a retainer suddenly re-justified to a stranger. Swap **Nat** and the business stops existing — five clients' context, every media relationship and the real to-do list leave with her. There is no second brain. The hub of this inverted constellation is a single human point of failure distributed across five other companies' tools.

**Afterlife:** Engagements end; what persists is Nat's media relationships (her career asset, not the firm's), the testimonial, and the referral — micro-consultancies live on the repeat game. What evaporates: the client's context, the channel history scattered across platforms the agency loses access to, and the record of what was actually delivered (which matters precisely when a workslop dispute resurfaces at renewal). Currently nothing is deliberately archived; the firm's institutional memory is two people remembering things at each other.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Be the unified layer across channels the founders aren't allowed to choose. Chase invoices politely and persistently so a founder doesn't have to spend relationship capital doing it. Watch HMRC and cash-flow deadlines. Do the junior-staffer work — desk research on a journalist in seconds, proofing without rewriting ("proof it, don't change it" should not require a paragraph of instructions). Keep a clean record of what the agency delivered versus what came back. Never touch the bank.

## R — Roles

**Archetype load** (count of collaboration archetypes per seat; 3+ signals structural overload):

| Seat | Archetypes held | Load |
|---|---|---|
| Nat | Conductor · Specialist Maker · Coordinator · Strategist | **4 — "five-hatted" is the overload, expressed as a number** |
| Ash | Sponsor · Strategist (pipeline) | 2 |
| Priti | Specialist Maker · Peripheral Expert | 2 |
| Dominic (client) | Client Owner · Hidden Stakeholder | 2 — the combination that produces drive-by rewrites |
| Client finance contacts | Gatekeeper | 1 — and unreachable |

The product's first job: give Nat back the Coordinator hours, and hand the Gatekeeper edges (finance, HMRC) to something that doesn't sleep.

### Nat Calloway, 41 — Co-founder, PR *(The Five-Hatted)*
**Stockport.** Ex-MD of a mid-size agency; knows exactly what good looks like and does it all herself now. Uses ChatGPT like the junior team she no longer has: proofing, media-contact research, sanity-checking contract clauses she's not qualified to read. Rates her AI enthusiasm "3.5 to 4 — could live without it." Allergic to tools demanding upfront annual payment. **Attitude to AI:** pragmatic delegator — it's staff, not magic; it gets the work a junior would get and the scrutiny a junior would get. **Winning her looks like:** the five channels collapse into one view she checks once, the invoices chase themselves, and nothing she sends a client has been rewritten en route.

### Ash Devine, 43 — Co-founder, Sales *(The Pipeline Half)*
**Brighton.** Outlook loyalist (the reason they're on 365). Runs the new-business funnel on instinct and a spreadsheet. Half-watches the cash position and assumes Nat has the rest. **Attitude to AI:** mild interest, zero patience for new interfaces — if it isn't in Outlook, it doesn't exist. **Winning him looks like:** pipeline follow-ups drafted in his voice, in his inbox, and a cash-flow picture he didn't have to build.

### Priti Rao, 36 — Freelance PR *(The Overflow Valve)*
**Bristol.** Two days a month, more when it spikes. Sees fragments of accounts, never the whole; every brief arrives needing twenty minutes of context she has to ask for — unbillable, in both directions. **Attitude to AI:** fluent, uses it across all her clients; wary of any client-side tool that watches her hours. **Winning her looks like:** briefs arrive self-contained, context included, and she can be useful from minute one of a two-day month.

### Dominic Shaw, 48 — Client Founder *(The Improver)*
**Client-side, B2B tech.** Pays promptly, interferes constantly, owns a ChatGPT subscription and the confidence to use it on the agency's copy. Genuinely believes he's helping. **Attitude to AI:** maximal, ungoverned enthusiasm. **Winning him looks like:** he feels *more* involved, not less — his input lands as tracked, attributed feedback on the artefact instead of an 11pm rewrite with the provenance stripped.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nat ↔ each client contact | Monthly retainer | Warm → **eroding quietly under channel strain**: every missed nuance in an unwatched platform spends it. Sustained by: senior-quality work. Eroded by: slow responses the client never connects to their own channel choice | Theirs: Teams / Slack / WhatsApp | A spoke can't impose tooling on a hub; the AI must operate inside channels the agency doesn't control |
| Nat ↔ client finance contacts | None (process) | Low-warmth, transactional → flat | Email, into the void | The single best edge to automate: high-stakes, zero relationship at risk |
| Nat ↔ Dominic | Retainer (his firm) | Good → **strained by each "improvement"** — the workslop edge | His channel + surprise attachments | The AI's job is partly forensic: delivered vs. returned, with provenance |
| Nat ↔ Ash | Co-founders | Deep → stable | Calls + the spreadsheet | Two halves of one business on two different native tools; the Outlook gap is where things drop |
| Nat ↔ Priti | Day rate, ad hoc | Professional, warm → **shallow by design**: context expires between engagements | Email + Drive | Priti only ever sees fragments; the asymmetry is the onboarding cost |
| Nat ↔ journalists | None — reputational | Career-long, personal → the firm's real moat | Email, DMs, lunches | Belongs to Nat, not the firm; invisible to clients who think they're buying process |
| Practice ↔ HMRC | Statutory | N/A → deadline-driven | Gateway portal | Misses are personal liability; vigilance is currently the only system |
| **Nat ↔ AI** | — | Pragmatic 3.5–4/5, conditional | In-product + ChatGPT habits | Treats it as junior staff; will fire it like junior staff if it rewrites instead of proofing |
| **Ash ↔ AI** | — | Indifferent until it's in Outlook | Email bridge | Won't adopt an interface; the AI meets him in his inbox or not at all |
| **Priti ↔ AI** | — | Neutral, conscripted | In-product | Fine with context-giving, watchful for time-surveillance |
| **Dominic ↔ AI** | — | Enthusiastic, ungoverned | His own ChatGPT | The rogue-AI edge: the product must make the *governed* path more satisfying than the rewrite |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- The five client edges all run on platforms the agency can't choose. The product is the layer *behind* them — a unified agency-side view — or it's nothing.
- The finance-contact and HMRC edges are pure process with no relationship at risk: automate these first, win immediately.
- The Dominic edge defines a product principle: when the client has their own AI, the battle isn't adoption, it's provenance — keep an authoritative record of what was delivered, by whom, before "improvement".
