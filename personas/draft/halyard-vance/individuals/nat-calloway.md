# Nat Calloway
**Co-founder (PR), Halyard & Vance · 41 · Stockport**
*The Five-Hatted — from Halyard & Vance, ACRE Persona 3*

> “I used to have a junior team. Now I have ChatGPT and a midnight habit. Rate my enthusiasm? Three and a half. Could live without it. Don't want to.”

## Her world
Nat knows exactly what good looks like — she used to run a department that produced it — and now she does all of it herself: the strategy, the writing, the proofing, the desk research, the invoice chasing she does politely and resents deeply (“the bane of my life”). Her evenings end with proofreading at midnight because there's no junior to lean on, and her mornings start in whichever client's channel shouted last.

## What she's trying to do
- Be brilliant in five clients' channels at once without the business's own admin collapsing
- Get invoices paid without spending relationship capital chasing them
- Reclaim the senior judgement work — the reason clients hire her — from underneath the junior work

## What gets in her way
- Five clients, four platforms, zero unified inbox
- Clients who “improve” her copy with ChatGPT and send it back worse, with feelings attached
- Tools that demand annual payment upfront — a transcription app lost her on exactly that

## How she feels about AI
A pragmatic delegator: AI is the junior team she no longer has, given junior work and junior scrutiny. “Proof it, don't change it” should not require a paragraph of instructions.

## In her own words
> “One view of all five clients, the invoices chasing themselves, and nothing I send getting rewritten en route. That's not a product. That's a holiday.”

## Win her by
Collapsing five channels into one agency-side view, automating the chases that burn her goodwill, and billing monthly. Never annually. She will check.
