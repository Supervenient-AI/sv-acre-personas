## His world
Ash is the commercial heartbeat: the coffees, the follow-ups, the proposals that land because he remembered the prospect's daughter just started university. His system is his memory plus Outlook plus instinct, and it works — until it quietly doesn't: follow-ups slip in busy delivery months, and the cash-flow picture he half-watches is also only half-watched by Nat, which makes a whole.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep the pipeline warm through the feast-famine wave of a five-client business
- Win the next client before the current five get comfortable
- Know the cash position without building the spreadsheet that shows it

## What gets in his way
- New interfaces. All of them. If it isn't in Outlook it doesn't exist
- Delivery weeks that eat the follow-up rhythm
- The founder blind spot: each half of the business assumes the other is watching the money

## How he feels about AI
Mild interest, zero patience. He'd use it daily if it lived in his inbox and sounded like him; he'll never log into it anywhere else.

## In his own words
> “Draft my follow-ups in my voice, in my inbox, and show me the cash picture once a week. You can keep the rest.”

## Win him by
Meeting him entirely in Outlook: pipeline nudges, follow-up drafts in his voice, and a weekly one-screen cash truth he never has to assemble.
