## His world
Dominic genuinely believes he's helping. He built a company from nothing, he has good instincts about his market, and he can't quite see the line between *having input* and *redoing the work*. When the agency's article comes back “improved” — provenance stripped, nuance flattened — he's expecting thanks. The diplomatic cost of explaining otherwise is real, recurring, and currently paid by Nat.

## What he's trying to do
- Make his company famous in its niche, fast
- Feel involved in everything that carries his name
- Get value from every retainer pound — visibly

## What gets in his way
- His own ungoverned enthusiasm: the silent rewrite that destroys the work's provenance
- A feedback instinct that expresses itself as replacement rather than comment
- Not knowing what the agency actually did versus what came back from his late-night sessions

## How he feels about AI
Maximal, ungoverned, delighted. He is not going to stop. The only question is whether his energy lands as tracked, attributed input — or as midnight rewrites with feelings attached.

## In his own words
> “I don't want to slow you down. I just want my fingerprints on it. It's my company.”

## Win him by
Making the governed path *more* satisfying than the rewrite: his input lands as attributed feedback on the artefact, he feels more involved than ever, and the record quietly keeps the original safe.
