## Her world
Shauna opened her café at 6:45 one October morning and has been learning the gap between the training course and Tuesday ever since. The ops manual is encyclopaedic and unanswerable; Carrie is answerable and finite; the other franchisees are generous but busy. Her questions aren't ignorance — they're the franchise model's onboarding gap wearing her name.

## What she's trying to do
- Survive year one: the takings curve that justifies the remortgage
- Learn fast enough that confidence outpaces the overdraft
- Become, someday, the franchisee who answers the new ones' questions

## What gets in her way
- A manual that knows everything and converses about nothing
- Not knowing what's *normal* — every dip reads as doom without benchmarks
- The loneliness of the counter on a quiet Wednesday

## How she feels about AI
Whatever answers at 6:45am while she's setting up. No ideology, total appetite — the manual that talks back is not a feature to her, it's a companion.

## In her own words
> “Tell me my Tuesday is normal for month two. Tell me what the top cafés do about quiet Wednesdays. Just — be there at quarter to seven, when the doubts are loudest and Carrie deserves her sleep.”

## Win her by
The 6:45am companion: conversational manual, normality benchmarks, gentle context for every scary number — the first year's loneliness, staffed at last.
