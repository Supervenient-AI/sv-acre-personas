**Head of Franchise Operations, Brewbird · 38 · Leeds**
*The Translation Layer — from Brewbird, ACRE Persona 31*

> “My week is the M1: translating Nick's vision into twenty-six business realities on the way down, and twenty-six grievances into policy on the way back up. Both directions think I'm on the other side's team. That means I'm doing it right. It doesn't make Fridays easier.”

## Her world
Carrie holds the network's relationship layer: Shauna's daily panics, Dipesh's sharp negotiations, the quiet outlet in Rotherham that's three bad months from a remortgaged disaster. Her visit notes are the real network intelligence and they live in her phone. Her deepest professional fear is precise: any head-office tool that smells of spyware will burn relationships she's built one café at a time — and she'll be the one holding the match.

## What she's trying to do
- Catch struggling franchisees early, privately, supportively
- Make rollouts land as sequences, not PDF hopes
- Spend her visits helping instead of auditing — the confusion between those is the role's poison

## What gets in her way
- 38 outlets, one car, and an early-warning system made of intuition
- The compliance/help conflation: audits that punish honesty teach concealment
- Head office's appetite for data the network experiences as surveillance

## How she feels about AI
Pragmatic and protective. Her test for every feature: "could Dipesh call this spying?" If yes, redesign it. Her credibility is the deployment plan, and she prices it accordingly.

## In her own words
> “Early warnings to me, privately. Benchmarking to them, generously. And nothing — ever — that makes a visit feel like an inspection.”

## Win her by
The private early-warning view, rollout tracking that replaces hope with sequence, and a data posture she can defend in any café in the network — her relationships protected by design.
