# Nick Sandry
**Founder & MD, Brewbird Coffee · 45 · Sheffield**
*The Brand's Author — from Brewbird, ACRE Persona 31*

> “I can taste when a café's grinder is two days out of calibration, and I notice every wrong font on every A-board in the network. What I can't do is *make* anyone fix either — twenty-six franchisees own their own businesses, which was the whole idea, and also the whole problem.”

## His world
Nick built something he now governs only by persuasion: the brand is his, the cafés aren't. Every mandate costs franchisees money and returns as resentment; every inconsistency costs the brand equity he spent sixteen years building. He knows the network's best ideas are out in the outlets — Dipesh's loyalty-card hack, a Doncaster breakfast menu — and that head office's machinery for finding them is an annual conference and anecdote.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep 38 cafés feeling like one brand without ruling like a chain
- Find and spread the network's local genius
- Hear the network's truth before it becomes the secret WhatsApp group's consensus

## What gets in his way
- Authority he contractually doesn't have and temperamentally keeps reaching for
- Data flowing up reluctantly, padded, late
- His own rollout instincts — mandate-shaped in a consent-shaped world

## How he feels about AI
Enthusiastic about insight, naive about adoption. The EPOS data could answer half his questions; the network's trust decides whether he ever gets to ask.

## In his own words
> “Make the consistency argument with numbers instead of my taste buds, and find me the Doncaster ideas before the conference does. Just — apparently — don't let me mandate it.”

## Win him by
Evidence over edict: benchmarking that makes the brand case empirically, innovation-spotting from the EPOS truth — and adoption mechanics that route around his mandate reflex.
