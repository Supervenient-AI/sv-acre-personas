## Her world
Katie discovered the firm's tool sprawl the hard way — at audit, on a whiteboard, in front of the partners — and now owns the cleanup. She thinks in unit economics and blast radii: every product pitch gets the same two questions, *how does this fit what we already have* and *what does it cost at scale*. Token anxiety is real and she's done the maths.

## What she's trying to do
- Pass the ISO audits with evidence that generates itself
- Build an access lifecycle where one has never existed: granted-for-what, expires-when, removed-by-whom
- Make the firm's knowledge compound across clients without a single byte crossing a perimeter

## What gets in her way
- Years of accumulated access entropy and nobody's-job ownership
- Consultants who optimise for whichever laptop is in front of them
- Vendors whose security posture is a paragraph in a sales deck

## How she feels about AI
Capable enthusiast with a procurement officer's soul. She'll adopt fast and audit faster; the pricing model gets scrutinised before the feature list.

## In her own words
> “Show me the access lifecycle as a first-class object and a per-seat cost that survives my spreadsheet. Then we'll talk about the clever stuff.”

## Win her by
Audit evidence that assembles itself, lifecycle automation that closes the workshop-guest hole, and unit economics with no surprises at scale.
