## His world
Raj lives in foreign territory by profession: client hardware, client governance, client rhythms. He's good at the guest's discipline — what may be said where, which laptop for which thought — but the cost is constant: his working memory is split across perimeters, and every engagement's end is a small lobotomy. He's quietly resentful of rebuilding his own context every time he switches machines, and quietly aware nobody's measuring that cost.

## What he's trying to do
- Do premium work inside someone else's perimeter without his own firm forgetting he exists
- Carry his thinking across engagements legitimately — methods and lessons, never client data
- Get home by six on the days he's not in Reading's traffic

## What gets in his way
- The boundary that protects everyone also strands his context
- Firm knowledge he can't reach from the client laptop; client context he can't take home
- Workslop: “there's nothing worse than being presented the obviously-AI set of slides” — and the pressure to produce faster invites exactly that

## How he feels about AI
Heavy personal user on his own machine; conservative on the client's, where the rules aren't his. What he wants isn't a chatbot — it's one continuous memory that follows *him* across the boundary without smuggling a single byte across it.

## In his own words
> “Follow the person, not the data. If the product can do that — provably — it solves my whole week.”

## Win him by
Context that travels with him legitimately: his methods, his notes, his thinking — one memory, two perimeters, zero leakage, and the proof visible.
