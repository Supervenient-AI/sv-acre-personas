# Helen Voss
**Programme Owner (client-side) · 44**
*The Sponsor in the Building — from Northbeam, ACRE Persona 6*

> “Every time the consultants stumble, I pay for it — in my building, with my credibility. They go home to their firm. I work here.”

## Her world
Helen bought the consultants to move faster than her own organisation allows, and now spends her credibility brokering between them and the antibodies: IT tickets, security reviews, procurement renewals. She wants the pod to feel like *her* team; her employer's systems treat them as visitors with expiry dates. Every consultant rotation costs her continuity she's personally promised her steering committee.

## What she's trying to do
- Land the programme — her next role depends on it
- Keep the pod productive inside a perimeter she doesn't control either
- Convert consultant knowledge into her organisation's capability before the engagement ends

## What gets in her way
- Consultant rotation resetting context she's paying premium rates to accumulate
- IT and security processes that treat her programme's urgency as everyone's optional
- Being accountable for a pod whose tools and methods she can't see into

## How she feels about AI
Governed by a policy she didn't write. Personally pragmatic: if the consultants' tooling makes the pod continuous and never creates a ticket she has to explain upward, she's for it.

## In her own words
> “Arrive briefed, stay continuous, leave capability behind. That's what I think I'm buying. Make it true.”

## Win her by
Pod continuity through rotation, zero IT surprises, and visible knowledge transfer — the consultants getting better at her business month by month, demonstrably.
