# Vern Olusoga
**Staging & Structures Contractor · 47 · Cardiff**
*The Load-Bearing Supplier — from Hern & Salt, ACRE Persona 40*

Hern & Salt do both. That's why their stage goes up even in the bad-weather years — loyalty is just logistics with a memory.”

## His world
Vern's summer is a routing problem: crews, trucks and steel sequenced across thirty events whose build weeks collide by design. His loyalty economy is explicit — pay on time, plan straight, change early — and Hern & Salt's decade of clean dealings buys them the build-week phone answer that money can't. His paperwork burden (RAMS, sign-offs, wind-loading docs per event) grows with every season's regulation.

## What he's trying to do
- Route the summer: every event built safe, on time, crews home intact
- Reward the straight planners — his calendar is his honest opinion of every client
- Get the paperwork flowing without portals: he will never log into thirty event systems

## What gets in his way
- Build schedules that are hopes wearing Gantt clothes
- Changes reaching him after his trucks have rolled
- Thirty events' RAMS and sign-off processes, each bespoke, none his

## How he feels about AI
Indifferent; his rigging calcs are his own and staying that way. But a build schedule that's *real*, changes that arrive before the trucks, and paperwork that flows by email — that's just a client worth keeping.

## In his own words
> “Real dates, early changes, paperwork by email, money on time. Do those four and you're top of the diary. There is no fifth thing.”

## Win him by
Making Hern & Salt his easiest client: live schedule truth, change alerts before trucks roll, RAMS flowing portal-free — the loyalty economy, fed by the product without ever showing it to him.
