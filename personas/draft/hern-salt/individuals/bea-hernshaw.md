# Bea Hernshaw
**Founder & Festival Director, Hern & Salt · 43 · Frome**
*The Licence and the Dream — from Hern & Salt, ACRE Persona 40*

> “Wildhern started as 800 people in a field with a generator. Now it's 15,000 tickets, a council licence, a bank facility and a farmer's goodwill renewed annually over kitchen tea. The magic has to stay handmade. The paperwork, dear God, does not.”

## Her world
Bea holds the festival's two truths at once: it's a piece of culture made by hand, and it's a regulated mass-gathering that exists at the council's annual pleasure. Her year is a sine wave — the off-season's paperwork archaeology, the spring's assembly, the six-week inflation, the 120-hour show, the dissolution that deletes the organisation's memory. Every February she rebuilds knowledge last July already paid for.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the licence: the SAG pack impeccable, the relationship cumulative
- Keep the magic: nothing about Wildhern may ever feel produced-by-machine
- Keep the core four: the August burnout trough is where festivals quietly die

## What gets in her way
- Pulse amnesia: the debrief written exhausted, the lessons evaporating by spring
- The ESP as a Word-document lineage instead of a living record
- Cash flow shaped like a sine wave with a weather clause

## How she feels about AI
Open wherever it buys back February evenings; immovable on the texture of the thing itself. The SAG pack assembling itself from a record that's already true — that's not automation to her, that's the off-season returned.

## In her own words
> “Bank everything we learn in July and hand it back to me in February. The festival's memory shouldn't dissolve with the festival.”

## Win her by
Memory across the trough: decisions, supplier scores and site lessons banked and resurfaced at the matching moment — plus an ESP that assembles its own evidence. The magic stays hers.
