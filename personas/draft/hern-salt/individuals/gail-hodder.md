# Gail Hodder
**Seasonal Event Ops Manager · 35 · Glastonbury (town)**
*The Returning Pulse — from Hern & Salt, ACRE Persona 40*

> “Two hundred and fifty volunteers, and I can tell you which thirty per cent won't show — by vibe, by signup date, by whether they asked about the lineup before the shifts. That maths lives in my gut. Every spring Bea and I do the availability dance, and every spring the maths nearly doesn't come back.”

## Her world
Gail inflates the festival's human layer in eight weeks: recruit, confirm, brief, rota, and then — race weekend — absorb the 30% evaporation with over-recruitment maths she's never written down. Her contract ends in August; her knowledge ends with it, unless she returns, which is annually uncertain. She is the seasonal-key-staff condition in person: the machine pretends she's permanent because the alternative is admitting the rebuild.

## What she's trying to do
- Staff the festival's gates, campsites and crowds with humans who actually arrive
- Make onboarding work on a phone in four minutes — the PDF era must end
- Get her folklore banked: the no-show maths, the returner gems, the bin-hiders list

## What gets in her way
- Confirmation decay: yeses in March becoming silence in July
- Briefing documents read by 40% and retained by fewer
- Her own seasonality: the knowledge resets if she doesn't return

## How she feels about AI
Practical: 250 humans on spreadsheets is her June, and predictive no-show maths is just her gut, formalised. She'd adopt mid-season if it worked on a phone in a field.

## In her own words
> “Four-minute phone onboarding, confirmation pulses that detect the fade, and over-recruitment by data instead of by dread. I've been the algorithm for ten summers. I'd like to supervise it instead.”

## Win her by
The volunteer pipeline: phone-native onboarding, decay detection, no-show prediction — her gut promoted to model, her folklore surviving the winters she doesn't come back.
