# Jules Hartigan
**Founder, Bowline Search · 42 · Glasgow**
*The Portfolio Holder — from Bowline, ACRE Persona 36*

> “AI flooded my market with spam — a thousand auto-generated approaches a week hitting the same candidates. Wonderful news, honestly. We never played the volume game. Now volume is worthless and considered is priceless, and considered is the only thing we ever sold.”

## Her world
Jules runs a business made of confidences: candidate truths and client truths held apart by professional honour and consultant memory. Her firm's knowledge — market maps, salary truths, who's quietly looking — lives in her consultants' heads and walks out at 6pm daily, occasionally forever (the desk *is* the consultant; she's lost two desks that way). The slop-flood is her differentiation moment if she can arm the craft side.

## What she's trying to do
- Win on considered: quality submissions in a market drowning in spray
- Make the firm's knowledge survive consultant churn — without making consultants feel mined
- Keep the candidate-respect culture as she scales past her own line of sight

## What gets in her way
- Disclosure discipline running on vigilance instead of architecture
- The ATS recording half of what matters and none of what's sensitive
- Counter-offers ambushing deals her consultants thought were closed

## How she feels about AI
Strategically sharp: sourcing-spam has devalued her competitors' game, and tools that weaponise *quality* — disclosure architecture, knowledge continuity, offer intelligence — are the ones she'll fund.

## In her own words
> “Make the wrong fact structurally unable to cross the desk, and make the firm's memory outlast any consultant's notice period. Those two features are the business, insured.”

## Win her by
Disclosure-as-architecture and consultant-knowledge continuity with attribution — the boutique's honour codified, and its biggest fragility retired.
