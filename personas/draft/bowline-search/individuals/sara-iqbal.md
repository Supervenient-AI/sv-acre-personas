# Dr Sara Iqbal
**Candidate — Senior Data Scientist · 34 · Edinburgh**
*The Career In Transit — from Bowline, ACRE Persona 36*

> “My field is small enough that everyone knows everyone. If my employer learns I'm looking before I'm ready, that's not awkward — that's eight years of standing repriced overnight. So yes, I'm careful. I build recommendation systems for a living. I know exactly how careless data gets.”

## Her world
Sara is being processed by worse versions of the technology she builds: scraped, scored and spammed by recruiters' automation that addresses her as "a great fit for an exciting opportunity." Callum earned her trust the old way — he told her *not* to apply for something once. She extends that trust precisely as far as his confidence discipline holds, and not one message further.

## What she's trying to do
- Make the right move: scope, team and progression, with compensation that reflects the market she can't openly test
- Stay undiscovered until the moment of her choosing
- Be treated as a senior professional, not pipeline inventory

## What gets in her way
- A small field's gossip physics
- Processes that go silent for a fortnight (which her anxiety reads as rejection and her experience reads as chaos)
- The counter-offer she already knows her employer will make, and the conversation it will require

## How she feels about AI
Expert and unillusioned: she can tell slop from service in one message. A candidate experience that's responsive, honest about the role's real politics, and visibly confidence-disciplined would be — she'd note professionally — a well-designed system.

## In her own words
> “Tell me the truth about the role, keep my secret like it's your own, and never make me feel like a row in someone's pipeline. That's the entire premium service. It's strange that it's rare.”

## Win her by
Confidence discipline made visible, honest calibration, responsive choreography — the candidate surface as proof that the agency's craft survives its tooling.
