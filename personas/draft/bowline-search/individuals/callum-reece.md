## His world
Callum's day is synchronisation and disclosure: aligning two diaries, extracting feedback before it goes stale, calibrating what each side learns about the other — forty times a day, at speed, on memory. He already uses AI for sourcing and outreach drafts; what he guards is the relationship layer, because in recruitment the consultant's network is the consultant's career.

## What he's trying to do
- Bill well: the desk's numbers are his trajectory
- Keep candidates feeling like humans mid-career-leap — Jules's culture, sincerely held
- Never, ever let the wrong fact cross the knot

## What gets in his way
- Feedback latency killing deals to faster processes
- The double ledger's fidelity depending on his Friday discipline
- Counter-offers he saw coming and couldn't evidence

## How he feels about AI
Uses it daily at the edges; suspicious of anything that wants his candidate relationships. The Viktor/Andrei condition, sales edition: externalisation must visibly raise his billing, or it's an extraction he'll quietly starve.

## In his own words
> “Run the choreography, log the disclosures, flag the counter-offer risk — and make my knowledge raise my numbers, with my name on it. Then we're partners.”

## Win him by
Synchronisation automation plus a disclosure ledger that protects him — and knowledge capture framed, honestly, as making the knot more valuable rather than more replaceable.
