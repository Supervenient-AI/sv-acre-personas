# Martin Kerr
**Engineering Director (client) · 46 · Glasgow**
*The Pressured Buyer — from Bowline, ACRE Persona 36*

> “Eight open heads, a delivery date, and a CEO who asks about both weekly. My internal talent team sends me AI-screened longlists. Bowline sends me three people who can all do the job. Guess which invoice I defend at budget time.”

## His world
Martin buys outcomes: appointable people, fast, who stay. The machinery between him and that outcome — agency terms, internal talent-team politics, scheduling friction, offer approvals — is overhead he tolerates in proportion to results. His feedback latency is the process's biggest killable delay, and he doesn't know it: from his side, a three-day debrief delay is just a busy week.

## What he's trying to do
- Fill eight roles before the roadmap slips its quarter
- Keep the hires he makes — every leaver re-runs the whole expensive loop
- Spend his interview hours only on people worth them

## What gets in his way
- His own diary: feedback trapped in it, candidates lost to faster processes
- Counter-offers landing on winners he'd mentally onboarded
- The internal talent team's volume machinery competing with Bowline's craft

## How he feels about AI
Outcome-agnostic: his own team's screening AI produces longlists he distrusts; Bowline's shortlists interview well, whatever produced them. He judges the dish, not the kitchen.

## In his own words
> “Three appointable people, feedback turned round in hours not days — mine included, fine, point taken — and an offer process that doesn't lose the winner. Invoice me.”

## Win him by
Velocity he can feel: his own feedback extracted painlessly within hours, interview logistics that just happen, and counter-offer risk flagged before the offer letter — the craft agency, accelerated.
