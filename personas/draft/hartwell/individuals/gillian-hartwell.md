# Gillian Hartwell
**Senior Partner, Hartwell & Co Conveyancing · 52 · Nottingham**
*The Careful Link — from Hartwell, ACRE Persona 20*

> “I've seen every way a chain dies. Most of them start with somebody, somewhere, not saying something for three weeks. None of them start with a solicitor being too thorough.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|

| Channels | The case management system (the firm's third), email at industrial volume, the phone her assistants shield |

## Her world
Gillian's files are legally intricate and procedurally absurd: the actual law takes hours; the chasing, waiting and answering "any update?" takes weeks. Her assistants spend half their day relaying stale statuses; the chains her transactions sit in are invisible beyond the next link; and the one thing she will never do — skip a search, soften an enquiry — is the one thing chain pressure constantly demands.

## What she's trying to do
- Complete safely: her clients' life savings move through her account
- Free her staff from being a call centre for a status nobody can actually see
- Detect the silent stall — the other side's three-week nothing — in days

## What gets in her way
- A chain whose true state is unknowable from any seat in it
- Five parties chasing the same redemption statement through each other
- Fee pressure that punishes the thoroughness her insurance requires

## How she feels about AI
Deeply sceptical of anything touching legal judgement — and quietly desperate for anything that kills the status-call tax. The line is bright: process automation welcome; an enquiry reply drafted without her, never.

## In her own words
> “Show every party their legitimate slice of the truth, automatically — and keep your hands off the law.”

## Win her by
The shared status layer: client-grade reassurance for buyers, professional-grade state for solicitors, stall detection for her — and a hard wall around the legal work itself.
