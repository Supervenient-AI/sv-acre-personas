## His world
Kyle is the chain's customer-service layer: buyers, sellers, agents and the other side all ring him for a status that no one — including him — actually holds. He's good at the soothing ("nothing's wrong, the council search just takes six weeks") and knows the soothing is a system failure wearing his voice. Every hour on the phone is an hour not spent on the file work that counts toward his qualification.

## What he's trying to do
- Qualify: the CLC course, the portfolio of real legal work, the way up
- Give callers real answers instead of warm guesses
- Be noticed for file craft, not phone manner

## What gets in his way
- A status that updates by archaeology
- Being cheap enough to absorb the chain's information failure indefinitely
- Days that end with the legal work untouched and the phone log full

## How he feels about AI
Please, today. He's the rare persona whom automation straightforwardly liberates: every self-answered "any update?" is an hour returned to the work that builds his career — and he knows it.

## In his own words
> “Let the status answer itself and give me the files. I didn't take night classes to read a spreadsheet down a phone.”

## Win him by
Self-serve status that empties his call log — and a visible record of the legal work he's freed to do, feeding straight into his qualification portfolio.
