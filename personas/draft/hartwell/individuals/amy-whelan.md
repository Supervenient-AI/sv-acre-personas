# Amy Whelan
**First-Time Buyer · 31 · Beeston**
*The Anxious Civilian — from Hartwell, ACRE Persona 20*

> “Everyone keeps saying ‘these things take time.' My savings, my flat deposit refund, and a baby due in March are inside ‘these things.' Tell me what's actually happening or my brain will invent something worse.”

## At a glance
| | |

## Her world
Amy has handed her life savings into a process that communicates like a Victorian institution: weeks of nothing, then a request for a document with a deadline. She doesn't know what a redemption statement is, why the council takes six weeks, or whether silence is normal — and the not-knowing is the cruelty. She's not demanding speed. She's begging for *weather reports*.

## What she's trying to do
- Get the keys before the baby, or at least know which is coming first
- Understand enough to stop catastrophising at 2am
- Not be "that client who keeps ringing" — a fear that keeps her uninformed

## What gets in her way
- Total information asymmetry: she sees nothing, risks everything
- A chain whose stalls are invisible even to her own solicitor
- Plain-English explanations existing nowhere in the process

## How she feels about AI
Irrelevant as a concept — she wants to know what's happening, in human words, from something that won't make her feel stupid for asking.

## In her own words
> “‘Waiting on the council's search — typical wait three more weeks — nothing is wrong.' That sentence would have saved me a month of sleep.”

## Win her by
The live, honest, plain-English picture: what stage, what's normal, what (rarely) needs her — converting dread into mere impatience, which is all a chain can honestly offer.
