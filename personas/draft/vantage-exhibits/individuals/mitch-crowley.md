# Mitch Crowley
**Workshop Foreman & Install Crew Chief, Vantage Exhibits · 51 · Nuneaton**
*The 2am Sovereign — from Vantage, ACRE Persona 41*

> “Thirty years of installs on three continents. I can level a stand on a sloping floor by eye and talk a forklift out of a Catalan venue crew with no shared language. At 2am in hall 8, every question the project didn't answer in week three gets answered by me, with whatever's in the van. I'm very good at it. I'd rather not be needed for it.”

## His world
Mitch is where the firm's optimism meets physics. His week-three verdicts ("that cantilever won't survive the breakdown crew") are the quality system; his 2am improvisations are the cost of every verdict not sought. The reusable stock inventory and the venue folklore — two of the firm's most valuable datasets — exist behind his eyes, and his knees have opinions about how many more seasons that arrangement has.

## What he's trying to do
- Land every install by 9am, crew intact, client none the wiser about the night
- Kill the missing 10%: deferred decisions dragged into daylight in week three
- Bank the folklore — his name on it — before the knees retire him to the workshop

## What gets in his way
- Drawings that are 90% resolved meeting venues that demand 100%
- The job bag in the van while the answer's needed at the rig point
- Warehouse amnesia: rebuilding what the racking already holds

## How he feels about AI
None — until the phone in his pocket answers the 2am question (which drawing, which rule, which crate). Then it's part of the toolkit, like the laser level: judged purely on whether it's right in the dark.

## In his own words
> “Everything in my pocket at 2am: current drawings, venue rules, what's in which crate, who to ring. Get me that and I'll give you the folklore — fair trade.”

## Win him by
The crew-phone surface (seconds-to-answer, offline-tolerant, one-handed) and credited folklore capture — the 2am sovereign given a kingdom that isn't held up by his knees.
