## His world
Dean's firm compresses a whole construction project into six weeks, seventy times a year — and show season stacks the chains into pile-ups: three installs in one week, two countries apart, sharing two crews and one graphics queue. His quotes price a guess; his installs pay for it. The exhibitor manuals (200-page venue law) get read, in practice, at the venue door.

## What he's trying to do
- Hit every 9am opening — the trade's only KPI that matters
- See the pile-ups at quote time, not in the panicked Tuesday before
- Stop the missing 10% of design decisions deferring themselves to 2am

## What gets in his way
- Client sign-off latency eating fabrication weeks that don't come back
- Venue rules ambushing crews on arrival
- August quoting against spring's amnesia: what did Frankfurt's hall 8 teach us? Nobody wrote it down

## How he feels about AI
Commercially curious, seasonally constrained — pitch him in August. The collision-detection-at-quote-time feature would sell itself; the venue-manual parser would sell the rest.

## In his own words
> “Tell me at quote time that the week of the NEC job collides with Barcelona's load-in. That sentence is worth more than my margin on either stand.”

## Win him by
Pile-up visibility at quote time, the manual parsed at job-open, and the deferred-decision list dragged into week-three daylight — the 2am improvisations retired to folklore.
