## Her world
Elena buys certainty in an uncertain trade: the stand must be perfect at 9am in front of her prospects, her team and her CEO. Between her and that moment stands her own organisation's sign-off latency — a committee deliberating in days while fabrication windows close in hours — and she's never once seen that cost computed until it arrived as overtime, risk or a 2am text she wasn't meant to see.

## What she's trying to do
- Open three flawless stands a year and convert them to pipeline
- Get her committee's opinions in early — especially the CEO's week-four specials
- Look composed upward while the chaos stays invisible downward

## What gets in her way
- Internal sign-off latency she can't see the price of
- The CEO render-review bomb, structurally unannounced
- Show-week stress arriving as surprises that were six weeks old

## How she feels about AI
Fluent, deadline-driven, mechanism-agnostic. The sign-off cost meter — "approval today = full fabrication; Friday = overtime; Monday = 2am risk" — would change her internal conversations overnight.

## In her own words
> “Show me what my company's delays cost while they're still cheap. I can move my committee — I've just never had the invoice in advance.”

## Win her by
The latency meter as a shared dashboard: her delays priced in real time, her CEO's late opinions converted to costed options — the client transformed from the risk into the ally.
