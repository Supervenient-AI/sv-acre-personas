# Sasha Reeve
**Senior Designer, Vantage Exhibits · 33 · Coventry**
*The Dream Seller — from Vantage, ACRE Persona 41*

> “My render wins the pitch. Then the venue says the rig point doesn't exist, the client's CEO wants the logo bigger in week four, and Mitch tells me my cantilever won't survive the breakdown crew. I'd rather fight those battles at concept stage — but nobody brings the constraints to the sketchbook.”

## Her world
Sasha designs in a constraint field she can only partly see: venue rigging law, build-window physics, breakdown-crew brutality, the client's internal politics — all discoverable, none delivered to her design environment at concept stage. Her fights with Mitch are the firm's quality engine and she knows it; her fights with late CEO opinions are just margin bleeding in render form.

## What she's trying to do
- Win pitches with dreams that survive contact with the workshop
- Get the constraints — venue rules, buildability, budget truth — into the sketch phase
- Build a portfolio that gets Vantage (and her) the bigger international jobs

## What gets in her way
- Venue law discovered after the concept's sold
- "Locked" designs unlocked by week-four stakeholders
- Sign-off rounds absorbed silently instead of tracked and priced

## How she feels about AI
Already uses the render tools' AI; what she wants is constraint-aware design — the manual's rules and Mitch's buildability folklore surfacing *in* her environment, at concept stage, before the dream commits to the impossible.

## In her own words
> “Put hall 8's rigging rules and Mitch's greatest hits in my design environment. The dream gets better when it knows the rules early — constraint is a material.”

## Win her by
Constraints at concept stage and sign-off rounds tracked — the dream-to-plywood translation made collaborative instead of corrective.
