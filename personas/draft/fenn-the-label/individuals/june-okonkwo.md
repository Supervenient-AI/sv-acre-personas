# June Okonkwo
**Freelance Pattern Cutter · 58 · Tottenham**
*The Fit Memory — from Fenn the Label, ACRE Persona 12*

> “The block remembers everything I know. The trouble is, nobody can read a block but me — and I'm not going to cut forever.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 58 |
| Lives | Tottenham — widowed, two grown sons, a sewing room that predates the term “home office” |
| Background | Thirty years cutting patterns for everyone from Savile Row diffusion lines to market-stall heroes |
| Work | Freelance pattern cutting; Fenn's blocks are hers — which means Fenn's *fit* is hers |
| Method | Paper, shears, a 1980s pattern hook, annotations in a hand like calligraphy |
| Channels | In person, by appointment; the phone if she must |
| Devices | None she'd call that. A kettle |

## Her world
June is the reason Fenn's customers say the trousers “just fit”. Her blocks encode three decades of knowing what cloth does on bodies — “this fabric needs half a centimetre more ease” is a fact she feels before she measures. None of it is written anywhere except in the patterns themselves, and the patterns speak only to her. She has seen labels die of losing their cutter; she'd rather Fenn didn't.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the fit consistent across seasons, fabrics and the grading no one respects enough
- Pass something on — she's begun to think about who comes after, which is new
- Work at her pace, in her room, on paper, until she chooses otherwise

## What gets in her way
- Designers who think a size 10 toile grades itself to an 18
- Decisions made about cloth without asking what the cloth will do
- Being re-asked things she settled, definitively, in 2024

## How she feels about AI
Serene indifference — she won't change a single habit. But she's not against the *workshop* changing around her: if her annotations get photographed, kept and honoured, that's just good manners with better filing.

## In her own words
> “Write down what I tell you. That's all the technology this needs. You lot just never write it down.”

## Win her by
Changing nothing at her table while capturing everything that leaves it: her annotations and rulings archived, attributed, never re-asked — the fit memory finally owned by the label as well as the legend.
