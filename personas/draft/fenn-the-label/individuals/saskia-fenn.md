## Her world
Saskia's year is two overlapping seasons of converging deadlines: design in London, patterns in Tottenham, samples in the East End, fabric from Milan via an agent, production in Porto — all aimed at delivery windows the industry sets and no one extends. Her brand is her taste; her risk is the gap between an 11pm voice note and a machinist's Tuesday morning, one border and one language away.

## What she's trying to do
- Hit both delivery windows every year without mortgaging the team's sanity
- Keep Catarina's goodwill — the factory's affection is the label's most valuable intangible
- Grow DTC without wholesale stockists feeling like an afterthought

## What gets in her way
- The calendar maths nobody runs until it's run out: a three-day lab-dip delay quietly costing the Porto slot
- Her own native format — voice, idiom, midnight — meeting a factory that needs precision, in writing, in daylight
- The tracker spreadsheet "PROD SS26 REAL FINAL", which is neither real nor final

## How she feels about AI
Uses ChatGPT for copy and grading maths already; would delegate the entire logistics layer if she trusted the picture it showed her. Voice-note-native input or nothing — she will not type into forms.

## In her own words
> “Turn my 11pm voice note into Porto's clear morning instruction, and show me what every delay costs in slot-language. That's the whole company saved, twice a year.”

## Win her by
Calendar maths surfaced continuously ("this approval delay = the May 14th slot") and her voice notes arriving in Porto as structured, translated, confirmable instructions.
