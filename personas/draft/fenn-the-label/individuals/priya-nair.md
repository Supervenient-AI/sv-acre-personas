## Her world
Priya converts ambiguity into manufacturability for a living. Every idiom in Saskia's voice notes, every missing measurement in a tech pack, every approval sleeping in an inbox overnight — she catches them, or they become defects and lost slots months later. She loves the work and resents the part of it that is pure chasing, which is most of it in production season.

## What she's trying to do
- Make Fenn the factory's easiest small label — the only queue priority money can't buy
- Get the tracker to tell the truth without her reconciling it nightly
- Automate the chase, keep the judgement

## What gets in her way
- Approvals that sleep in inboxes while production slots expire
- A tracker fed by hand from four channels in two languages
- Being the only person who knows how close to the edge each season runs

## How she feels about AI
Keen and precise. She wants the chasing, translating and reconciling automated — and the judgement calls (what to compromise, when to escalate) left exactly where they are.

## In her own words
> “Chase the approvals while I sleep. The timezone gap should be where the chasing happens, not where the delay grows.”

## Win her by
Overnight chase automation, a tracker that feeds itself from WhatsApp and email in both languages, and collision warnings early enough to act on calmly.
