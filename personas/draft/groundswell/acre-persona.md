# ACRE Persona 8 — The Enablement Network
### Groundswell Collective — a 3-person nonprofit equipping grassroots campaign groups

## A — Account
**Archetype:** The Capacity-Builder. Groundswell, a three-person nonprofit (plus freelancers, plus a new digital campaigner) that supports activists and community groups to run national campaigns — strategy, media, political engagement. Fully remote. Google Workspace (Drive "a total mess of stuff"), Slack internally — but the groups they serve live on WhatsApp, so WhatsApp it also is. Notion half-adopted, Canva, Zoom, Calendly: a lot going on, not much of it synced. AI posture: enthusiastic (self-rated 4–5) but early (impact so far: 2/5); two of the team did a six-week AI course and joined its WhatsApp community for ongoing help. House policy, informal but real: *use AI freely, but tell each other*, so checking accounts for it. Tool selection runs through an ethics screen — supplier politics and values genuinely move purchasing decisions — and a budget screen: subscription sprawl is a visible line item in a tight budget. Hardest red line: some of their work involves people recounting trauma; those conversations are never transcribed, full stop, until security is beyond doubt.

## C — Constellation
**Definition:** A tiny expert hub serving many even-less-resourced external groups — where the "clients" are volunteer campaigners, the relationships are the methodology, and the goal is to leave capability *behind* rather than retain dependency.

**Cast:** Director, campaign lead, new digital campaigner, occasional freelancers, the campaign groups themselves (volunteers, WhatsApp-native, hugely varied in capacity), funders (reports, pitches, relationships), the board (quarterly papers).

**Topology:** Hub-and-many-spokes, but with deliberately *outward* knowledge flow: the hub teaches templates and journeys to the spokes and wants the spokes to keep running after the hub steps away. A second, upward edge runs to funders, who supply the money and consume the evidence.

**Boundary objects:** The campaign plan, the supporter-email journey (taught from a template every time), the funder report, the board pack, the impact evidence — qualitative, relational, and chronically hard to quantify.

**Failure modes:**
1. Knowledge trapped in three heads while the team grows — "where does someone find stuff?" has no answer
2. Every campaign rebuilt semi-from-scratch despite the journeys being 70% standard
3. Impact going unmeasured because evaluation is the most deferrable task on a tiny team
4. The reporting treadmill (board, funders) eating one to two days a week of the director's time
5. Voice erosion: their work *must* sound human and relational; over-AI'd output is mission damage, not just quality damage

**Conflicting interests:** The groups need hand-holding and human warmth; the org needs leverage and standardisation; funders need legible impact numbers from inherently relational work; the ethics screen narrows the tool options the budget screen has already narrowed.

**What the constellation needs from the AI:** Become the organisational memory ("Notion, but easier"): weekly priorities surfaced without ceremony, knowledge findable by the new hire. Compress the governance treadmill — board reports and funder reports drafted from accumulated knowledge, not reconstructed each cycle. Productise the templates: a campaigner briefs in their campaign, out comes a six-month email journey *they* own. Help quantify qualitative impact. Respect the bright lines absolutely: trauma-adjacent material untouched, the human voice preserved, the values screen passed.

## R — Roles
**Ruth Calder-Jones, 45 — Director (The Relational Engine).** Sheffield, works from the kitchen table with a dramatic dog. Talks her thinking out loud — uses dictation software constantly, "blurts" at ChatGPT and asks it to organise her back. Drafts awkward funder emails with AI and rewrites them to sound like herself; would never send anything unchecked ("never the last draft"). Did the AI course, thrived for three weeks, got lost in week four — her ceiling isn't curiosity, it's the absence of anyone in-house to ask.
**Maya Osei, 29 — Digital Campaigner (The New Capacity).** Joining from a charity that *banned* AI outright — where, she reports, everyone simply used it in secret. Carries both digital fluency and a sharp sense of how trust breaks when tools are imposed.
**Group-side: Donna Pryor, 52 — Volunteer Campaign Lead (The Spoke That Stays).** Runs a health-justice campaign evenings and weekends from WhatsApp and a shared Gmail. Any tool left with her must work on a phone, cost nothing, and never make her supporters feel processed.
**Funder-side: programme officer at a foundation.** Sympathetic, deadline-bound, numbers-hungry.

## E — Edges
Defining edges: **org ↔ campaign groups** (the sacred edge: high-trust, WhatsApp-native, and it must never feel automated — AI works *behind* this edge, preparing what the humans then deliver warmly); **org ↔ funders** (formal, cyclical, evidence-hungry — the highest-leverage edge to automate); **Ruth ↔ the team's AI use** (the transparency edge: "tell each other" as policy is a product feature waiting to exist); **org ↔ its own future staff** (the knowledge edge: everything currently in heads must land somewhere a new hire can find).
