*The New Capacity — from Groundswell, ACRE Persona 8*

## Her world
Maya arrived carrying two things: digital craft (journeys, analytics, content that converts) and a sharp, lived sense of how trust breaks when tools are imposed on people. Groundswell's house rule — *use AI freely, but tell each other* — is exactly the policy her last employer needed and refused. Now she's onboarding into an organisation whose knowledge lives in two other people's heads.

## What she's trying to do
- Self-serve the org's knowledge instead of queueing behind Ruth's bandwidth
- Upgrade the campaign templates with the digital instincts she was hired for
- Help write the AI norms she wishes she'd had — transparent, values-screened, humane

## What gets in her way
- An undocumented org: warm culture, no map
- Tool decisions double-filtered by ethics and budget (rightly, slowly)
- The newcomer's dilemma: seeing what to fix before having the standing to fix it

## How she feels about AI
Fluent and pro-transparency. She'll champion governed use loudly — and walk from surveillance dressed as collaboration, having seen that movie.

## In her own words
> “Make the AI use visible to the team by default. That one feature is the difference between a tool and a culture.”

## Win her by
Week-one self-service (the org's memory, searchable) and transparency as architecture — her last employer's failure, productised into her new one's strength.
