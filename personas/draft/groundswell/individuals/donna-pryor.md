# Donna Pryor
**Volunteer Campaign Lead · 52 · Rotherham**
*The Spoke That Stays — from Groundswell, ACRE Persona 8*

> “I do this on evenings and weekends because somebody has to. If a tool needs a training session, it's already lost me — I haven't got the evening it would take.”

## Her world
Donna's campaign exists because she refuses to let it not. Groundswell taught her the supporter-journey template and she runs it faithfully from her phone between shifts — but the campaign's entire institutional memory is her: contacts, history, what the MP's office said in March. If she burns out, the campaign dies, and she knows it, which is its own kind of weight.

## What she's trying to do
- Win the campaign — actual changes for actual people
- Keep her sixty supporters feeling like a movement, never like a mailing list
- Hand something on, someday, that doesn't evaporate when she rests

## What gets in her way
- Evenings-only capacity against institutions with office hours
- Tools that assume a desk, a budget or a training session
- The fear that it all lives in her head and her phone

## How she feels about AI
She doesn't think about AI; she thinks about whether the thing works on her phone, costs nothing, and never makes her supporters feel processed. Those are the terms. They're good terms.

## In her own words
> “If it feels like software, my supporters will feel like data. They're not data. They're my neighbours.”

## Win her by
Being invisible: the journey keeps running, the knowledge gets kept somewhere safe, and nothing about it feels like anything except the campaign, working.
