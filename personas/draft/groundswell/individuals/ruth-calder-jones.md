# Ruth Calder-Jones
**Director, Groundswell Collective · 45 · Sheffield**
*The Relational Engine — from Groundswell, ACRE Persona 8*

> “I blurt at ChatGPT and ask it to organise me back. It's the colleague I talk at. But never the last draft — nothing goes out that doesn't sound like us.”

## Her world
Ruth's week is one to two days of reporting treadmill (board papers, funder reports) wrapped around the real work: coaching volunteer campaigners through WhatsApp with the patience of someone who knows trust is the methodology. She did a six-week AI course, thrived for three weeks, got lost in week four — her ceiling isn't curiosity, it's having no one in-house to ask. House rule, hers: use AI freely, but tell each other.

## What she's trying to do
- Get the org's knowledge out of three heads before the team grows past them
- Halve the reporting treadmill without the reports losing her voice
- Leave every campaign group genuinely able to run without her — the whole point

## What gets in her way
- “Where does someone find stuff?” having no answer
- Impact that's real but unquantified, facing funders who need numbers
- A tool budget and an ethics screen that narrow every option twice

## How she feels about AI
Enthusiastic (4–5) with honest humility about impact so far (2). Bright lines, absolute: trauma-adjacent conversations are never transcribed; the human voice survives every draft.

## In her own words
> “Notion, but easier. That's the brief. And it has to pass the values screen before it passes the budget one.”

## Win her by
Becoming her blurt-organiser at org scale: dictation in, institutional memory out, funder reports drafted from what the org already knows — and her voice, intact, on everything.
