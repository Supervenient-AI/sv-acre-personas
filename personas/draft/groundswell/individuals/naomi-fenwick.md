# Naomi Fenwick
**Programme Officer, funding foundation · 41 · London**
*The Sympathetic Gatekeeper — from Groundswell, ACRE Persona 8*

> “I believe in their work. My trustees believe in tables. I spend my life translating between the two, and lately half my grantees' reports are obviously machine-written, which helps nobody.”

## Her world
Naomi is the friendliest gatekeeper a small nonprofit could hope for — and still a gatekeeper. She fights for relational, hard-to-measure work in a room that wants numbers, which means her grantees' reports are her ammunition. A late report leaves her empty-handed; an obviously-AI one insults the work; a great one — on time, in the grantee's voice, with a number she can put in front of trustees — wins everyone another year.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep good grantees funded through evidence their work resists producing
- Get reports she can quote, on dates she can rely on
- Protect the human texture of the work as it passes through her board's filters

## What gets in her way
- Reporting cycles that land mid-campaign for every grantee simultaneously
- Qualitative impact that arrives as anecdote when trustees want arithmetic
- The rising tide of grant-report slop

## How she feels about AI
Agnostic on tools, allergic to their fingerprints. If AI helps a grantee report well and sound like themselves, she'll never know and never mind. If she can tell, it's already failed.

## In her own words
> “Give me the story *and* the number, in their voice, on time. I'll do the rest in the boardroom.”

## Win her by
Never being visible: grantee reports that arrive punctual, authentic and quantified — ammunition, not homework.
