## His world
Sam is the person Donna messages when the council meeting goes sideways at 9pm. He carries each campaign's live state in his thumbs — who's wobbling, which journey's mid-flight, what the local paper said — and rebuilds 70%-standard campaign plans semi-from-scratch because the templates live in old folders and older memories.

## What he's trying to do
- Get campaign state out of his thumbs and into something the team can see
- Make the standard 70% assemble itself so his hours go to the human 30%
- Protect the groups from ever feeling processed — warmth is the deliverable

## What gets in his way
- Every campaign rebuilt from fragments of the last one
- Evenings-and-weekends pacing (the volunteers' clock) colliding with office-hours collaborators
- Evaluation as the most deferrable task on a tiny team

## How he feels about AI
Practical and group-protective. Uses it daily for research and drafts; anything that touches the groups gets triple-checked for tone, and anything that *faces* the groups had better be invisible.

## In his own words
> “Brief in the campaign, get a six-month journey out — one the group owns. That would change what three people can do.”

## Win him by
Productising the templates without flattening them, and making campaign state ambient — visible to Ruth and Maya without one extra message from his phone.
