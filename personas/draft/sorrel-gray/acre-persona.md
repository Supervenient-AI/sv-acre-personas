# ACRE Persona 9 — The Designer-Hub Fit-Out
### Sorrel & Gray — a 4-person interior design studio coordinating trades, suppliers and an anxious client through a residential project

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #1 (Family 1, hub-and-spoke); a stated-wedge gap, not yet interview-grounded.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Taste-Led Coordinator
**Instance:** Sorrel & Gray, Bath. Principal designer plus a senior designer, a junior and a part-time studio manager. £600k turnover, fees part fixed, part procurement margin. Three to six live projects at any time, each 6–14 months.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Everything crosses the boundary: contractors, joiners, decorators, fabric houses, antique dealers, installers — none employed, all essential. The studio sells *taste plus coordination* and only charges visibly for the taste. |
| Tooling | Studio: Mac, InDesign, Pinterest boards, a procurement spreadsheet of terrifying importance. Outward: email, phone, WhatsApp with trades, supplier portals that ignore them. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The principal — chasing fabric mills in Italy at 7am and joiners at 7pm. Lead-time arithmetic is done in her head, badly, constantly. |
| Buying behaviour | Buys beautiful software impulsively, abandons it quietly. Will pay for anything that prevents one missed lead time, because one missed lead time costs more than a year of subscriptions. |
| What failure costs | Physical-goods failure: a sofa stuck in a warehouse, a discontinued fabric discovered at order time, an install day with no rug. Every failure lands in the client's actual home, in front of them. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A small design studio as hub between a private client and a long tail of trades and physical-goods suppliers, where the deliverable is a finished room and the critical path runs through objects that ship, get delayed, arrive damaged, or get discontinued.

**Cast:**
- **Imogen Sorrel, 47** — principal designer, the hub (Bath)
- **Caro Lindsey, 38** — the client; renovating a Georgian townhouse, living in it throughout
- **Mick Tully, 55** — main contractor (builder), runs his own sub-constellation of trades
- **Józef Wrona, 44** — bespoke joiner, six-week lead times, allergic to changed drawings
- **Plus:** the studio team, a decorator, a curtain-maker, fabric houses (London/Italy), antique dealers, a two-man install crew

**External immovable nodes:**
- **Supplier lead times** — the mill weaves when the mill weaves; 14 weeks means 14 weeks and no email changes it
- **Building control / listed-building officers** — for structural and heritage work, gates with their own calendar
- **The client's mortgage/funds release schedule** — invisible until a stage payment stalls everything

**Topology:** Hub-and-spoke with a *physical critical path*: unlike a creative sprint, dependencies here are objects in vans. Imogen routes everything; Mick runs a sub-hub of trades she can't see into; the client has a direct line to both and uses it, asymmetrically.

**Lifecycle:**
- *Assembly:* concept and scheme design — the romance phase; trust at maximum, budget optimism too.
- *Procurement (the hidden middle):* orders placed against lead times; errors made now surface months later with no undo.
- *Midpoint reset:* the client lives in the dust. Around month four, fatigue converts to scope anxiety ("can we just simplify the snug?") right as the longest-lead items become uncancellable.
- *Install crunch:* everything converges on a two-week window — trades finishing, deliveries landing, snagging exploding. One late item un-sequences twenty people.
- *Dissolution & reveal:* photography, a gift, an invoice dispute risk; then the studio's memory of 200 supplier interactions evaporates.
- *Trust curve:* presumed high (the portfolio sold it); spent on every surprise cost and slipped week; the client's Instagram-fed expectations are a counterparty no one ever meets.

**Cadence:** Weekly client meetings early, then bursty procurement silence (which the client experiences as neglect), then daily chaos at install. Trades work by phone and WhatsApp photo; suppliers by formal email and portal.

**Boundary objects:** The scheme board (emotion to the client, specification to nobody), the procurement schedule (the real system of record, in a spreadsheet), drawings (gospel to Józef, suggestions to Mick), the lead-time list, the snagging list.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** Entirely on Imogen — design intent to trade reality, lead times to site programme, budget truth to client comfort. The studio manager holds fragments; nobody else can sequence the whole.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Imogen holds the design intent and every supplier relationship. Mick holds site reality and what the trades actually did. Józef holds tolerances and what the drawings don't say. The spreadsheet holds order status, three days stale. Caro holds her own changing mind, unannounced. The fabric house knows the discontinuation six weeks before anyone asks.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Lead-time blindness — discovering at order time the fabric is discontinued or 16 weeks out — **eliminate**
2. Drawing-version drift between studio and joiner — **eliminate**
3. The client's direct line to Mick producing un-designed decisions ("Mick said we could just…") — **contain**: the client may talk to her builder; what must go is decisions bypassing the record
4. Procurement-silence anxiety: no visible progress for weeks reads as neglect — **eliminate** (an information failure)
5. Budget drift surfacing as one horrible conversation instead of thirty small ones — **eliminate** the surprise; **contain** the underlying taste-vs-budget conflict, which is permanent
6. Józef's "that detail won't survive a damp winter" — **preserve**: the maker's no is quality control

**Conflicting interests:**
- Caro wants certainty, beauty and a fixed number; renovation offers none of these
- Imogen wants design integrity and her procurement margin protected
- Mick wants sequencing freedom and hates working around precious deliveries
- Józef wants frozen drawings and deposits before timber
- Suppliers are structurally indifferent to everyone

*Contain-class: the AI anchors these to the schedule and the budget — visible trade-offs, priced changes, recorded decisions.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap the junior designer — fine. Swap Mick mid-project and the site's tacit knowledge (what's behind which wall) leaves with him; weeks of re-discovery. Swap *Imogen* and the project halts: supplier relationships, design intent and the lead-time arithmetic are all her. Swap *Caro* (divorce, sale — it happens) and the constellation dissolves with bills attached.

**Afterlife:** Strong repeat game: a good project yields the next client at Caro's dinner table, and Mick and Józef appear in the next three projects. What should persist: supplier performance memory (who delivered, who lied about lead times), the as-built record, the client's preferences for phase two. What persists today: photos and a spreadsheet nobody reopens.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Track every ordered object against its lead time and the site programme; surface collisions months early. Keep drawings and decisions in one versioned record trades actually consume (WhatsApp-friendly). Convert procurement silence into ambient client reassurance. Log every client-initiated change with its cost before it becomes a dispute. Never make Mick feel managed by an app.

## R — Roles

**Archetype load** (3+ = structural overload):

| Seat | Archetypes held | Load |
|---|---|---|
| Imogen | Conductor · Creative Lead · Coordinator · Sponsor | **4 — founder-hub pathology, with vans** |
| Caro | Client Owner · Sponsor (the money) · Hidden Stakeholder (her partner, off-stage) | **3 — an overloaded civilian** |
| Mick | Production Realiser · Conductor (of his own trades) | 2 |
| Józef | Specialist Maker · Gatekeeper (buildability) | 2 |

### Imogen Sorrel, 47 — Principal Designer *(The Taste Hub)*
**Bath.** Twenty years in interiors, ex-London studio. Visual memory like a museum catalogue; numerical memory outsourced to a spreadsheet she doesn't trust. Phone-first, beautiful handwriting, four unread project-management app trials. **Attitude to AI:** interested if it speaks her language (images, schedules) and bored the moment it wants data entry. **Winning her looks like:** the lead-time collision she didn't see coming, flagged twelve weeks out.

### Caro Lindsey, 38 — Client *(The Resident Variable)*
**Bath.** Marketing director, two kids, living in the renovation. Decisive in meetings, revisionist at midnight on Pinterest. **Attitude to AI:** uses ChatGPT for everything; would happily interrogate "her" project bot at 11pm instead of emailing Imogen — if it never feels like being managed. **Winning her looks like:** she always knows what's happening and what her changes cost, before she's committed to them.

### Mick Tully, 55 — Main Contractor *(The Site Sovereign)*
**Radstock.** Thirty years building; communicates in 7am phone calls and photos of problems. Runs his trades on loyalty and cash flow. **Attitude to AI:** none; attitude to anything that adds admin: hostile. **Winning him looks like:** the delivery schedule is finally true, and nobody asks him to type.

### Józef Wrona, 44 — Bespoke Joiner *(The Maker Gate)*
**Frome.** Workshop-proud, drawings-literal, burned twice by mid-build design changes he absorbed for free. **Attitude to AI:** indifferent; will engage through email and PDFs only. **Winning him looks like:** frozen drawings, dated sign-offs, and changes that arrive as priced variations, not surprises at delivery.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Imogen ↔ Caro | Design fee + procurement margin | High → **fraying at silence and money**: every surprise spends double | Meetings, email, late-night texts | Imogen knows what things cost; Caro learns in instalments — the margin itself is a quiet asymmetry |
| Imogen ↔ Mick | Separate client contract usually | Long, robust → stable | Phone + WhatsApp | He controls the site she's accountable for styling; sequencing power is his |
| Imogen ↔ Józef | PO per piece | Professional → **brittle on changes** | Email + drawings | His lead time is her critical path; her changes are his margin erosion |
| Caro ↔ Mick | Building contract | Direct, warm → **the bypass risk**: decisions leak around the designer | Site chats | "Mick said" is the most expensive phrase in the project |
| Imogen ↔ suppliers | Trade accounts | Transactional → indifferent | Portals, email | They know about delays and discontinuations first and tell last |
| **Imogen ↔ AI** | — | Curious, ceremony-averse | In-product | Wins by watching the spreadsheet and email she already produces |
| **Caro ↔ AI** | — | Eager, boundary-blind | Client window | The 11pm self-serve edge; must show progress without exposing the margin or the chaos |
| **Mick ↔ AI** | — | Refusenik | WhatsApp/phone bridge | If it isn't a message or a call, it didn't happen |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- This persona adds **physical-goods edges** to the library: supplier edges with lead times, damage, and discontinuation risk — the AI must model objects in transit, not just decisions in flight.
- The Imogen↔Caro edge carries a legitimate information asymmetry (procurement margin): an early instance of *one truth per legitimate audience* outside the matchmaker family.
- The bypass edge (Caro↔Mick) can't be closed, only recorded: the AI's job is making the undesigned decision visible within hours, not policing the friendship.
