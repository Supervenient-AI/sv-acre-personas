# Imogen Sorrel
**Principal, Sorrel & Gray Interiors · 47 · Bath**
*The Taste Hub — from Sorrel & Gray, ACRE Persona 9*

> “I can hold an entire house in my head — every fabric, every finish, every handle. What I can't hold is forty lead times and a builder's programme. Nobody can. I just haven't admitted it yet.”

## Her world
Imogen's gift is the scheme — the room nobody else could see. Her tax is everything between the scheme and the reveal: chasing Italian mills at 7am, joiners at 7pm, and doing lead-time arithmetic in her head, badly, constantly. She has bought beautiful project-management software four times and quietly abandoned it four times; it always wanted data entry, and she wanted her evenings.

## What she's trying to do
- Deliver rooms that make clients cry (the good way) at reveal
- See lead-time collisions months out instead of discovering them at install
- Protect the procurement margin and the client relationship simultaneously — a quiet balancing act

## What gets in her way
- Forty suppliers' lead times, one builder's programme, zero shared system
- Clients whose direct line to the builder produces undesigned decisions
- Software that demands ceremony from a person who works visually, by phone

## How she feels about AI
Interested if it speaks her language — images, schedules, suppliers — and bored the instant it wants forms filled. She'd trust a tool that watched her existing email and spreadsheet and just *told her things*.

## In her own words
> “Tell me in March that the linen won't land before the decorator needs the room in June. That one sentence is worth the year's subscription.”

## Win her by
The early collision warning — twelve weeks out, unprompted, correct. After the first one saves an install, she'll forgive the product anything and recommend it at every dinner party in Bath.
