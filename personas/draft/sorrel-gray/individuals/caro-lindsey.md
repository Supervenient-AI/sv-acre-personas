# Caro Lindsey
**Client — Georgian townhouse renovation · 38 · Bath**
*The Resident Variable — from Sorrel & Gray, ACRE Persona 9*

> “Decisive? In meetings, completely. At midnight, on Pinterest, in a house full of dust? Less so.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 38 |
| Lives | Bath — in the renovation, with her husband, two kids and a temporary kitchen in the dining room |
| Background | Marketing director at a tech firm; runs campaigns for a living, which makes the project's opacity extra galling |
| Project | Whole-house renovation of a Georgian townhouse; six figures, fourteen months, lived-in throughout |
| Money | Saved for years; every variation lands on a number she knows by heart |
| Channels | Email for decisions, late-night texts for wobbles, site chats with Mick on Saturdays |
| Devices | iPhone (Pinterest, 11pm), work MacBook, a moodboard folder of 1,400 images |

## Her world
Caro lives inside the project — literally. The dust is in her coffee, the decisions are in her inbox, and the gap between Instagram's renovations and her own Tuesday is where her anxiety lives. Weeks of procurement silence read to her as neglect; a chance chat with Mick produces a "Mick said we could just—" that costs everyone a fortnight. She's not difficult. She's uninformed, at midnight, in the dust.

## What she's trying to do
- Get the house in her head built in the house she's standing in
- Know what's happening, what's decided, and what her changes actually cost — before committing
- Survive the renovation with marriage, budget and taste intact

## What gets in her way
- Silence: no visible progress for weeks while orders sit in lead-time limbo
- Money arriving as surprises instead of running totals
- Her own midnight revisions, made without prices attached

## How she feels about AI
Uses ChatGPT for everything at work; would happily interrogate "her" project bot at 11pm instead of emailing Imogen — provided it never feels like she's being managed or fobbed off.

## In her own words
> “I don't need it faster. I need to *see* it. Show me what's ordered, what's decided, what my Pinterest idea costs — and I'll be the easiest client they've ever had.”

## Win her by
The 11pm self-serve window: live progress, decision queue, running budget — honest enough to trust, kind enough to sleep on.
