## His world
Józef's reputation is built on millimetres and kept promises. His enemies are ambiguity and the changed mind: a designer's “small revision” in week four costs him material, bench time and the schedule of every job queued behind it. His "no" — *that detail won't survive a damp winter* — has saved more projects than any drawing, and is heard, on average, too late.

## What he's trying to do
- Build to frozen drawings, deliver to honest dates, get paid without quibbles
- Have his buildability warnings land at design stage, not delivery stage
- Keep the order book deep enough to choose his clients

## What gets in his way
- Drawings that evolve after the deposit
- Install dates set by builders he's never met, moving without notice
- Changes arriving as charm instead of as priced variations

## How he feels about AI
Serene indifference. He'll engage through email and PDFs only. But dated sign-offs, frozen revisions and changes that arrive *priced* — that's not technology to him, that's professionalism, and he'll respect whatever delivers it.

## In his own words
> “Sign the drawing. Date the signature. After that, changes cost money and time, in writing, like adults.”

## Win him by
Frozen drawings with dated sign-offs, variations priced before they reach his bench, and install dates that are true. The medium can be email; the message must be certainty.
