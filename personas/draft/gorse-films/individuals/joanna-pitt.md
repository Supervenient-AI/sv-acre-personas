> “My email says ‘a few small thoughts from the team.' What it means is: six people watched the cut, three disagreed, one of them runs the company, and I've spent two days turning that into something sendable.”

## Her world
Joanna bought the film to move her company's story forward and her own with it. Between her and that outcome stands the approval gauntlet: stakeholders with opinions, a CEO with veto power, and a production company that experiences her consolidated diplomacy as "the client keeps changing her mind." She's not changing her mind. She's *managing six minds* with one email address.

## What she's trying to do
- Land a film her CEO publicly loves and her sales team actually uses
- Look decisive upward and reasonable downward, simultaneously
- Get the internal politics processed without the agency seeing the sausage being made

## What gets in her way
- Stakeholders who arrive at v3 with v1 opinions
- Her own company's policy fog about what she may share with whom
- Translating a voicemail rant into a numbered note without losing the part that matters

## How she feels about AI
Moderate personal use; her company has a policy she hasn't read. She'd value anything that makes her consolidation job faster — and nothing that exposes its existence.

## In her own words
> “Help me send one coherent note instead of six contradictory ones, and make me look like I had it together all along. Because I did. It just took two days.”

## Win her by
Stakeholder-input consolidation on her side of the wall: contradictions surfaced privately, one clean note out, her composure intact in both directions.
