## Her world
Tasha's job compresses a company's worth of coordination into 72-hour bursts: forty people, one location, irreversible costs by the hour. Her call sheets are works of precision read by perhaps 60% of recipients — and the gap between sent and *received-and-understood* is where shoot days die. She can't see who's read what, so she confirms by message, by call, by hope.

## What she's trying to do
- Land every shoot day with zero surprises that weren't weather
- Know — actually know — who has read and understood the current call sheet
- Survive the season with her reputation (unflappable) and her sleep (negotiable) intact

## What gets in her way
- Last-minute changes that reach eleven of twelve WhatsApp groups
- Confirmation by silence: no reply could mean fine, could mean catastrophe
- Re-building the same logistics knowledge (that location's parking, that client's politics) every job, because wrap deletes everything

## How she feels about AI
Desperate yes. Anything that confirms receipt, chases silence and updates everyone's phones in real time is hired on the spot.

## In her own words
> “A call sheet that updates itself on everyone's phone and shows me who hasn't seen it. That's it. I'd cry.”

## Win her by
The living call sheet: self-updating, receipt-confirmed, silence-chasing — and a wrap archive so next quarter's shoot at the same location starts from knowledge, not archaeology.
