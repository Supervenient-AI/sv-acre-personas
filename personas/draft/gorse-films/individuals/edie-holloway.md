**Founder & Executive Producer, Gorse Films · 44 · Bristol**
*The Address Book — from Gorse Films, ACRE Persona 16*

> “The company is five people and a reputation. The other thirty-five on any shoot come out of my phone — and my phone knows things about this industry that no database ever will.”

## Her world
Edie's company inflates from five to forty people per shoot and deflates by wrap — and the institutional knowledge that makes each assembly work lives entirely in her head and phone. Every crew call is a small exam: availability roulette, rate negotiation, chemistry calculus. She knows the company's deepest risk is that its memory is co-extensive with hers, and she's never had a slow enough month to fix it.

## What she's trying to do
- Win better work and assemble it faster — assembly speed is competitive advantage
- Get the company's knowledge out of her head without giving away her career's edge
- Keep the trusted list loyal in a market where loyalty is one better-paying call away

## What gets in her way
- Crew assembly by phone roulette against availability nobody can see
- Wrap amnesia: everything learned about crew, client and location evaporating at dissolution
- The edit-round creep that quietly eats each job's margin in post

## How she feels about AI
Commercially curious, creatively protective. She'd delegate the call-around tomorrow; she'll guard the list's *judgements* (who's difficult, who's worth double) like the asset they are.

## In her own words
> “Externalise my address book without expropriating it. It's my pension. But yes — please, someone else do the ring-around.”

## Win her by
Assembly in hours not days, wrap archiving that banks every lesson, and an iron-clad answer on who owns the list's data: her.
