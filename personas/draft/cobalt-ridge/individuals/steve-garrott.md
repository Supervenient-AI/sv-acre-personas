**MD, Cobalt Ridge IT · 47 · Warrington**
*The Reluctant Executive — from Cobalt Ridge, ACRE Persona 28*

> “Sixty clients' worth of admin credentials. One engineer's laptop compromised and we're not a company, we're a headline. The insurance questionnaire asks if I sleep well. It doesn't, but it should.”

## His world
Steve sells certainty to sixty businesses that can't spell their own infrastructure — while privately auditing his own firm's: credentials in muscle memory, documentation decaying since onboarding, Andrei as a single point of knowledge. Every MSP podcast says adopt AI or die; every insurance renewal says prove your controls; the gap between those imperatives is where he lives.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep sixty clients safe and the firm off the incident reports
- Make documentation self-maintaining — it's also the firm's sale price someday
- Answer the insurer's questionnaire from a system, not a scramble

## What gets in his way
- Documentation truth decaying from the moment it's written
- The access lifecycle running on memory across sixty client estates and his own staff churn
- Root access plus generative AI being a sentence that frightens underwriters

## How he feels about AI
Commercially convinced, operationally cautious. The product that holds the keys to his keys must have a security posture he can present *before* its feature list.

## In his own words
> “Your architecture is the first meeting. Your features are the second. Get the order wrong and there's no second.”

## Win him by
Security-first credibility, then the compounding asset: documentation that builds itself from the work, an access lifecycle with evidence, and an insurance questionnaire that answers itself.
