# Pam Hesketh
**Practice Manager (client-side) · 52**
*The Conscripted IT Person — from Cobalt Ridge, ACRE Persona 28*

> “I became ‘the IT contact' because I sat nearest the server. Eleven years ago. I still don't know what a server does, but I know payroll runs Thursday, so nobody touches anything Wednesday night. Andrei knows that. The ticket system has never once known that.”

## Her world
Pam translates between two languages she doesn't speak: the partners' "the thing's broken again" and the MSP's "have you raised a ticket?" Her business context — payroll Thursdays, the tax-deadline crunch in January, which partner cannot be allowed near settings — is the most valuable client knowledge Cobalt Ridge has, and it lives in Andrei's memory because she told *him*, once, on the phone, like a human.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the firm working and the partners calm
- Explain each problem exactly once, in her own words, and have that be enough
- Get through January without the practice-management system wobbling

## What gets in her way
- Ticket forms that demand a vocabulary she was never taught
- Repeating context to every new engineer ("it's the same printer; it's always the same printer")
- Being accountable for technology she never volunteered to represent

## How she feels about AI
None personally; gratitude in advance for anything that means plain-English reporting and remembered context. If "the IT company got good", she'll never ask why.

## In her own words
> “Let me say it once, in English, and have everyone who ever helps us already know about Thursdays. That's all. That's the whole wish.”

## Win her by
Plain-language intake, context that persists across engineers, and the Thursday rule finally belonging to the *firm* — her renewal verdict writing itself.
