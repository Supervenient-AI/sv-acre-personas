## Her world
Chloe conducts the firm's reactive layer: forty tickets in flight, alerts crying wolf at industrial volume, and context scattered between documentation (stale), Andrei (interrupted) and ticket history (unsearched). Her triage instinct — which alert is real, which client is about to escalate, which engineer fits which mess — is excellent and entirely uninstrumented.

## What she's trying to do
- Defend the SLAs without burning the engineers
- Get tickets arriving *pre-contexted*: client, history, quirks, last-time's fix
- Cut the alert noise so the real wolf gets heard

## What gets in her way
- Context assembly as the hidden first hour of every ticket
- Alert fatigue normalising the exact signals that matter
- The Pam-bypass: clients ringing "their" engineer, skipping queue and record alike

## How she feels about AI
Immediate, total adopter — triage support, context assembly and alert filtration is her entire pain surface in three features. She'll be the firm's power user by week two and its internal champion by month one.

## In her own words
> “Pre-context the ticket, pre-filter the alerts, and show me the queue's truth. My instincts are good. I'd like them to be the last step, not the whole system.”

## Win her by
Tickets that arrive knowing things, alerts that earn attention — her instinct promoted from infrastructure to judgement.
