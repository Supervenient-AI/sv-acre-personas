## His world
Andrei holds what the documentation platform lost: the payroll-server quirks, the legacy app nobody else dares touch, the reason Client 23's VPN does that thing. Every interruption confirms his value and consumes his day; the project work he's been promised for three years stays perpetually behind the next page. Externalising his knowledge feels, on bad days, like dismantling his own standing — and he knows that feeling is a trap.

## What he's trying to do
- Do the interesting work: projects, architecture, the migrations with his name on them
- Cut the interrupt tax — half his day is answering what a system should know
- Stay the expert while becoming less of a hostage to being one

## What gets in his way
- Documentation decay he'd have to fix manually, in time he doesn't have, for credit he wouldn't get
- The bypass channel: clients ringing him directly because he's better than the queue
- The quiet equation between his irreplaceability and his standing

## How he feels about AI
Highly capable, quietly ambivalent. The Viktor/Chen Wei condition, confirmed again: harvest with attribution (his name on the knowledge, his interrupts halved, his promotion case strengthened) and he'll feed the system generously. Harvest anonymously and watch it starve.

## In his own words
> “Capture what I do as I do it, put my name on it, and give me my afternoons back. That's not a threat to my job. That's finally the job.”

## Win him by
Credit-preserving knowledge capture from his actual sessions — interrupts down, attribution up, project work unlocked. The expert raised, not mined.
