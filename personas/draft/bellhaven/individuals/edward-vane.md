# Edward Vane
**Founder & Principal, Bellhaven Capital · 58**
*The Storyteller — from Bellhaven, ACRE Persona 7*

> “The family doesn't want a dashboard. They want to know their money is in good hands and good brands. My job is to be both of those sentences.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 58 |
| Lives | Between Mayfair and Wiltshire — long marriage, grown children, a cellar he's prouder of than the returns |
| Background | Thirty years in consumer brands — built one, sold one, advised a dozen — before the family asked him to run their money |
| Role | Founder/principal; the fund's face to the family and the market's face to the fund |
| Style | Reads people perfectly, opens SharePoint never; phones Matthew the night before the quarterly with the pack in hand |
| Channels | Lunches, calls, the quarterly meeting; email through an assistant |
| Devices | An iPhone used as a telephone, a printed pack with margin notes |

## His world
Edward operates on instinct, relationships and a brand sense that two generations of the family have learned to trust. He consumes conclusions only: by the time anything reaches him, the uncertainty must already be resolved, because what he carries into the quarterly meeting is not analysis but *assurance* — and one bad number in that room would dent something that took thirty years to build.

## What he's trying to do
- Keep the family's confidence absolute and their questions answerable
- Back the right founders in the portfolio and stay out of their way otherwise
- Hand the fund on, eventually, in better shape than he found it

## What gets in his way
- A pack that arrives late or contains a number that wobbles under questioning
- The growing gap between how the portfolio's digital natives work and how he does
- Time — the only asset class he can't rebalance

## How he feels about AI
Personally: not at all. Institutionally: decisively — he'll never use the product, but he is its ultimate acceptance test. If the pack is right, early, and the story holds, the experiment continues.

## In his own words
> “I don't need to understand the machinery. I need Matthew calmer in March than he was last March.”

## Win him by
Never meeting him. He experiences the product as a CFO with answers, a pack without errors, and a quarterly meeting that ends early.
