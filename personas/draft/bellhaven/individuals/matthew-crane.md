## His world
Matthew runs the numbers behind a family's fortune and a portfolio's reality, and the gap between those two stories is his job. He's precise about his scepticism: AI gets used for what it's provably good at, and the real reason he won't connect it to SharePoint goes unsaid in meetings — the nagging 11pm voice that says *don't put the portfolio numbers in the chatbot*. He'd never phrase it as policy. It's stronger than policy.

## What he's trying to do
- Get the consolidation clean without re-litigating “net sales” every quarter
- Answer the five-minute questions (the facility rate, the covenant date) without 30 minutes in a 150-page PDF
- Keep the family's quarterly meeting boring, in the best way

## What gets in his way
- Five portfolio CFOs, five charts of accounts, five definitions of everything
- A document estate where everything exists in five places and none of them is the one you'd check
- His own (correct) caution gating the tools that would help most

## How he feels about AI
A firm 3/5 — and that score is a door, not a wall. Provenance, citations, and a deletion policy he can recite are the price of the SharePoint connection. Pay it, and the 3 becomes a quiet 5.

## In his own words
> “Tell me where the answer came from, page and line. Tell me where my data goes and when it's deleted. Then we can be ambitious.”

## Win him by
Citations on every answer, a security story he can recite to himself at 11pm — and then the five-minute tax disappearing, which he'll notice within a week and mention to another CFO within a month.
