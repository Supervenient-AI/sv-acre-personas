# Nicolas Faure
**CFO, flagship portfolio brand · 52 · Paris/London**
*The Blunt Instrument — from Bellhaven, ACRE Persona 7*

> “Your question costs my team a day. Your *better* question would have cost them an hour. Send the better question.”

## His world
Nicolas runs a finance function bigger than the entire fund that owns his company, and the asymmetry shapes everything: every reporting demand from Mayfair is a tax on a team with real work to do, and he prices it accordingly. His resistance isn't obstruction — it's signal. When Nicolas pushes back hard on a metric, the metric is usually badly defined.

## What he's trying to do
- Protect his team's time from upstream curiosity dressed as governance
- Keep his company's narrative accurate in the fund's consolidation — not flattened into someone's template
- Maintain standards: his numbers are right, and he expects the questions about them to be worthy

## What gets in his way
- Repetition: the same question about the same line, every quarter, from people who didn't write down last quarter's answer
- Templates designed for the smallest portfolio company applied to the largest
- His own delivery, which costs his good arguments half their force

## How he feels about AI
Quietly sophisticated, publicly dismissive. He already uses it on the layer he struggles with — tone. He'd cooperate with a fund-side system that asked pre-aligned, non-repeating questions, and he'd never admit it was a relief.

## In his own words
> “Ask once. Record the answer. Ask the *next* question next time. This is not advanced technology. It is manners.”

## Win him by
Requests that arrive pre-mapped to his chart of accounts, never repeat themselves, and cost his team minutes. Earn that, and the bluntest CFO in the portfolio becomes the consolidation's strongest pillar.
