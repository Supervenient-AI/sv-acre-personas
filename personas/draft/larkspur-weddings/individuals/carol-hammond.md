**Mother of the Bride · 61 · Harrogate**
*The Shadow Sponsor — from Larkspur, ACRE Persona 34*

> “I'm paying for the marquee and the band, and I have opinions about the rest — which apparently makes me ‘a dynamic to manage.' I raised that bride. I'll have my say. And then, yes, fine, it's her day. Obviously it's her day.”

## Her world
Carol occupies the constellation's strangest seat: real money, real love, no formal standing. Her inputs arrive sideways — a call to the florist "just to ask," an email to Frankie about the band's set list — and every one creates a small diplomatic event, because nobody has ever given her a legitimate channel. The vacuum does to her what vacuums do: amplifies.

## What she's trying to do
- Give her daughter a wedding worthy of her — and, quietly, of the family
- Be *heard*: the opinions are love with nowhere official to go
- Not become the mother-of-the-bride story planners tell each other (a fear she'd never admit)

## What gets in her way
- No legitimate channel, so every input is an incursion
- The gap between funding and deciding, felt as a status wound
- Her own certainty, which has organised many things well and one daughter imperfectly

## How she feels about AI
Unknowing and irrelevant — she feels about *being acknowledged*. An input that's logged, attributed and visibly considered would defuse half her sideways campaigns.

## In her own words
> “I don't need to win. I need to know my suggestion reached the table before it was declined. There's a difference, and it's called respect.”

## Win the edge by
The attributed-input log: every Carol contribution captured, acknowledged and anchored to the couple's decision — heard without being empowered, which is, deep down, the whole request.
