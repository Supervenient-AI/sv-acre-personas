## Her world
Zoe hired Larkspur precisely because she knows what good project management costs — and still finds herself awake at 11pm wondering whether the florist confirmed, because the project's state lives in Frankie's head and Zoe's professional brain abhors an invisible backlog. Her oscillations aren't indecision: they're a decisive person processing once-in-a-lifetime stakes plus Carol's commentary.

## What she's trying to do
- Marry Beth wonderfully — the actual point, occasionally visible through the logistics
- Know what's decided, what's pending and what it costs — self-served, at her hours
- Keep her mother heard, loved and *non-executive*

## What gets in her way
- Status that requires messaging a human she's trying not to over-message
- Decisions arriving un-sized ("thoughts on napkins?" versus the eleven implications thereof)
- The budget drifting in other people's heads

## How she feels about AI
Fluent and keen — she'd interrogate her wedding's status page at midnight with genuine pleasure, provided it never makes her wonder why she's paying a planner.

## In her own words
> “Give me the backlog view: decided, pending, blocked-on-me, with costs. I'll stop asking. I only ask because I can't see.”

## Win her by
The couple surface: live decision queue, running budget, midnight self-service — the civilian PM given a read-only console, and the planner's value made *more* visible by it.
