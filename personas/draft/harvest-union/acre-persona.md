# ACRE Persona 33 — The Federation of Equals
### Harvest Union — a buying co-operative of 60 independent food retailers where nothing can be imposed, only agreed

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #25 (Family 6, hubs of hubs); not yet interview-grounded.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Mandate-Free Centre
**Instance:** Harvest Union, Hebden Bridge. A buying group owned by its 60 member shops — independent wholefood and farm retailers. Secretariat of six: general manager, buying coordinator, finance, member services, comms, admin. Turnover through the group ~£14m; the secretariat's budget set annually by members who are also its customers, owners and boss.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | The Bellhaven/Brewbird shape stripped of even contractual power: members are *explicitly equal owners*. Every standard, every system, every supplier deal exists because enough members agreed — and survives only while they keep agreeing. |
| Tooling | The secretariat: M365 + an ordering platform members half-use. The members: sixty different EPOS systems, paper habits, and opinions about all of the above. The AGM: the constitution's beating heart, once a year, with biscuits. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The secretariat absorbs what it can; the rest is distributed across volunteer member committees — coordination by goodwill, which is real but exhaustible. |
| Buying behaviour | Collectively deliberate (anything significant goes to committee or vote), values-screened (the Groundswell pattern: supplier ethics move decisions), and slow by structure rather than temperament. |
| What failure costs | The group's whole point is aggregated leverage: fragmented ordering dissolves the discount that justifies membership. Worse: a governance rupture — members feeling steamrollered — can crack the federation; equals can always leave. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A centre that serves rather than rules: sixty equal owner-members pooling purchasing power and knowledge, where standardisation — the source of all the value — must be negotiated through governance that any member can stall, and the secretariat's only currencies are competence and trust.

**Cast:**
- **Dom Pickard, 49** — general manager of the secretariat; servant of sixty masters (Hebden Bridge)
- **Viv Holt, 58** — founding member; wholefoods shop, 40 years' trading, keeper of the founding spirit (Todmorden)
- **Kwame Boateng, 36** — newer member; urban refill store, digital-native, impatient with the pace (Manchester)
- **Sandra Ellery, 47** — buying coordinator; negotiator of the supplier deals (Hebden Bridge)
- **Plus:** 58 other member shops, the elected member council, volunteer committees, suppliers (the leverage target), the AGM

**External immovable nodes:**
- **Suppliers and their volume thresholds** — the discounts exist at order volumes the group must aggregate to hit; the leverage is arithmetic, not relationship
- **Co-operative governance law and the rulebook** — one member one vote; process that cannot be shortcut however obvious the answer
- **The AGM calendar** — big decisions wait for it; momentum dies in the gaps
- **Members' own businesses** — sixty shops' survival pressures outrank any federation initiative, always

**Topology:** A hub that is constitutionally a *spoke of its members*: authority points inward. Power lives in shifting member coalitions (the founders' bloc, the new urban shops, the big-volume rural stores); the secretariat navigates by weather sense. Sub-meshes everywhere: regional clusters, the council, committees, and the member-to-member back-channels where positions actually form.

**Lifecycle:** Perpetual, governed by cycles:
- *The ordering rhythm:* weekly/monthly consolidated orders — the operational heartbeat where leverage is won or leaked.
- *The deal cycle:* Sandra negotiates supplier terms on projected volumes; members' actual ordering then honours or undermines the projection — the federation's recurring integrity test.
- *The governance year:* motions, committees, council meetings, the AGM — proposals maturing at constitutional speed.
- *The initiative lifecycle (the painful one):* someone proposes a standard (shared EPOS data, a common label scheme) → enthusiasm → committee → pilot with eight keen members → the long stall at member 30 → quiet death or, occasionally, the tipping point. The federation's graveyard is full of good ideas that needed mandates.
- *Midpoint pattern:* every initiative hits the "who is this really for?" reckoning — the moment member trust in the centre's neutrality gets re-examined.
- *Trust curve:* the secretariat's trust is earned in deals delivered and spent in anything resembling empire-building; member-to-member trust is generational (Viv's bloc) or absent-yet (Kwame's cohort); the whole structure runs on it.

**Cadence:** Weekly order deadlines, monthly council, quarterly regional meetups, the annual AGM — and the constant low hum of member WhatsApp groups where consensus pre-forms.

**Boundary objects:** The consolidated order (the leverage made flesh), the supplier deal sheet, the member handbook, the motion paper (where change goes to queue), the rebate statement (the value made visible, annually), the pilot report (the initiative's make-or-break artefact).

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On the secretariat — Dom translating member weather into feasible proposals, Sandra sequencing deals against unreliable volume projections — and *invisibly on volunteer members* who run committees in evenings after their own shop's books.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Each member knows their shop's reality and their region's gossip. Viv holds the institutional memory ("we tried that in 2009; here's why it died"). Kwame holds the digital fluency the group needs and can't yet absorb. Sandra holds the supplier folklore (who'll flex on thresholds, who won't). Dom holds the political map. The ordering platform holds the volume truth that would settle most arguments — under-analysed because analysing it looks like surveillance.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Leverage leakage: members ordering off-group (habit, impatience, a local deal) and dissolving the collective discount — **contain** the autonomy (it's constitutional); **eliminate** the invisibility — members can't honour a projection they can't see their part in
2. Initiative death-at-member-30: standards that need network effects stalling below them — **eliminate** the friction costs (make joining a pilot near-zero effort); **contain** the consent requirement itself, which is the co-op working
3. Volunteer burnout on committees — **eliminate** the admin load that causes it
4. The founders/newcomers culture gap: Viv's bloc and Kwame's cohort talking past each other — **contain**: generational renewal tension is healthy; anchor it to evidence and pilots rather than identity
5. Secretariat neutrality suspicion: any centre tool reading as empire-building — **contain** permanently by design
6. Viv's "we tried that in 2009" — **preserve**: institutional memory as friction is cheap insurance; it just needs the *2009 evidence* attached so it informs rather than vetoes
7. The AGM's slow constitutional pace — **preserve**, mostly: the legitimacy is the point; the product speeds the preparation, never the consent

**Conflicting interests:**
- Every member wants the group's leverage and their own autonomy — the federation's founding paradox
- Big-volume members subsidise the leverage; small members democratically outnumber them
- The secretariat needs standardisation to function; the members' equality makes it negotiable forever
- Suppliers would love to divide and conquer; the group exists to prevent it

*Contain-class to the bone: this constellation is institutionalised contained conflict with a rulebook. The AI serves the negotiation; it never substitutes for it.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap a committee volunteer: that committee stalls a season. Swap *Sandra*: the supplier folklore and deal-relationships leave; next negotiation round underperforms visibly in the rebate. Swap *Dom*: the political map and the members' trust in the centre's neutrality must be re-earned — a two-year project; secretariat GMs who arrive "from industry" and act like CEOs last eighteen months, by folklore. Swap *Viv* (retirement looms): the institutional memory and the founders' bloc's centre of gravity go at once — a constitutional moment disguised as a leaving do. Members themselves churn slowly; each exit slightly weakens the arithmetic that holds the rest.

**Afterlife:** Perpetual by design — co-ops outlive everyone, which is the point. What compounds: the deal history, the rebate record (the value proof at every renewal of faith), the governance archive (every motion, pilot and post-mortem — currently in minutes nobody indexes), and the member-to-member relationships that make the federation more than its discount. What's at risk: the founding generation's memory, retiring shop by shop.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Serve the members first, visibly: each shop's own ordering view, rebate position and "your share of this deal's threshold" — making the collective arithmetic personal and the leverage leakage self-evident without surveillance. Make pilots near-free to join (the initiative graveyard is mostly friction, not dissent). Halve committee admin: papers drafted from the record, minutes that write themselves, motions tracked from proposal to AGM. Index the institutional memory — 2009's evidence attached to 2009's verdict — so Viv's caution and Kwame's impatience argue over facts. Arm Sandra with volume truth for negotiations. And structurally guarantee the centre can't see what members haven't consented to share: neutrality as architecture, the co-op's version of the perimeter promise.

### Dom Pickard, 49 — General Manager *(The Servant of Sixty)*
**Hebden Bridge.** Ex-wholesale, a decade in the role; has learned that the GM who proposes wins less than the GM who orchestrates members proposing. **Attitude to AI:** quietly keen, politically careful — anything the centre adopts must be explainable at the AGM in one sentence with no surveillance subtext. **Winning him looks like:** the governance machinery lightens, the leverage arithmetic becomes self-evident to every member, and the centre's neutrality is verifiable rather than asserted.

### Sandra Ellery, 47 — Buying Coordinator *(The Deal Maker)*
**Hebden Bridge.** Negotiates with forty suppliers on volumes she can project but not promise. **Attitude to AI:** practical — volume truth and deal-history at the table would be worth points of margin. **Winning her looks like:** projections she can defend, members who can see their own contribution to thresholds, and the folklore captured before she retires too.

### Viv Holt, 58 — Founding Member *(The Memory)*
**Todmorden.** Forty years of wholefoods retail; co-wrote the rulebook on a kitchen table. Suspicious of centres, platforms and anything that smells of head office — she's seen federations die of both bureaucracy and naivety. **Attitude to AI:** deeply sceptical, persuadable by evidence and by respect for process. **Winning her looks like:** the 2009 minutes surface *with* her caution, the co-op's character survives its modernisation, and nobody ever makes her use a dashboard to cast a vote.

### Kwame Boateng, 36 — Newer Member *(The Impatient Future)*
**Manchester.** Refill store, strong Instagram, joined for the leverage and stayed for the values; chairs the digital committee and feels the pace in his teeth. **Attitude to AI:** native; runs his own shop on it already. **Winning him looks like:** pilots he can join in one click, data he can actually use, and a federation that moves at the speed of evidence instead of the speed of biscuits.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Secretariat ↔ members | Membership rules (mutual) | Earned, conditional → **neutrality-tested by every tool and proposal** | Newsletters + meetings + visits | Authority points inward: the centre serves, the members rule |
| Member ↔ member | Co-ownership | Generational or absent → **the real political layer** | WhatsApp + regional meetups | Consensus pre-forms here; the centre designs around it, never inside it |
| Sandra ↔ suppliers | Deal terms | Professional, leverage-based → arithmetic-funded | Negotiations | Their divide-and-conquer instinct vs. the group's aggregation: the founding edge |
| Viv ↔ Kwame (blocs) | Equal votes | Wary respect → **the renewal tension** | The AGM floor | Memory vs. momentum; both right, both incomplete |
| Members ↔ ordering platform | Operational | Grudging → adoption-partial | The platform | The volume truth lives here, politically untouchable until consent is designed |
| **Dom ↔ AI** | — | Keen, AGM-explainable only | In-product | One-sentence-explainable features or none |
| **Members ↔ AI** | — | Sixty postures, one veto culture | Member-first surfaces | Value must land at the shop before the centre, ×60 — the Brewbird reciprocity rule under co-op law |
| **Viv ↔ AI** | — | Sceptical guardian | Paper-compatible | The product must coexist with members who will never touch it |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- The mandate-free centre is the family's purest constraint: *everything* is opt-in, so every feature must be individually worth opting into — the product as a stack of sixty separate yeses, with the network value emerging from voluntary overlap.
- Consent-as-architecture (the centre provably can't see unshared data) is the trust mechanism that unlocks the volume truth — the same perimeter promise as Northbeam and Cobalt Ridge, deployed for democracy instead of confidentiality.
- The governance machinery (motions, pilots, minutes, memory) is an unglamorous, uncontested wedge: nobody's autonomy is threatened by the minutes writing themselves — start there, earn the rest.
