# Dom Pickard
**General Manager, Harvest Union · 49 · Hebden Bridge**
*The Servant of Sixty — from Harvest Union, ACRE Persona 33*

> “GMs who arrive from industry and act like CEOs last eighteen months here. I've lasted ten years by learning the one trick: the GM who orchestrates members proposing wins more than the GM who proposes. It's slower. It's also the only speed that exists.”

## His world
Dom serves sixty masters who are also his employers, customers and constitution. Every standard that creates the group's value must be negotiated through governance any member can stall; every tool the centre adopts gets read for surveillance subtext. His decade of trust is the secretariat's real operating system — and his succession risk is the co-op's quiet constitutional question.

## What he's trying to do
- Deliver the leverage: deals that justify membership, rebates that prove it annually
- Lighten the governance machinery without touching its legitimacy
- Get initiatives past member 30 — the graveyard where good ideas go

## What gets in his way
- Authority-free articulation: he coordinates what he cannot compel (the Sofia Brandt condition, with biscuits)
- Volunteer committee burnout on the admin layer
- Every proposal's "who is this really for?" reckoning

## How he feels about AI
Quietly keen, politically careful: anything the centre adopts must be explainable at the AGM in one sentence, with no surveillance subtext, to Viv, while she watches his face.

## In his own words
> “One-sentence-explainable features only. ‘The minutes write themselves' passes. ‘The centre sees your ordering patterns' does not — even though the second would help most.”

## Win him by
The governance machinery first (motions tracked, minutes drafted, memory indexed) — uncontested ground that frees the hours his diplomacy actually needs.
