# Viv Holt
**Founding Member, Harvest Union · 58 · Todmorden**
*The Memory — from Harvest Union, ACRE Persona 33*

> “We wrote the rulebook on my kitchen table in 1989. I've watched federations die of bureaucracy and die of naivety — both deaths start with someone saying ‘it's just a tool.' So when I say ‘we tried that in 2009,' I'm not being difficult. I'm being the archive.”

## Her world
Viv's suspicion of centres, platforms and head-office energy is not technophobia — it's pattern recognition across four decades of co-operative history. She's watched the movement's funerals. Her "we tried that in 2009" is institutional memory doing its job; what it lacks is the *2009 evidence* attached, so it lands as veto when it's meant as data.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the co-op's character intact through its necessary modernisation
- Pass the founding generation's memory on as something better than folklore
- Retire, eventually, with the shop in good hands and the Union in good health — the order negotiable

## What gets in her way
- Modernisers who mistake her caution for obstruction
- Her own memory being the archive's only index
- The kitchen-table generation thinning, AGM by AGM

## How she feels about AI
Deeply sceptical, procedurally fair: she'll judge any tool by the rulebook's standards — member consent, centre restraint, one-member-one-vote untouched. Persuade the process and you've persuaded her.

## In her own words
> “Index the minutes back to 1989 and attach the evidence to my cautions. If 2009 says I'm right, you'll be glad of it. If it says I'm wrong — well. I've been wrong before. Once. In the nineties.”

## Win her by
Honouring the archive: the governance memory indexed and citable, her cautions upgraded from folklore to evidence — and not one feature that requires her to touch a dashboard to remain a full member.
