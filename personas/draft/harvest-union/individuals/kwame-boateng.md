## His world
Kwame loves what the Union *is* and despairs of how fast it *moves*. His shop already runs on the data practices the federation debates annually; his committee's proposals mature at constitutional speed while his competitors' tooling compounds quarterly. He respects Viv genuinely — her caution has been right before — and wants their arguments settled by evidence instead of seniority.

## What he's trying to do
- Drag the federation's tooling into this decade without breaking its democracy
- Pilot things: one click to join, evidence in weeks, verdicts from data
- Build the bridge between the founders' wisdom and his cohort's velocity

## What gets in his way
- Initiative death-at-member-30: friction, not dissent, killing good ideas
- The pace of consent — legitimate, constitutional, glacial
- Being read as "the digital one" when he's making commercial arguments

## How he feels about AI
Native — the shop runs on it already. For the Union: pilots with one-click joining, evidence attached to every old caution, and consent built in so deeply that even Viv signs off.

## In his own words
> “Make pilots near-free to join and verdicts data-shaped. I don't need the federation to move fast. I need it to move at the speed of evidence — which is still faster than the speed of biscuits.”

## Win him by
One-click pilot mechanics and the evidence-indexed memory — his impatience and Viv's caution finally arguing over facts, which is the argument the federation deserves.
