# ACRE Persona 37 — The Roster of Tiny Businesses
### Orbit Talent — a 7-person influencer management agency between creators, brands and the algorithm

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #29 (Family 8, matchmakers); not yet interview-grounded.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Two-Sided Career Custodian
**Instance:** Orbit Talent, London (Hackney). Founder, four talent managers, a campaigns coordinator, a finance/ops manager. A roster of 28 creators (lifestyle, food, parenting, fitness; 80k–2m followers) — each of whom is, legally and emotionally, a tiny business with one irreplaceable asset: themselves.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Matchmaker squared: agent between each creator and a rotating cast of brands — negotiating fees, usage rights and creative control — while the roster itself is 28 separate client businesses with their own accountants, burnout cycles and ambitions. |
| Tooling | Email + WhatsApp (the industry's actual infrastructure), a talent CRM half-fitted to the job, spreadsheets for campaign tracking, the platforms' creator dashboards (data silos by design), DocuSign for the contracts that matter. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The talent managers — each running 7 creators × multiple live campaigns × usage-rights tails, plus the pastoral care nobody invoices. |
| Buying behaviour | Young, fast, WhatsApp-native; adopts tools mid-campaign if they help, abandons them by the next one. Commission economics (15–20%) make creator-retention the only metric that ultimately matters. |
| What failure costs | A deliverable missed or a disclosure botched burns a brand relationship; a usage-rights breach (the brand running content beyond licence) burns money the creator never knows she lost; and a creator feeling like inventory leaves for a rival agency — taking 100% of her revenue line with her in one WhatsApp message. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A matchmaker hub where one side of every match is a person-as-business: the agency synchronises brand campaigns (briefs, contracts, content approvals, usage windows) while managing the creator's whole commercial life — and the asymmetries run in both directions: the brand must not see the creator's full rate-card reality; the creator must not see every blunt brand note.

**Cast:**
- **Remi Adachi, 39** — founder & senior talent manager (Hackney)
- **Tilly Marchant, 26** — talent manager; seven creators, infinite WhatsApp (Peckham)
- **Kayla Brooks, 24** — creator: food & lifestyle, 800k followers, a one-woman media company who never planned to be one (Manchester)
- **Joe Lindo, 33** — brand partnerships manager at a DTC kitchenware brand (the recurring buyer)
- **Plus:** the campaigns coordinator, finance (chasing brand invoices on creators' behalf), brand legal teams, the platforms, Kayla's accountant, and the audience — the constellation's invisible sovereign

**External immovable nodes:**
- **The platforms** — algorithm changes that reprice a creator's reach overnight; ToS that gate formats; analytics APIs that giveth and taketh
- **The ASA / CMA disclosure rules** — #ad compliance with legal force; the regulator reads Instagram now
- **The audience** — ultimate veto: a partnership that reads inauthentic costs the creator the only capital that matters
- **Brand legal & procurement** — contract turnaround times that kill momentum-driven deals

**Topology:** A bowtie per deal (brand-side process, creator-side process, the manager as the knot — the Bowline geometry), multiplied across a roster the agency must also manage *as a portfolio*: 28 tiny businesses whose interests occasionally collide (two creators, one brand category, one exclusivity clause).

**Lifecycle (per campaign, 2–8 weeks; per creator relationship, years; per usage right, up to perpetuity):**
- *The brief & the match:* brand wants reach and vibe; manager matches roster, protecting fit over fee (the long game).
- *Negotiation:* fee, deliverables, exclusivity, usage rights (the clause where careless wording costs five figures), approval rounds.
- *Creation & approval (the friction zone):* the brand's marketing committee meets the creator's authenticity instincts; notes fly; the manager translates both directions, softening neither into mush.
- *Live & the algorithm's verdict:* posting windows, performance screenshots, the brand's dashboard hunger.
- *The tail:* usage windows running (and overrunning — the systematically unpoliced revenue leak), payment chased through brand AP departments, results reported.
- *Midpoint pattern, per creator:* the annual "what is my career?" reckoning — burnout, platform anxiety, a rival agency's DM — where management proves it's management and not just deal-flow.
- *Trust curve:* the creator's trust is total and personal (career, money, mental health in one relationship) and spent by any moment of feeling like stock; brand trust is performance-based and portfolio-wide. The two trade constantly: pushing a tired creator into one more campaign spends the deep curve for the shallow one.

**Cadence:** WhatsApp-continuous with creators (including the 11pm wobble before a big post), email-formal with brands, the monthly invoice-chase cycle, and platform-shock fire drills.

**Boundary objects:** The contract (usage rights, exclusivity — the clauses that bite later), the brief, the content draft and its approval thread (the two-audience translation zone), the rate card (real version and negotiating version — the asymmetry made paper), the usage-rights register (mostly nonexistent; should be the crown jewels), the campaign report.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On the talent managers, doubled per the matchmaker condition, plus the pastoral layer no other persona carries this heavily: the manager is agent, project manager, therapist and bad-cop-by-proxy in one WhatsApp thread.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Tilly holds each creator's real state (rates, exclusivities, mental weather, ambitions). Kayla holds her audience's tolerances — knowledge no analytics dashboard captures. Joe holds the brand's internal politics and real budget. Remi holds the market's rate folklore and the roster's collision map. The usage-rights truth — what's licensed where until when — lives in PDF contracts nobody re-reads, which is why it leaks.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Usage-rights leakage: brands running content beyond licence, unnoticed and unbilled — **eliminate**; the industry's quietest systematic theft
2. Approval-round chaos: notes from five brand stakeholders relayed raw into a creator's anxiety at midnight — **eliminate** the rawness (translate, consolidate, time-shift); **preserve** the brand's right to notes and the creator's right to refuse (the authenticity veto is the product everyone's buying)
3. Roster-collision blindness: two creators sold conflicting exclusivities — **eliminate**
4. Payment-tail drag: creators paid when brands pay, 60–90 days of someone else's AP process — **eliminate** the chase burden; **contain** the cash-flow reality
5. The rate-card asymmetry fumbled: the negotiating number and the real number confused across the knot — **eliminate** structurally (the Bowline disclosure architecture, commercial edition)
6. Manager burnout from the pastoral layer — **contain**: the care is the moat; the admin around it is the eliminable part
7. Kayla's "my audience will smell this a mile off" — **preserve**: the authenticity veto is the entire long-term value of both businesses

**Conflicting interests:**
- The brand wants maximum usage for minimum fee; the creator's content is her pension
- The agency's commission wants deal flow; the creator's longevity wants curation — Remi polices her own incentive
- Two creators on one roster can want the same brand slot
- The platform wants content volume; the creator's mental health wants a fortnight off

*Contain-class, with the asymmetry named for protection again: the rate-card and the approval-translation are legitimate dual truths — the knot's integrity is the agency's licence to exist.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap the campaigns coordinator: a messy month. Swap *Tilly*: seven creators' trust — the personal kind — wobbles; one or two follow her if she goes to a rival (the Callum-pension pattern, with WhatsApp intimacy). Swap *Kayla's platform* (the algorithm turns): her reach reprices overnight and the roster portfolio absorbs the shock — the only persona where a *node's value* can halve in a week through no one's act. Swap *Joe*: the brand relationship resets to the contract's letter; usage leakage risk spikes during the gap. Swap *Remi*: the rate folklore and the roster's political map leave; the agency becomes a diary service for six months.

**Afterlife:** Campaigns end; usage rights run on (the tail that must be policed for years). Creator careers arc — and the agency's reputation is written by how it handles the *descents*: the creator whose numbers faded and was managed out with grace recruits the next rising star; the one dropped by text empties the pipeline. Content outlives everything, licensed or leaked. The roster alumni network is the industry's referral bloodstream.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** The usage-rights register as the crown-jewel feature: every licence, window, territory and renewal parsed from contracts and policed automatically — found money, monthly. The Bowline disclosure architecture for rates and notes: audience-scoped truth, translation-by-design across the knot. Approval threads consolidated, translated and humane-by-schedule (nothing lands on a creator at midnight). Roster-collision checking at deal entry. The payment chase run by something with no relationship to spend. And the pastoral layer protected: the manager's WhatsApp stays human; the AI clears everything else out of its way.

### Remi Adachi, 39 — Founder *(The Market Memory)*
**Hackney.** Ex-label A&R; built Orbit on the thesis that creators are artists with P&Ls. Knows every brand's real budget and every rival agency's poaching style. **Attitude to AI:** commercially sharp — sees the usage-rights register as free money and the slop-flood as her differentiation moment. **Winning her looks like:** the leakage stops, the folklore outlives her phone, and Orbit's creators visibly get the considered deals.

### Tilly Marchant, 26 — Talent Manager *(The Knot With a Heart)*
**Peckham.** Seven creators, four hundred WhatsApps a day, a translation engine between brand committees and human beings. **Attitude to AI:** native and selective — automate the chasing and the consolidation; touch the relationship and she's gone. **Winning her looks like:** the admin layer evaporates, the midnight-notes problem ends, and her care work becomes visible instead of invisible.

### Kayla Brooks, 24 — Creator *(The One-Woman Media Company)*
**Manchester.** Built 800k followers in three years from a student kitchen; now runs shoots, edits, negotiates (badly, which is why Orbit), and carries an audience's parasocial expectations daily. **Attitude to AI:** uses it for editing and captions; fiercely protective of her voice and her audience's trust. **Winning her looks like:** deals that fit, money that arrives, rights that don't leak, notes that arrive translated and kind — and a manager with time to actually manage.

### Joe Lindo, 33 — Brand Partnerships Manager *(The Recurring Buyer)*
**Shoreditch.** Spends £400k a year on creators; answers to a CMO who wants ROI screenshots. **Attitude to AI:** his own stack scores creators already; he trusts Orbit for what scores miss — fit. **Winning him looks like:** matches that perform, approvals that move, usage terms that are clear enough to never accidentally breach.

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tilly ↔ Kayla | Management agreement (20%) | Deep, personal → **inventory-moment fragile** | WhatsApp, constant | Career, money and mental weather in one thread; the agency's true asset |

| Orbit ↔ platforms | ToS | None → weather | Dashboards + APIs | Repricing events arrive by changelog |
| Orbit ↔ brand AP departments | Invoices | None → glacial | The chase | The payment tail; automate with zero relationship risk (the Halyard pattern) |
| **Tilly ↔ AI** | — | Selective native | In-product | Admin yes, relationship never — the hard boundary |
| **Kayla ↔ AI** | — | Voice-protective | Creator surface | Rights, money and schedule visibility; never her captions uninvited |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- The usage-rights register is the family's most quantifiable wedge: parse the contracts, police the windows, invoice the overruns — revenue recovered, not workflow improved. Lead with it.
- The matchmaker disclosure architecture (Persona 36) extends here with a twist: *translation*, not just scoping — the same note must exist in brand-committee dialect and human-at-midnight dialect, both true, both logged.
- The person-as-business node (Kayla) imports the whole library in miniature: she's a founder-hub (Wildfen), a creative supplier (Mara), and a one-person constellation set (Hale) simultaneously — the roster is 28 nested personas, and the product that serves the agency should expect to meet them.
