## Her world
Kayla is the library in miniature: a founder-hub, a creative supplier and a one-person constellation simultaneously. Her authenticity is the asset every party monetises and only she pays to maintain — every misjudged partnership lands in her mentions, not the brand's quarterly review. Tilly handles the machinery; the algorithm's verdicts and the audience's moods remain, at 1am, hers alone.

## What she's trying to do
- Keep the audience's trust — the only capital that compounds
- Get paid: properly, promptly, including the usage money she doesn't know she's owed
- Build past the algorithm: a cookbook, maybe a product line — something she owns

## What gets in her way
- Brand notes that arrive at midnight, plural and bruising
- Contracts she signs trusting Tilly read the clauses (Tilly mostly did)
- The burnout maths of being talent, studio and brand in one body

## How she feels about AI
Uses it for editing and captions; fiercely protective of her voice and her audience's trust. Anything that touches her content uninvited is an enemy; anything that polices her rights and money invisibly is a friend she'll never need to meet.

## In her own words
> “Show me my deals, my money and my rights in one place — and make the notes arrive kind, in daylight. I make the content. I'd like to stop also being the back office.”

## Win her by
The creator surface: rights and payments visible and policed, approvals humane-by-schedule, her voice untouched — the back office lifted off the talent.
