## Her world
Remi built Orbit on the bet that talent treated like artists outlasts talent treated like inventory — and the bet pays in retention: her roster stays. Her threats are structural: usage leakage bleeding her creators invisibly, the slop-flood compressing brand budgets, and the rate folklore that is her negotiating edge living exclusively in her phone and memory.

## What she's trying to do
- Stop the leakage: every licence policed, every overrun invoiced
- Scale the management craft past her own head — the folklore has to outlive her phone
- Keep the roster loyal through the descents, not just the ascents — graceful career-arc management is the brand

## What gets in her way
- Rights terms buried in contracts nobody re-reads
- Roster-collision risk as the agency grows (two creators, one category, one exclusivity clause)
- The pastoral layer consuming her managers unsupported

## How she feels about AI
Commercially sharp: the usage-rights register is free money, the slop-flood is her differentiation moment, and the disclosure architecture (rates, notes) is the professionalisation her industry never had.

## In her own words
> “Parse every contract, police every window, invoice every overrun. Lead with that — it pays for itself in a quarter — and I'll let you near everything else.”

## Win her by
The usage-rights register as the wedge (found money, monthly), then the folklore engine and roster-collision checking — the agency's craft, instrumented.
