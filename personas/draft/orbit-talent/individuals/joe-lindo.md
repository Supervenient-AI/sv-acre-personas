# Joe Lindo
**Brand Partnerships Manager (client) · 33 · Shoreditch**
*The Recurring Buyer — from Orbit, ACRE Persona 37*

> “My stack scores creators on reach, engagement, brand safety — a thousand data points. What the scores can't tell me is fit: whether Kayla's audience will buy a frying pan from her because she actually cooks. That's what I pay Orbit for. The scores are a longlist. Fit is the deal.”

## His world
Joe is the matchmaker's counterparty done well: clear briefs, decent budgets, prompt-ish payment (his AP team's pace shames him), and a marketing committee behind him whose notes he aggregates before they reach Orbit — usually. His recurring pain is contractual fog: usage terms he can't quickly check, which means his team occasionally breaches them innocently, which means awkward calls he'd pay to never have.

## What he's trying to do
- Make creator spend provably outperform paid social — the CMO's standing question
- Build repeatable relationships with talent that fits, instead of one-off transactions
- Never accidentally breach a usage window again (twice was educational; three times is a pattern)

## What gets in his way
- Usage terms living in PDFs his content team never reads
- His own committee's notes arriving plural and late
- Approval rounds dragging campaign momentum into next quarter

## How he feels about AI
Stack-augmenting pragmatist: scores for the longlist, Orbit for the fit, and any tool that makes terms clear and approvals fast is on his side of the table too.

## In his own words
> “Give me the usage terms in plain sight and approvals that move in days. Half my problems with agencies are actually problems with PDFs.”

## Win him by
Clarity as a service: machine-readable usage terms, consolidated approval flows, performance reporting he can screenshot — the buyer's friction removed by the agency's own tooling.
