# Heather Macrae
**Customer Liaison, contract distillery · 45 · Speyside**
*The Capacity Sovereign — from Copperline, ACRE Persona 13*

> “I look after twelve brands' hopes and one bottling hall's hours. The brands that plan straight get my best. The ones that panic get my sympathy, which fills no bottles.”

## At a glance
| | |
|---|---|
| Age | 45 |
| Lives | Speyside — husband works the stills at a rival; two teenagers; weather apps consulted like scripture |
| Background | Twenty years in production: line operator, planner, now the human interface for all contract clients |
| Role | Manages a dozen contract brands against one capacity calendar; the voice that says when bottling happens |
| Power | The calendar. Slots booked months out; missed slots don't move — seasons do |
| Channels | Email and phone, office hours; allergic to clients' panics, kind to organised ones |
| Devices | A desktop with the planning system, a phone she answers on the second ring before 4pm |

## Her world
Heather is Dave Hartley promoted to structural power: she doesn't just hold constraints, she holds *capacity* — and capacity decides which small brand's Christmas exists. She quietly likes Copperline (good liquid, decent people) more than its order volume strictly merits, and Cal's organisational chaos taxes that affection monthly.

## What she's trying to do
- Run the calendar at full, calm utilisation — the distillery's margin lives in unbroken sequences
- Keep her contract brands honest about dates, specs and ABVs
- Reward the easy clients without ever saying that's what's happening

## What gets in her way
- Artwork arriving late and rippling through everyone's slots
- Spec changes ("just a small ABV tweak") that re-trigger duty calculations and label approvals
- Panicked phone calls that consume the planning hours that would prevent them

## How she feels about AI
Indifferent — the distillery has its systems. But she'll happily feed dates and confirmations into a client's tool if it stops the panicked calls; quiet phones are her love language.

## In her own words
> “Complete spec, on-time artwork, no surprises. Do that for three runs and you'll find the calendar gets friendlier. Funny how that works.”

## Win her by
Making Copperline organised at her: confirmed specs, artwork early, changes priced and flagged — so her affection for the brand and her calendar's logic finally point the same way.
