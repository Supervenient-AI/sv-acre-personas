## Her world
Lena is Mara Kovač's pattern in a different vertical: hired on portfolio, briefed on enthusiasm, and structurally last to learn the constraints that govern her canvas — dielines, duty-stamp zones, mandatory label copy. She's eaten one reprint-adjacent disaster already in her career (specs that arrived after the artwork) and reads every new client tool as a possible surveillance instrument until proven otherwise.

## What she's trying to do
- Make Copperline's shelf presence sing — it's a portfolio piece with national-listing potential
- Get the compliance constraints up front, in the brief, not at artwork rejection
- Protect her revision rounds: enthusiasm is not a scope document

## What gets in her way
- Briefs that arrive as a voice note and a moodboard
- Label law she's expected to know by osmosis
- Conscription into client tools that cost her time and watch her hours

## How she feels about AI
Uses it for ideation and mockup contexts; wary near final artwork and warier of anything observational. The Mara rule applies: the product must cost her less effort than the email threads it replaces, from message one.

## In her own words
> “Put the dieline, the duty-stamp zone and the mandatory copy in the brief on day one. That's not a feature request. That's the difference between one round and five.”

## Win her by
Day-one constraint packs and feedback that arrives annotated on the artwork — useful from first contact, observational never.
