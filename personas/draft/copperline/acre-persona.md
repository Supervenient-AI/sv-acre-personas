# ACRE Persona 13 — The Contract-Production Challenger
### Copperline Spirits — a 5-person craft rum brand whose "factory" is a supplier

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #5 (Family 1, hub-and-spoke). Deliberately built as a **validation persona**: a near-clone of Wildfen (Persona 1) used to test that the Founder-Hub Creative Sprint constellation survives a change of vertical. Reads as a delta against Persona 1.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Outsourced-Everything Challenger Brand (Wildfen's archetype, different shelf)
**Instance:** Copperline Spirits, Leeds. Five staff: founder, ops lead, sales lead, brand exec, events/trade person. ~£1.4m turnover. Spiced rum and a canned serve; stocked in independents, two regional supermarket listings, chasing a national one.

**Delta from Wildfen:** the core product itself — not just the creative — is made by an outside party. A contract distillery owns the stills, the capacity calendar and, in practice, the launch date. Where Wildfen's existential supplier was a printer at artwork stage, Copperline's is a producer at *every* stage. Add excise duty: HMRC sits inside the operating model, not just at year-end.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Radically networked, Wildfen-pattern: brand, liquid development, production, design, PR all cross the boundary. |
| Tooling | WhatsApp + Google Workspace + a stock spreadsheet that is load-bearing for a regulated product. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The founder, 9pm–midnight, Wildfen-pattern — plus the ops lead on the duty/stock side, which Wildfen doesn't have. |
| Buying behaviour | Founder champion-buyer, free-trial-at-11pm pattern confirmed. |
| What failure costs | Wildfen's costs, plus: a missed bottling slot loses a season (Christmas is 40% of revenue); a duty error is a legal problem, not an embarrassment. |

---

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A founder-hub coordinating external specialists toward hard retail deadlines — Wildfen's constellation with the production node promoted from late-joining spoke to permanent, capacity-constrained co-dependency.

**Cast:**
- **Cal Brody, 38** — founder, the hub (Leeds)
- **Heather Macrae, 45** — customer liaison at the contract distillery (Speyside) — *the promoted Dave Hartley*
- **Femi Adeoye, 35** — ops lead; owns stock, duty and logistics (Leeds)
- **Lena Crowe, 29** — freelance brand designer (Sheffield)
- **Plus:** a drinks PR freelancer, bottle/label suppliers, a national wholesaler, the supermarket buyer

**External immovable nodes:**
- **HMRC excise** — duty points, AWRS registration, warehousing rules: a regulator inside the supply chain itself
- **The supermarket buyer** — Wildfen's Sainsbury's-buyer node, confirmed: sets the deadline, reviews late and binarily
- **The distillery's capacity calendar** — bottling slots booked months out; miss one and the season moves, not the slot
- **Alcohol labelling & duty-stamp regulation** — constrains every label Lena designs, checked too late, Wildfen-pattern

**Topology:** Hub-and-spoke around Cal — but with one spoke (the distillery) thicker than the hub: it has more power than any Wildfen supplier, because it cannot be substituted within a season.

**Lifecycle:** Wildfen's 10-week sprint shape recurs per SKU/listing, nested inside a *seasonal* cycle the distillery's calendar dominates. Same midpoint reset (the brief means different things to brand exec, designer and distillery), same crunch (artwork → label print → bottling slot), same abrupt dissolution. The trust curve matches Wildfen, with one addition: the distillery edge carries trust *both* ways — Copperline is also being assessed as a client worth keeping when capacity tightens.

**Boundary objects:** The label artwork (Wildfen's mockup, with legal compliance attached), the spec sheet (ABV, liquid, fill — the tech-pack equivalent), the bottling-slot booking, the stock/duty spreadsheet, the brief in its usual four remembered versions.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Wildfen-pattern (founder's brain as system of record — confirmed by hypothesis), plus: Heather knows real capacity and queue position; Femi knows the duty position; nobody else can read the stock spreadsheet.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged — Wildfen's five recur; deltas below)*:
1–5. Version chaos, late constraint discovery, hub decision bottleneck, the two-briefs problem, blame ambiguity — **all confirmed at hypothesis level, same tags as Persona 1**
6. Slot-miss cascade: artwork late → label print late → bottling slot lost → season lost — **eliminate** (Wildfen's preflight failure, with a calendar amplifier)
7. Duty/stock divergence: the spreadsheet says one thing, the bonded warehouse another — **eliminate**; this failure class has a regulator attached
8. The distillery's "that ABV change re-triggers duty calculations and label approval" — **preserve**: constraint-holder's no, Dave-pattern

**Conflicting interests:** Wildfen's set, plus one structural addition: **the distillery profits from inflexibility** (change fees, minimum runs) while Copperline's brand depends on agility — a thicker version of the Calverton late-change dynamic, running all year.

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap Lena: Wildfen's illustrator-swap cost, confirmed. Swap *the distillery*: not a substitution — a re-founding; liquid consistency, compliance history and a year of calendar leave at once. The hub single-point-of-failure (swap Cal, constellation halts) is identical to Wildfen.

**Afterlife:** Wildfen-pattern (next SKU within a year, edges in WhatsApp scrollback) — plus a regulated paper trail (duty records) that *must* survive dissolution, and currently lives in the same spreadsheet as everything else.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Everything Persona 1 lists — relieve the hub, hold live state, surface constraints early, chase decisions, make handoffs explicit, never make spokes feel watched — plus: track the artwork-to-bottling-slot critical path as a first-class object, and keep the duty/stock record clean enough to show an inspector.

## R — Roles

**Archetype load:**

### Cal Brody, 38 — Founder *(The Brand Hub)*
**Leeds.** Ex-bar manager turned brand founder; sells the story brilliantly, runs the business off his phone. Priya-pattern: enthusiastic, over-trusting of AI, system-of-record-in-head. **Winning him looks like:** an evening back in week two, and the Christmas slot maths visible in July.

### Femi Adeoye, 35 — Ops Lead *(The Regulated Spreadsheet)*
**Leeds.** Ex-3PL operations; the only person who understands the duty position, and painfully aware of it. **Attitude to AI:** keen, audit-minded — wants automation with a paper trail. **Winning her looks like:** the stock/duty record reconciles itself and she stops being the single point of compliance.

### Heather Macrae, 45 — Distillery Liaison *(The Capacity Sovereign)*
**Speyside.** Twenty years in production; manages a dozen contract brands' hopes against one capacity calendar. Email and phone; allergic to clients' panics, kind to organised ones. **Attitude to AI:** indifferent; will feed dates into anything that stops the panicked calls. **Winning her looks like:** Copperline becomes the easy client — complete specs, on-time artwork — which is what actually protects its slots.

### Lena Crowe, 29 — Freelance Designer *(The Conscripted Periphery)*
**Sheffield.** Mara-pattern: found on Instagram, lives in WhatsApp and a Drive folder, burned before by specs that arrived after the artwork. **Winning her looks like:** label dielines, compliance rules and duty-stamp zones in the brief on day one — not at rejection.

## E — Edges

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cal ↔ Heather | Annual contract + per-run POs | Professional → **mutually assessed**: he audits her dates; she audits his act-together-ness | Email + phone | The thick spoke: capacity power outweighs the hub. Wildfen has no equivalent |
| Cal ↔ Femi | Employment | High → **load-bearing** | Office + WhatsApp | She holds the regulated truth; he holds the relationships |
| Cal ↔ Lena | Per project, informal | Warm, new → fragile (Mara-pattern, confirmed) | WhatsApp + Drive | Payment power vs. creative dependency |
| Cal ↔ supermarket buyer | Listing (prospective) | Asymmetric → all power one side (Wildfen-pattern, confirmed) | Email + range reviews | Sets every deadline; visible to no one else |
| Copperline ↔ HMRC | Statutory | N/A → inspection-shaped | Portal + records | The edge that turns "spreadsheet chaos" from cost into liability |
| **Cal ↔ AI** | — | Eager, over-trusting (Priya-pattern) | In-product | Same risk: delegating calls the spokes expect from a human |
| **Femi ↔ AI** | — | Keen, audit-first | In-product | Wants provenance on every number — the compliance twin of Bellhaven's Matthew |
| **Heather ↔ AI** | — | Indifferent until it quiets her phone | Email bridge | Dave-pattern, confirmed: meet her in her inbox |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- **Validation verdict (hypothesis):** the Wildfen constellation transfers nearly intact — same hub pathology, same conscripted periphery, same gatekeeper-buyer. The product designed for Persona 1 serves Persona 13 with two additions: a critical-path object tied to externally owned capacity, and a compliance-grade record layer.
- The distillery edge teaches the general lesson: when one spoke holds capacity power over the hub, "being easy to work with" is the hub's only leverage — and is a property the product can directly manufacture.
