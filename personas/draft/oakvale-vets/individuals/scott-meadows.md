## His world
Scott handed his family's dog through a door marked "specialist" and entered two parallel processes: the clinical one (excellent, mostly legible through morning calls) and the financial one (a fog of authorisation codes and assessment language). The aftercare plan's success now lives in his kitchen — restricted stairs, lead-walks by week, the kids negotiated with daily — decoded from a discharge letter via Laura's translation.

## What he's trying to do
- Get Biscuit home and right: the rehab done properly, the other knee's odds understood
- Survive the money: know the claim's status before the desk does the awkward maths aloud
- Keep the kids informed at child-grade truth — his nightly editorial challenge

## What gets in his way
- Insurance fog: claim status discovered at the payment desk
- Aftercare instructions written clinician-to-clinician, executed parent-to-Labrador
- The vigil: hospitalisation days with imagination filling the gaps between calls

## How he feels about AI
Invisible-is-fine: he wants morning updates, money clarity and his dog back. If a system delivers the daily call's content and the claim's live status, he'll never ask what powered it.

## In his own words
> “Tell me how he is every morning, where the claim's up to, and what we do at home in words Kerry and I can actually follow. That's it. He's not a case to us. He's Biscuit.”

## Win him by
The owner surface: daily updates warm and true, the money lane visible end-to-end, aftercare in kitchen-table English — the family's proxy informed, the desk never awkward again.
