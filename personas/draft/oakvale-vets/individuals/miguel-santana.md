# Miguel Santana
**Clinical Director & Specialist Surgeon, Oakvale Veterinary Referrals · 47 · Derby**
*The Specialist Node — from Oakvale, ACRE Persona 45*

> “My discharge letters are legendary when they're not late. The case should never beat its instructions home — a Labrador's cruciate repair undone by a fortnight of unmanaged stairs is a surgical failure that happened in an inbox. I operate Tuesdays and Thursdays. The correspondence operates on my evenings.”

## His world
Miguel's cases arrive from ninety practices with histories of variable completeness and owners of uniform anxiety. His clinical work is excellent and bounded; his coordination work — the records chase before consult, the letters after discharge, the free phone advice that keeps referrers loyal — is unbounded and structurally invisible. The referrer relationships his letters maintain *are* the centre's pipeline.

## What he's trying to do
- Operate well and discharge better: the letter beating the patient home, every time
- Get complete histories at consult instead of reconstructing them live
- Keep the referrer network warm without donating every evening to prose

## What gets in his way
- The records chase stalling intake on other companies' admin
- Letters drafted from memory at 9pm instead of from the record at discharge
- The free-advice economy: load-bearing, unlogged, unresourced

## How he feels about AI
Dictation-converted already; letters drafted from the clinical record for his approval is the obvious next instrument. Clinical judgement is his; the prose-assembly never needed to be.

## In his own words
> “Draft the letter from the record the moment I close, in my structure, for my signature. The complication call to Laura I'll always make myself — that's medicine. The rest was always machinery.”

## Win him by
Discharge letters before the patient's in the car, histories assembled before consult — his evenings returned, his network fed by the letters' new punctuality.
