# Laura Brignall
**First-Opinion Vet · 34 · Loughborough**
*The Returning Edge — from Oakvale, ACRE Persona 45*

> “I refer where my cases are treated well *and returned well*. The discharge letter is the whole relationship: if it beats the patient home, clear enough to decode at a kitchen table, my client stays loyal to me and I stay loyal to Oakvale. If the centre's system ever emails my client directly, that's the last case they see from this practice. Etiquette isn't decoration here. It's the economy.”

## Her world
Laura's referrals are acts of trust performed in front of her client: she's saying *these people are better than me at this one thing, and they'll send you back*. Her tests of a referral centre are operational — five-minute referral submission, letters before patients, zero client-poaching — and her loyalty, once earned, routes fifty cases a year. Her own system can't talk to Oakvale's; the interoperability void is her admin and her irritation.

## What she's trying to do
- Get her patients the right care at the right tier — and back
- Keep her clients hers: the lifetime relationship is the practice's pension
- Spend her referral admin minutes on medicine instead

## What gets in her way
- Referral forms that demand twenty minutes she doesn't have
- Discharge letters arriving after the patient, decoded by an anxious owner from memory
- The quiet fear every independent vet carries: the corporate consolidators' captive referral flows

## How she feels about AI
Pragmatic user already (notes, client comms); judges referral centres partly on how easy they are to work with — a property she's watched become software.

## In her own words
> “Five-minute referrals, letters before patients, my client's loyalty untouched. Be the easiest centre to work with and you'll be the only one I use. It's not sentiment. It's Tuesday-afternoon arithmetic.”

## Win her by
The referrer surface: frictionless submission, letters that beat the patient home, the etiquette honoured by architecture — loyalty earned in admin minutes saved.
