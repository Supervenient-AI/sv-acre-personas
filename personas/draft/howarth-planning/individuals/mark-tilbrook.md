**Developer (client) · 45 · Leeds**
*The Viability Engine — from Howarth Planning, ACRE Persona 25*

> “Every committee cycle costs me interest on borrowed money. So when I read planning policy at midnight on ChatGPT and arrive with theories, that's not me being difficult. That's me watching my carry costs eat my margin in real time.”

## His world
Mark's business is arbitrage between land's current permission and its potential one — and the planning system is the expensive, opaque middle of every deal. He's charming, impatient and selectively transparent about his numbers (viability is a negotiation, after all). He trusts Cath's craft and chafes at the system's pace, and his midnight policy theories are equal parts genuine insight and creative hope.

## What he's trying to do
- Get consents at densities that make the numbers work
- See momentum honestly: which schemes are moving, which are quietly stuck
- Make the cost of delay visible to everyone who causes it

## What gets in his way
- Committee calendars and consultee windows that don't price his interest payments
- His own optimism at land purchase pricing the planning risk at zero
- The gap between what officers signal and what he hears

## How he feels about AI
Already self-serving with it, ungoverned and enthusiastic. He'd rather have a sourced answer at midnight than a theory — barely.

## In his own words
> “Show me the honest momentum view and the cost of each delay. I can handle bad news. I'm pricing it anyway — just badly.”

## Win him by
A truthful client window — stage, momentum, delay costs, sourced answers to his midnight theories — converting the impatient client into an informed one.
