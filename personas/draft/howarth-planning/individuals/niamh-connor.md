> “Forty live cases. The agents whose submissions arrive complete, consistent and honest get processed first — not because I play favourites, but because they're the only ones I *can* process in the time I have.”

## Her world
Niamh decides — or rather, recommends — what gets built across half a district, from a desk where forty cases compete for attention measured in fragments. She will never be anyone's software user; she is the desk every product's output lands on. Her professional kindness is genuine and strictly budgeted: agents who waste her time with contradictory evidence pay in queue position, without malice, by arithmetic.

## What she's trying to do
- Process the caseload defensibly — every recommendation survivable at committee and appeal
- Protect her pre-app advice from being quoted back at her, distorted, months later
- Get home by 5:30 enough days to remember why she liked this job

## What gets in her way
- Submissions whose six specialist reports disagree with each other
- Agents who treat her signals as opening positions
- A workload that converts professional judgement into triage

## How she feels about AI
Institutionally cautious, personally curious, practically irrelevant — her authority's tooling is a decade behind the question. What reaches her desk *from agents* is what matters.

## In her own words
> “Make the submission complete, consistent and honest. That's it. The agents who do that have, functionally, hired me.”

## Win the edge by
Designing for her desk without her ever logging in: contradiction-free evidence, complete checklists, commitments tracked accurately — Howarth's applications becoming the ones she reaches for first.
