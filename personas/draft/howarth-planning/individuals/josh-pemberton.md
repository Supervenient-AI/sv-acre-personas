## His world
Josh's applications are coordination meshes: heritage, ecology, transport and landscape consultants — each from a different firm, each on their own timeline — converging (theoretically) on submission dates set against committee calendars. He summarises 300-page evidence bases with AI already, and watches the consultee windows by spreadsheet vigilance, because nothing else watches them at all.

## What he's trying to do
- Run twelve coherent applications without one contradiction reaching the authority
- Watch every validation checklist, consultee window and committee date without it ageing him
- Write actual planning arguments — the work he trained for — instead of chasing PDFs

## What gets in his way
- Six specialists' drafts disagreeing in submitted evidence
- A late ecology response discovered after it stalled the cycle
- Clients ringing on Fridays for momentum he can't manufacture

## How he feels about AI
Daily, practical user — summarisation is already his. The clock-watching, version-control and contradiction-checking layer is what he'd hand over tomorrow.

## In his own words
> “Check the six reports against each other before the case officer does. Catch one clash per scheme and you've saved a committee cycle. Catch the consultee windows too and I'll write you a testimonial.”

## Win him by
Assembly automation: contradiction-checking, clock infrastructure, version truth — twelve applications run like one tidy desk.
