# Dr Frieda Stannard
**Freelance Heritage Consultant · 47 · Harrogate**
*The Conscience Node — from Howarth Planning, ACRE Persona 25*

> “My entire market value is that my assessments can't be bought. The day a client's commercial gravity bends one of my reports, every report I've ever written is worth less. So no — I won't ‘soften the harm paragraph.'”

## Her world
Frieda's reports decide schemes — and her independence is the only reason they're worth commissioning. She works for developers' money and the historic environment's interest simultaneously, which works precisely as long as everyone understands the order of those loyalties. Her early "this harms the conservation area" reshapes schemes; her late one kills them; her suppressed one would end her.

## What she's trying to do
- Get her assessments into schemes early enough to shape rather than veto
- Keep her advice history accurate everywhere it's quoted
- Defend the discipline as AI-generated heritage statements start appearing (she can tell; authorities increasingly can too)

## What gets in her way
- Being commissioned after the design is emotionally settled
- Commercial pressure arriving as charm, then as silence, then as a different consultant next time
- Her past advice paraphrased into something she didn't say

## How she feels about AI
Uses it for archive research happily; would resign from any process that asked it to soften an assessment. Her line is the discipline's line.

## In her own words
> “Bring me in at sketch stage and I'm an asset. Bring me in at submission and I'm a verdict. Same fee — wildly different outcomes.”

## Win her by
Early integration into scheme development, her reports carried into evidence undiluted, and her advice history quoted with citation-grade fidelity — independence, protected by the record.
