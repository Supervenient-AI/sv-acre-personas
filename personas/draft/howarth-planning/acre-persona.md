# ACRE Persona 25 — The Committee-Calendar Mesh
### Howarth Planning — an 11-person planning consultancy orbiting local authorities, consultees and committee dates

*Status: hypothesis persona — generated from ACRE target universe candidate #17 (Family 4, peer mesh); not yet interview-grounded.*

---

## A — Account

**Archetype:** The Statutory Navigator
**Instance:** Howarth Planning, York. Director, six planners, a heritage lead, support staff; freelance specialists (heritage, ecology, transport, landscape) attached per application. ~120 applications and appeals a year for developers, landowners, businesses and the occasional anxious homeowner.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Collaboration posture | Mesh-assembler: every significant application is a temporary team of specialist consultants (each from a different firm) producing one coordinated submission — judged by an authority that is itself a constellation of case officer, consultees and councillors. |
| Tooling | M365, the Planning Portal, every council's own portal (each different, most broken), GIS, long Word documents with longer appendices. |
| Who carries coordination cost | The case planner per application — assembling six consultants' reports into one coherent argument against a validation checklist and a committee date. |
| Buying behaviour | Document-heavy and precedent-driven; already experimenting with AI for policy summarisation, nervous about hallucinated precedent. Buys per-seat tools reluctantly, per-outcome value gladly. |
| What failure costs | A refusal costs the client months and the firm its win-rate reputation; a missed consultation response or validation failure costs a committee cycle (often three months); a heritage misjudgement can cost a listed building and the firm's standing with the authority it must work with forever. |

## C — Constellation

**Definition:** A consultancy assembling temporary specialist meshes to argue cases before a permanent institutional mesh — where the decisive nodes (case officers, statutory consultees, elected committees) are outside everyone's control and run on calendars of their own.

**Cast:**
- **Cath Howarth, 50** — founder & director (York)
- **Josh Pemberton, 29** — planner; runner of a dozen applications (York)
- **Dr Frieda Stannard, 47** — freelance heritage consultant (Harrogate)
- **Mark Tilbrook, 45** — client: small residential developer, 4–30 unit schemes (Leeds)
- **Niamh Connor, 36** — case officer at the local planning authority *(external immovable node with a name — she matters that much)*
- **Plus:** ecology/transport/landscape consultants per scheme, the architect, parish councils, objectors

**External immovable nodes:**
- **The planning committee** — elected members, political weather, a calendar that quantises everything into cycles
- **Statutory consultees** — Historic England, highways, the Environment Agency, ecology: 21-day consultation windows they routinely exceed, holding effective veto
- **The case officer** — overworked, under-resourced, the single human through whom everything flows
- **National policy (NPPF) and case law** — reshuffled by every government, retroactively re-weighting live arguments

**Topology:** A double mesh: the consultant team (Howarth-coordinated, temporary) addressing the authority mesh (permanent, opaque, political). Howarth is the hinge — the only party fluent in both.

**Lifecycle (per application, 6–24 months):**
- *Pre-application:* strategy, site appraisal, the pre-app meeting with the authority — where expectations get set and half-remembered later.
- *Assembly:* specialist reports commissioned, each consultant on their own timeline, all converging (theoretically) on a submission date.
- *Validation & consultation:* the authority's checklist gauntlet, then the public stage — objections arrive, consultees respond late, the scheme's fate forms in correspondence.
- *Midpoint reset:* the case officer's "concerns" letter — the mesh reconvenes, the scheme amends, viability re-runs; the client's patience and budget both thin.
- *Committee (the cliff):* a five-minute presentation, three minutes of member questions, a vote swayed by things said at a parish meeting in March.
- *Decision, conditions or appeal:* approval breeds condition-discharge (a whole second lifecycle); refusal breeds the appeal mesh (add a barrister).
- *Trust curve:* the client's trust tracks momentum — every silent month spends it; the *authority's* trust in Howarth (professional, cross-application) is the firm's true capital, accumulated by never wasting an officer's time and never springing surprises.

**Cadence:** Committee cycles quantise everything; consultee windows set the tempo; the client rings on Fridays. Officer contact is precious and rationed — Niamh has 40 cases.

**Boundary objects:** The planning statement (the argument itself), the specialist reports (six firms' work that must read as one mind), the validation checklist, the officer's report to committee (written by Niamh, shaped by everything Howarth submitted), the decision notice with conditions, the policy map.

**Where articulation work concentrates:** On the case planner — chasing six specialists toward one deadline, version-controlling a 400-page submission, translating officer signals to client expectations. On Cath for the political reading.

**Where who-knows-what lives:** Cath holds the authority's political map and each officer's tells. Josh holds the live state of every application. Frieda holds heritage judgement and Historic England's likely view. Niamh holds the authority's true position, expressed in careful signals. Mark holds his viability numbers, revealed selectively. Precedent — the firm's argument arsenal — lives in old files and Cath's memory.

**Characteristic failure modes** *(tagged)*:
1. Specialist-report divergence: six consultants' documents contradicting each other in submitted evidence — **eliminate**
2. Consultee-window blindness: a late ecology response discovered after it stalled the cycle — **eliminate**
3. Pre-app amnesia: officer expectations from month two un-recorded and re-litigated at month nine — **eliminate**
4. Client-expectation drift in the long silences — **eliminate** (Wrenfield's update-vacuum pattern, with committee dates)
5. The objectors and the parish — **contain**: legitimate democratic friction; the product tracks and answers it, never games it
6. Frieda's "this scheme harms the conservation area and I won't say otherwise" — **preserve**: a consultant's professional integrity is worth more than any single application, to everyone including the client
7. Niamh's overload — **contain**: the authority's resourcing is beyond reach; making Howarth's submissions the easiest on her desk is the available move

**Conflicting interests:**
- Mark wants maximum units; the policy-compliant scheme is always smaller; Cath brokers the gap
- Each specialist defends their discipline's integrity against the scheme's commercial shape
- The authority wants defensible decisions; the committee wants re-electable ones — not the same thing
- Howarth needs this application won *and* the authority relationship intact for the next hundred

*Contain-class: the firm lives on the long game with the authority; no single client's interest may spend that capital — a structural conflict the client never fully sees.*

**Substitution stress-test:** Swap a specialist: re-commissioned report, a cycle lost. Swap Josh: a dozen applications' state and officer-correspondence nuance transfers badly; momentum stalls. Swap *Niamh* mid-application (authority churn is chronic): the application effectively restarts socially — new officer, new concerns, pre-app history worthless unless written down (it wasn't). Swap Cath: the political map and authority trust — the firm's moat — walk out. Swap Mark's funder (invisible, off-stage): the scheme dies regardless of planning merit.

**Afterlife:** Decisions live forever: precedent feeds the next argument; conditions bind the land; refused schemes return revised for years. The firm's win-rate and the authority's regard compound across applications — the deepest repeat game with a *regulator* in the library. The pre-app and correspondence archive is the next application's intelligence, currently scattered across inboxes and memory.

**What this constellation needs from the AI:** Assemble the mesh's documents coherently — cross-report contradiction checking before the authority finds them. Track every consultee window, committee date and condition deadline as first-class clocks. Keep the officer-correspondence record (pre-app commitments, signals, concerns) live and citable. Maintain the precedent arsenal: every decision, appeal and committee outcome searchable with reasoning — hallucination-proof, citation-first. Give Mark a truthful momentum view. And protect the long game: never let one application's tactics borrow against the authority relationship.

### Cath Howarth, 50 — Founder & Director *(The Political Reader)*
**York.** Twenty-five years, both sides — ex-authority herself, which is the firm's quiet superpower. Reads a committee like Imogen Sorrel reads a room. **Attitude to AI:** intrigued for policy digestion, scathing about hallucinated case law ("cite it or bin it"). **Winning her looks like:** the precedent arsenal is instant and sourced, the officer record is total, and her political judgement gets the time the document-wrangling used to eat.

### Josh Pemberton, 29 — Planner *(The Application Runner)*
**York.** RTPI-chartered last year; runs twelve applications with twelve casts. **Attitude to AI:** daily user for summarising 300-page evidence bases; wants the clock-watching automated before it ages him. **Winning him looks like:** consultee windows, validation checklists and report versions manage themselves; he writes arguments instead of chasing PDFs.

### Dr Frieda Stannard, 47 — Heritage Consultant *(The Conscience Node)*
**Harrogate.** PhD in vernacular architecture; consults for six planning firms. Her professional integrity is her entire market value, and she protects it against every client's commercial gravity. **Attitude to AI:** uses it for archive research; would resign from any process that asked it to soften her assessments. **Winning her looks like:** her reports integrate without dilution, her objections land early enough to shape schemes rather than kill them, and her advice history is never misquoted.

### Mark Tilbrook, 45 — Developer Client *(The Viability Engine)*
**Leeds.** Builds 4–30 unit schemes on borrowed money; every committee cycle costs him interest. Charming, impatient, selectively transparent about his numbers. **Attitude to AI:** uses ChatGPT to read planning policies at midnight and arrives with theories. **Winning him looks like:** an honest momentum view, costs of delay made visible, and his midnight theories met with sourced answers instead of sighs.

### Niamh Connor, 36 — LPA Case Officer *(The Named Immovable)*
**The authority.** Forty cases, no time, judged on throughput and defensibility. Not a user and never will be — but every Howarth submission is designed for her desk. **Winning the edge looks like:** Howarth's applications are the complete, contradiction-free, easy ones — which is worth more than any advocacy.

| Edge | Contract | Trust (level → trajectory) | Channel | Key asymmetry / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cath ↔ Niamh / the authority | None — professional standing | Deep, institutional → **the firm's moat**: spent by surprises, earned by clean submissions | Pre-apps + correspondence | Cross-application capital no single client may borrow against |

| Mesh ↔ committee | Democratic | None possible → political weather | The chamber | Five minutes that outweigh fourteen months |
| **Cath ↔ AI** | — | Citation-or-bin | In-product | Precedent search with sources is the trust gate |

**Product needs from the edge map:**
- The authority edge generalises Compass's Pete-edge pattern: when the counterparty can't be a user, make your side's outputs *systematically easier to process* — completeness as a relationship strategy, designable and measurable.
- The contradiction-check across six specialist reports is the mesh-assembly equivalent of preflight: catching the clash before the gatekeeper does is worth a committee cycle.
- The officer-record (pre-app commitments, signals) is this persona's golden thread — same artefact class as Persona 4, pointed at a regulator instead of a courtroom.
